How Holy Paladin Works

Holy Paladin healing revolves around one core loop: Holy Shock on cooldown to generate Reclamation procs, then spend your holy power on either Light of Dawn for AoE healing or Eternal Flame for single-target healing. Between Holy Shock cooldowns, you fill with Flash of Light and Holy Light to keep healing going every global.

Light of Dawn is your bread-and-butter AoE heal in raid — it hits 5 targets in front of you and it’s your primary holy power spender when the raid is taking damage. Top Holy Paladins cast it 10+ times per minute. If you’re only casting it 3 times per minute, the raid is getting less than a third of the AoE healing it should be getting from this healer.

Shield of the Righteous is a tank ability. As a healer, those GCDs should be going to Flash of Light or Light of Dawn instead. Every Shield of the Righteous cast is a global cooldown that produces zero healing for the raid.

How It’s Affecting Your Healing

Light of Dawn at 3.2/m vs 10.3/m (31%). This is the single biggest HPS gap. Light of Dawn is the main AoE heal for Holy Paladin — it should be the primary holy power spender whenever the raid is taking damage. The raid is getting roughly a third of the AoE healing it should be from this healer.
Flash of Light at 2.4/m vs 10.9/m (22%). Combined with the Light of Dawn gap, total healing casts per minute are drastically below top players. Flash of Light should be filling every gap between Holy Shock cooldowns and Reclamation procs. The dead GCD time is very high.
Shield of the Righteous at 1.1/m. This is a tank ability that provides no healing to the raid. Every Shield of the Righteous cast is a GCD that could have been a Flash of Light or Light of Dawn instead. Top Holy Paladins cast it 0.3/m or less.
0 mana pots, 0 health pots, 2/16 pre-pots. Consumable usage is extremely low. Mana potions extend your ability to heal in long pulls — on a 6+ minute Mythic LBV attempt, running out of mana without ever using a mana pot means the raid loses a healer in the final phase.

What You Need to Change

1
Cast Light of Dawn WAY More
Target 8+ per minute. This should be your primary holy power spender on raid damage. You’re at 3.2/m and the #1 is at 10.3/m — that means you’re spending most of your holy power on Eternal Flame (single-target) instead of Light of Dawn (5 targets). In a 20-player raid taking constant AoE damage, Light of Dawn is almost always the better choice.
2
Cast Flash of Light as Your Filler
Target 8+ per minute. Between Holy Shock cooldowns and Reclamation procs, Flash of Light fills every gap. You’re at 2.4/m and the #1 is at 10.9/m. That means you have long stretches where you’re not pressing any healing button at all. Every empty GCD is healing the raid doesn’t get.
3
Stop Casting Shield of the Righteous
Shield of the Righteous is a tank ability that provides armor and no healing. You’re casting it 1.1 times per minute — those are globals that should be Flash of Light or Light of Dawn. Replace every SotR with a healing spell and your CPM gap closes significantly.
4
Use Consumables
Pre-pot every pull — you only pre-potted 2 out of 16. Mana pot in longer pulls — you used zero across all 16 pulls, which means on 5+ minute attempts you’re running dry. Healthstone + health pot for survival — health pot is on a separate CD from pre-pot, it’s a free emergency heal you’re not using.
