Actionable Improvements

1 Prioritize Final Verdict over Divine Storm in Single-Target / Low-Target Windows
Estimated impact: +7-10% DPS (~8K-11K DPS)
Final Verdict CPM: 11.3 vs reference 18.3 (-38%). Divine Storm CPM: 5.4 vs reference 1.9 (+178%). Alitheria is using Divine Storm nearly 3x more than the reference, which means Holy Power is being spent on AoE when single-target damage matters more. Divine Storm should only be used at 3+ targets. On 1-2 targets, Final Verdict always wins. Each Divine Storm replacing a Final Verdict on the boss costs roughly 30-40% of that spender's damage. Fix: use Final Verdict as the default spender, only switching to Divine Storm when 3+ targets are stacked.
2 Eliminate the 24-Second Dead Zone at 2:43-3:08
Estimated impact: +4-5% DPS (~41 lost casts recovered)
Two back-to-back casting gaps (13.5s + 10.2s) create a near-total shutdown from 2:43 to 3:08. This overlaps with Festering Vines #3 at 2:59, suggesting either getting rooted and unable to attack, or excessive repositioning. The reference maintains activity through equivalent windows. Fix: pre-position before Vines, use Divine Steed to break roots faster, and ensure you have a target in range at all times. If forced to move, use instants (Judgment, Hammer of Wrath if available).
3 Reduce Crusading Strikes Gaps — Always Be Pressing a Button
Estimated impact: +3-4% DPS (~52 lost GCDs)
Crusading Strikes CPM: 29.2 vs reference 34.8 (-16%). 5 of 10 cast gaps >2.5s follow a Crusading Strikes auto-attack generator. This means the auto-attack fires, generates Holy Power, and then nothing happens for 2.5-4.8 seconds. The Ret rotation should have no dead GCDs — if generators are on cooldown, use a spender. If Holy Power is low, use the next available generator in priority order. Fix: practice the "always be casting" principle. If everything is on cooldown, Crusading Strikes auto-attacks should fill naturally, but you must follow them with the next available GCD.
4 Use a Combat Potion
Estimated impact: +2-3% DPS
No DPS potion was detected during the kill fight. A Tempered Potion or equivalent should be pre-potted 1-2 seconds before pull and used again during a burst window (typically with Avenging Wrath). This is free DPS that requires zero rotation changes. The Healthstone and Silvermoon Health Potion used were survival items, not DPS consumables.
5 Maintain Uptime During Fungal Bloom Windows
Estimated impact: +1-2% DPS
CPS drops 42% below the reference during all three Bloom windows (avg 1.69 vs 2.89). Bloom is raid-wide AoE damage that does not require movement — healers handle it. DPS should maintain full rotation through Blooms. If you are using a defensive (Divine Protection is well-timed here), you should still be pressing offensive buttons. The reference maintains near-full activity through every Bloom. Fix: treat Bloom windows as pure DPS windows. Your health is the healers' problem; your job is to keep hitting the boss.

Assessment

The 46% DPS gap vs the rank 1 reference is a mix of execution, talent build, and context

Of the gap, roughly 10-15% is talent build differences (10 nodes differ between Alitheria and Kopi), 5-8% is kill time context (Kopi's raid killed in 7:12 with presumably higher overall raid DPS, meaning less soft-enrage damage and more favorable fight conditions), and the remaining 20-30% is execution-based: the Final Verdict vs Divine Storm spender imbalance, the 53.6s of dead time from cast gaps, the missing potion, and the CPS drops during Bloom windows.

Gear is not a factor — Alitheria's 290 ilvl is actually 3 points higher than the reference. The comparison is apples-to-apples on gear.
