Critical: Flurry Starvation (−34% CPM)

Flurry is the cornerstone of the Spellslinger Frost Mage rotation. Each Flurry applies Winter's Chill to the target, enabling the next Ice Lance to Shatter for massive bonus damage. At 5.0 CPM vs 7.6 CPM, Snackznchill is casting Flurry 34% less often than the reference. This cascades through the entire rotation: fewer Flurries = fewer Winter's Chill debuffs = fewer Shatter-empowered Ice Lances = fewer Frost Splinter procs = fewer Thermal Void activations (12 vs 49). Flurry should be used on cooldown with Brain Freeze procs, and also when Thermal Void is not active (even without Brain Freeze, if a charge is available).

Combined with the Flurry deficit, this means fewer Brain Freeze procs are being generated (12 observed vs an expected ~22 for the alive time), creating a vicious cycle: less Frostbolt → less Brain Freeze → less Flurry → less Shatter damage.

Why Thermal Void Matters

In Spellslinger Frost, the rotation revolves around consuming Winter's Chill stacks with Ice Lance. Each Flurry applies Winter's Chill, and when Ice Lance consumes the stacks, it triggers Thermal Void (damage amp + haste), generates Frost Splinters (10.4% of total damage), and enables Shatter (38.6% of total damage). With only 12 Thermal Void procs vs the reference's projected ~24 in the same timeframe, Snackznchill is losing damage from every linked system. The fix is simple: cast Flurry more aggressively — use it on every Brain Freeze proc without delay, and use available charges even without Brain Freeze when Thermal Void is not active.

Actionable Items

1. Cast Flurry more aggressively — target 7+ CPM, currently at 5.0. This is the single most impactful fix. Every Flurry applies Winter's Chill, which enables Shatter-empowered Ice Lances (38.6% of total damage) and triggers Thermal Void (damage amp). Currently at 22 Flurry casts in 4:22 (5.0 CPM) vs the reference's 69 casts in 9:05 (7.6 CPM). The fix: use Flurry immediately on every Brain Freeze proc (do not hold it), and use available Flurry charges even without Brain Freeze when Thermal Void is not active. In Spellslinger, the rotation priority is: Flurry (with Brain Freeze) > Ice Lance (with Fingers of Frost) > Frozen Orb > Glacial Spike > Frostbolt. Estimated impact: raising Flurry CPM from 5.0 to 7.0+ would roughly double Thermal Void uptime and increase Shatter damage by ~15-20%.
2. Fill GCD gaps with Frostbolt — never let the GCD sit empty. Total CPM is 37.9 vs 43.1 (−12%), meaning approximately 23 GCDs were lost over 4:22 of combat. These lost GCDs should be Frostbolt casts, which generate Brain Freeze procs (fueling more Flurries) and build Icicles (fueling more Glacial Spikes). The progression environment makes movement necessary, but Shimmer (instant blink while casting) was only used 4 times — using Shimmer to maintain casts during movement would recover several lost GCDs. On a fight like Crown where movement is constant (platform transitions, Void Expulsion puddles, Silverstrike dodging), ABC (Always Be Casting) discipline is critical.
3. Use defensive cooldowns to survive — Ice Cold, Alter Time, Greater Invisibility. Snackznchill used zero of these three defensives and died at 4:22 to Void Remnants. The reference player used each one twice across a 9-minute kill. Ice Cold (immune) would have prevented the Void Remnants death entirely. Alter Time allows repositioning while reverting HP. Greater Invisibility provides 60% damage reduction. On mythic Crown of the Cosmos, these are not optional — they are mandatory survival tools for handling lethal overlap mechanics in Phase 2 and Phase 3. Bind them accessibly and practice using them preemptively when you see dangerous abilities channeling.
4. Avoid falling damage — 353K across 2 hits is avoidable. Two fall damage events (353K total) indicate platform transition issues during phase changes. Frost Mage has Slow Fall and Shimmer to manage these transitions safely. This damage strains healers and risks death during overlap phases. Practice the platform transition timing to eliminate this entirely.
5. Prioritize second potion usage for extended fights. Pre-pot was correctly used at 0:00, but no second potion was consumed. In a 4:53 pull, a second Potion of Recklessness should be available around 4:00 and should align with Bloodlust or a burn phase. The reference used 2 potions across the 9-minute kill. On kill attempts, this second pot will matter.
