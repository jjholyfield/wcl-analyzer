Bottom Line

Baodabao survived the kill and dealt 133.7K DPS across 7:58 — a 36.2% gap behind the #1 reference Золтен (209.6K DPS in 5:51). The core issue is casts per minute: 42.6 vs 60.8 — Baodabao is pressing roughly 30% fewer buttons per minute than the reference. Every rotational ability shows a CPM deficit: Ice Lance at 19.7 vs 29.5 CPM (−33%), Frozen Orb at 1.5 vs 2.9 CPM (−48%), Frostbolt at 6.9 vs 9.9 CPM (−30%), and Flurry at 6.0 vs 8.0 CPM (−25%). Part of this gap is contextual — the ref's 5:51 kill is 27% shorter, meaning more burst-phase-weighted CPM — but the magnitude of the gap across every ability suggests significant dead GCD time. The Frozen Orb gap (12 vs 17 casts) directly reduces Shatter damage, which accounts for the single largest damage gap (−4.3M). With similar ilvl (289.1 vs 290.3) and 5 talent differences, the gap is primarily execution — GCD efficiency and Frozen Orb usage on cooldown.

Baodabao's 42.6 total CPM vs the reference's 60.8 CPM is the dominant factor in the DPS gap. Every single rotational ability shows a 25-48% CPM deficit. In Frost Mage's current rotation (12.0.7 Frostfire), the gameplay loop is: cast Frostbolt/Flurry/Ray of Frost to stack the Freezing debuff on the target, then consume those stacks with Ice Lance or Glacial Spike to trigger Shatter explosions. Lower CPM across the board means fewer Freezing stacks built, fewer Shatter triggers, and less total damage from every source. The GCD should be rolling constantly — Frostbolt is the filler that should be cast whenever nothing higher-priority is available.

Frozen Orb: 48% CPM Deficit

The reference averaged a Frozen Orb every 21 seconds, Baodabao every 40 seconds. Every missed Frozen Orb is lost direct damage AND fewer Fingers of Frost procs feeding into Ice Lance and Shatter. This single ability accounts for −3.06M of the damage gap.

Where the Damage Gap Comes From

Despite doing less total damage, Baodabao actually does more per-cast damage on several abilities (Flurry, Glacial Spike, Ray of Frost) — indicating that when abilities are cast, they hit hard. The problem is cast frequency, not cast quality. The three biggest damage gaps are: Shatter (−4.3M) because fewer Freezing stacks are being built and consumed per minute, Frozen Orb (−3.1M) from 5 fewer casts, and Splintering Ray (−1.6M) which appears in the reference's damage but not Baodabao's — this may be a talent difference (1 of the 5 talent diffs). The Shatter gap alone is the single largest factor: Shatter is 38-39% of both players' damage, and Baodabao's 4.3M deficit in Shatter closely correlates with the lower Ice Lance and Frozen Orb CPM reducing the number of Freezing stack consumption events.

Why Frozen Orb Frequency Matters

In the Frostfire hero talent tree, Frozen Orb flies to the target and deals damage every 0.5s for 15 seconds, generating Fingers of Frost procs along the way. Each Frozen Orb window produces multiple free Ice Lance casts (via Fingers of Frost) that consume maximum Freezing stacks without needing to build them manually, triggering bonus Shatter damage. The reference casts Frozen Orb every ~20 seconds on average — meaning Frozen Orb is active for roughly 75% of the fight. Baodabao averages one every ~40 seconds, leaving long stretches without Frozen Orb's passive damage and proc generation. The −3.06M Frozen Orb damage gap is only the direct damage loss — the indirect loss from fewer Fingers of Frost procs (which feed Ice Lance and Shatter damage) compounds the problem significantly.

Actionable Items

1. Reduce dead GCD time — always be casting. This is the single biggest DPS improvement available. The 42.6 vs 60.8 CPM gap translates to roughly 30% of available GCDs going unused. In Frost Mage's current rotation, the GCD should never be empty: Frostbolt is always available as a filler that builds Freezing stacks on the target. Any time Ice Lance, Flurry, Frozen Orb, Glacial Spike, and Ray of Frost are all on cooldown or unavailable, Frostbolt should be cast immediately. With 696 Haste (81% more than the reference), Baodabao has faster GCDs available — the cast speed is there, the button pressing needs to match it. Estimated gain: closing even half the CPM gap would add ~25-30K DPS.
2. Cast Frozen Orb on cooldown — every 15-20 seconds. Frozen Orb is the highest-priority ability in the Frostfire Frost Mage rotation. It deals direct damage for 15 seconds AND generates Fingers of Frost procs that enable free Ice Lance casts with maximum Freezing consumption (Shatter damage). Baodabao averaged a Frozen Orb every 40 seconds vs the reference's 21 seconds. The Frozen Orb gap alone accounts for −3.06M direct damage plus additional indirect losses from fewer Fingers of Frost procs. Monitor the Frozen Orb cooldown and cast it the instant it becomes available. Estimated gain: ~15-20K DPS from direct damage and cascading proc generation.
3. Increase Ice Lance casting rate. Ice Lance at 19.7 CPM vs 29.5 CPM is a 33% deficit. Ice Lance is the primary Freezing stack consumer — every Ice Lance with 10+ Freezing stacks (Frostfire threshold) triggers a Shatter explosion. More Frozen Orbs (fix #2) will naturally increase Fingers of Frost procs, which feed more Ice Lances. Additionally, in the Frostfire priority, Ice Lance should be cast whenever Freezing stacks reach 10+ or whenever Fingers of Frost is active. Watching for the Fingers of Frost proc indicator and reacting immediately will increase Ice Lance frequency. This is partially downstream of fixes #1 and #2.
4. Improve positioning to reduce Festering Vines hits. Baodabao took 9 Festering Vines hits and used Alter Time 4 times (reference: 0). Festering Vines roots players and deals damage — it killed 5 raid members in this pull. As a ranged DPS, Frost Mages have the luxury of pre-positioning away from Vines spawn locations. Better awareness of Vines timing (every ~68 seconds) and proactive positioning would eliminate the need for defensive Alter Time usage, freeing those GCDs for damage and avoiding rotation interruptions during root recovery.
5. Investigate the Splintering Ray talent difference. The reference dealt 1.57M damage from Splintering Ray (2.1% of total) while Baodabao shows 0 damage from this source. This is likely one of the 5 talent differences — Splintering Ray appears to be a talent that adds bonus damage to Ray of Frost ticks. Since both players cast Ray of Frost the same number of times (12), having this talent would be a passive DPS gain with zero rotation changes required. Review the talent tree and consider picking up Splintering Ray if it does not require dropping a critical node.
