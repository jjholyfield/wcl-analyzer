Short P2 Pulls Have Extreme Cosmic Barrier Damage
Fights that died early in P2 (pulls 5, 6, 11, 17 — only 54-62 seconds of P2 time) show Cosmic Barrier DTPS of 494K-769K, compared to the reference average of 77K. That is 6-10x the reference rate. The barrier is pulsing unchecked for the entire time the raid is alive in P2 because nobody is breaking it. This alone accounts for why these pulls collapse immediately upon entering Phase 2.
Later Pulls Show Improvement
Full-duration P2 pulls later in the night (fights 19, 23, 25) brought Cosmic Barrier DTPS down to 44-112K, much closer to the reference range of 63-112K. The best pull (fight 25, our deepest at 26.5% boss HP) had Cosmic Barrier at 45K DTPS — actually below the reference average. This confirms: when we focus the Simulacrums and break the barrier quickly, P2 damage is manageable.

What This Means

Two Distinct Failure Modes
The data reveals two different problems happening in P2, and they are inversely correlated:

1. Cosmic Barrier not being broken (early pulls): In pulls 5, 6, 11, and 17, the Cosmic Barrier was dealing 494K-769K DTPS — 6-10x the reference rate. This is the barrier pulsing AoE damage while it is still active on the Rift Simulacrums. Reference kills break the barrier in seconds; our early pulls were ignoring it entirely. The raid was dying to uncontrolled barrier ticks before Simulacrum Backlash even had time to ramp up. That is why Backlash DTPS looks artificially low in these pulls — people were already dead.

2. Simulacrums living too long (later pulls): In our later full-duration pulls (20, 23), we fixed the barrier problem (44-58K DTPS, reference range), but Simulacrum Backlash climbed to 340-364K DTPS, above the reference average of 255K. The Simulacrums are now getting their barriers broken, but they are still staying alive too long afterward, allowing Backlash to pulse for extended periods.

Fight 25 was the sweet spot: Barrier at 45K (below reference), Backlash at 248K (close to reference), combined 293K (12% below reference). This was also our deepest pull at 26.5% boss HP. The correlation is clear: clean Simulacrum handling = survivable P2 = boss HP progression.
The Math on Simulacrum Kill Speed
Across all 5 reference kills, the combined Simulacrum DTPS ranged from 258K to 436K, with an average of 332K. Our best pull (fight 25) hit 293K — actually competitive with the cleaner reference kills (Fierce at 263K, Raging Time at 268K, D. Uncouth at 258K).

The two high-reference outliers (Tempo 436K, Disconnect 435K) suggest that even top guilds take significant Simulacrum damage — the difference is they have the DPS throughput and healing cooldown coordination to survive it. For our raid, bringing Simulacrum damage below 350K combined DTPS consistently is the target. Fight 25 proved this is achievable.

Action items:
1. Assign DPS to break Cosmic Barrier immediately when Simulacrums spawn — this alone eliminates the worst damage spikes
2. After barrier is broken, burn Simulacrums down as the #1 P2 priority before switching back to boss
3. Coordinate healing CDs around Simulacrum Backlash ticks, not just boss abilities
4. Track Cosmic Barrier DTPS per pull as the primary execution metric for P2
