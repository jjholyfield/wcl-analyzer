How Windwalker Monk Works

Windwalker is a priority-based spec, not a fixed rotation. Every GCD, you scan your priority list and press the highest available ability. There’s no memorized sequence — just a hierarchy of what matters most right now.

The resource system works in two steps. Energy regenerates passively and fuels Tiger Palm, which generates Chi. Chi is your spending resource — it goes into Rising Sun Kick, Blackout Kick, Fists of Fury, and Spinning Crane Kick. More Tiger Palms means more Chi, and more Chi means more spenders. If Tiger Palm falls behind, everything downstream slows down.

Your Mastery is Combo Strikes — every ability does bonus damage when it’s different from your last cast. Never press the same button twice in a row. This is what makes WW feel fluid — you’re constantly weaving between abilities.

The key cooldowns are Zenith (60-90s, 2 charges — resets Rising Sun Kick and makes Blackout Kick free), Invoke Xuen paired with Celestial Conduit (your big burst window), and Touch of Death (execute). These layer on top of the core loop, they don’t replace it.

The core loop is straightforward: Tiger Palm for Chi, spend Chi on RSK/BOK/FoF/SCK, weave in cooldowns, and never repeat the same ability back to back. WW has natural downtime between GCDs while waiting for Energy to regenerate — that’s normal. But 38 CPM vs 48 CPM from top players means too much of that downtime is idle time where abilities were actually available.

Touch of Karma is primarily a defensive — it absorbs incoming damage and reflects it back. It can be used offensively by standing in avoidable damage to max out the absorb, but keeping yourself alive comes first.

How This Is Affecting Your Damage

DPS is at 96% of the #1 WW Monk. 210,659 avg DPS vs 219,763. The Windwalker rotation fundamentals are there — this is a player who knows the spec. The gaps below are about tightening up, not rebuilding from scratch.
Blackout Kick at 5.1/min vs 8.5/min (60% of top) is the biggest rotation gap. BOK is the core Chi spender and it resets other cooldowns through talent interactions. When BOK casts are low, the entire priority system slows down — fewer RSK resets, fewer FoF resets, fewer opportunities to spend Chi. This one ability cascading is likely responsible for the Tiger Palm and RSK gaps too.
Tiger Palm at 6.8/min vs 9.2/min (74%). Tiger Palm is the Chi generator. Fewer Tiger Palms means less Chi, which means fewer spenders. The 2.4/min gap here directly feeds the Blackout Kick and RSK gaps downstream. This suggests Energy is being wasted sitting at cap instead of being converted into Chi.
Rising Sun Kick at 6.4/min vs 8.8/min (73%). RSK is the highest-priority Chi spender. Fewer RSK casts mean less damage from your hardest-hitting rotational ability. Some of this is downstream from fewer BOK resets and fewer Tiger Palms generating Chi.
23% early death rate is the biggest overall issue. Dying in 6 out of 26 pulls means almost 1 in 4 pulls are cut short. Dead players do zero DPS. The 210K average includes those shortened pulls, so actual alive-time DPS is likely even closer to top players. But dying this often holds back both personal numbers and raid progress.
0 health potions used across 26 pulls. Health potion is on a separate cooldown from pre-potion. You can use both. With a 23% death rate, having a health potion available every pull could turn some of those deaths into survived damage events.

What You Need to Change

1
Use healthstone and health potion
23% early death rate with 0 health potions and only 7 healthstones across 26 pulls. Health potion is a separate cooldown from pre-potion — you can and should carry both. These are the easiest survivability gains available. You’re already potting for damage 96% of the time — apply that same discipline to staying alive.
2
More Blackout Kicks — target 8+/min
5.1/min vs 8.5/min from top players. This is the single biggest rotation gap. Blackout Kick resets other cooldowns, so low BOK cascades into fewer Rising Sun Kick and Fists of Fury uses. When you have Chi and RSK/FoF aren’t available, press BOK. It should be your most common Chi spender between priority abilities.
3
Fill GCDs with Tiger Palm — target 9+/min
6.8/min vs 9.2/min. Tiger Palm is the Chi generator — more Tiger Palms means more Chi means more spenders. The 38 CPM vs 48 CPM gap is largely explained by empty GCDs where Energy was available but Tiger Palm wasn’t pressed. When nothing else is available, Tiger Palm. Don’t let Energy sit at cap.
