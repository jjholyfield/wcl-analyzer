How Demonology Warlock Works

Demonology Warlock is a pet-army spec that summons waves of demons and then empowers them all at once with Demonic Tyrant. The core loop is: cast Hand of Gul’dan to summon Wild Imps (your main damage source), cast Call Dreadstalkers for another set of demons, build Soul Shards with Shadow Bolt and Demonbolt, then press Summon Demonic Tyrant to extend and empower all active demons.

Hand of Gul’dan is the most important ability — it costs Soul Shards and summons Wild Imps that do your damage. The more Imps alive when you press Tyrant, the bigger your burst window. Demonbolt is a strong builder that also has procs — when it lights up, you press it immediately. Shadow Bolt is your filler between procs.

Implosion detonates your Wild Imps for AoE damage — timing this correctly (after Tyrant extends them, or when Imps are about to expire) is key. Ruination and Infernal Bolt are talent-specific abilities on longer cooldowns that deal heavy damage.

The gap between good and great Demo Locks is almost entirely Hand of Gul’dan frequency. More HoG casts = more Imps = more Tyrant value = more damage. Every missed HoG is compounding lost DPS.

How It’s Affecting Your Damage

Hand of Gul’dan at 76% of #1 is the single biggest issue. 12.7/m vs 16.7/m means approximately 4 fewer HoG casts per minute. Each HoG summons Wild Imps — your primary damage source. Fewer Imps means weaker Tyrant windows, fewer Implosion targets, and compounding DPS loss across the entire fight. This one gap accounts for most of the 58K DPS difference.
Demonbolt at 77% — missing procs or not prioritizing them. Demonbolt procs are your highest-priority builder. Every proc that expires while you’re casting Shadow Bolt instead is a lost Soul Shard, which means a lost Hand of Gul’dan cast downstream. React to procs immediately — they should interrupt your Shadow Bolt filler.
Implosion at 77% is a downstream effect. With fewer Hand of Gul’dan casts, there are fewer Wild Imps alive to detonate. Fix the HoG frequency and this number should come up naturally.
Shadow Bolt at 100% and Dreadstalkers at 93%. Filler usage and Dreadstalker uptime are both on par with the #1. The issue is not button pressing speed — it’s Shard management and proc priority.

What You Need to Change

1
More Hand of Gul’dan
Target 16+ per minute. Currently at 12.7. This means better Soul Shard management — don’t cap shards, don’t sit on them. Every second a shard sits unspent is an Imp that doesn’t exist. Hand of Gul’dan should be cast the moment you have 3 shards (or 1-2 if you’d otherwise cap).
2
More Demonbolt
Target 12+ per minute. Currently at 9.6. React to Demonbolt procs faster — don’t let them expire while casting Shadow Bolt. When Demonbolt lights up, it becomes your highest-priority cast. Every missed proc is a missed Soul Shard and a missed HoG downstream.
3
Health Pots Every Pull
12/30 is low. Health pots are on a separate cooldown from your DPS pot — using one doesn’t lock out the other. In a fight with this much raid damage, a free heal every pull is too valuable to leave on the table.
4
Continue Using Demonic Healthstone
20/30 (67%) is solid for a Warlock with extra Healthstone charges. Keep doing this. Combined with better Health Pot usage, your survivability will improve noticeably.
