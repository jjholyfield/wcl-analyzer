Bottom Line — Progression Context

Darthurg dealt 126K DPS on the longest pull (Fight 23, 4:53) before dying at 4:28 — a 37.8% gap behind the #1 ranked MM Hunter kill reference (110.7K DPS in 9:03). However, this comparison must be heavily contextualized: the reference is from a full kill (505s) while Darthurg's pull was a 293s wipe where the raid collapsed after 4:10 with mass deaths. The primary issues are Aimed Shot CPM (6.6 vs 9.5) — a 31% deficit that accounts for the largest share of the damage gap — and 53.8s of dead time across 6 significant gaps where no GCDs were pressed, likely from movement mechanics in Crown's Rift Simulacrum phase. Trueshot was used only 2 times in 4:53 vs the reference's 4 in 8:25 (CPM: 0.4 vs 0.5), and zero Kill Shots were fired despite the spec's Deathblow procs. The 3 ilvl gap (286 vs 289) and 6 talent differences contribute a small portion, but the majority of the gap is execution-based: uptime, Aimed Shot management, and cooldown frequency.

Critical: Aimed Shot CPM Deficit

Aimed Shot is the #1 damage ability for MM Hunter (40.9% of Darthurg's total damage, 42.6% of reference). At 6.6 CPM vs 9.5 CPM, Darthurg is pressing Aimed Shot 31% less often per minute. Over 4:53, that means roughly 14 missed Aimed Shots compared to what the reference would fire in the same time. Each Aimed Shot generates Precise Shots buffs that empower the next Arcane Shot — fewer Aimed Shots means fewer Precise Shots consumed, which explains the Arcane Shot over-casting (unbuffed Arcane Shots filling dead time). The Take Aim talent means Rapid Fire reduces Aimed Shot cooldown — the Rapid Fire deficit compounds this problem.

Zero Kill Shots Fired

The reference fired 11 Kill Shots across the fight via Deathblow procs (Aimed Shot has a 20% chance to reset Kill Shot cooldown). Darthurg fired zero. Kill Shot does significant damage and is essentially free when it procs — it should be used immediately after every Aimed Shot that triggers Deathblow. In a 4:53 fight with 32 Aimed Shots, roughly 6-7 Deathblow procs would be expected. Each missed Kill Shot is a high-damage ability that costs nothing to press. The reference's 11 Kill Shots accounted for 4.25M damage (4.6% of total).

Arcane Shot Over-Casting Without Precise Shots

Darthurg casts Arcane Shot at 12.7 CPM vs the reference's 8.2 CPM — 55% more often. In the MM Hunter rotation, Arcane Shot should only be cast when Precise Shots is active (after an Aimed Shot). Casting Arcane Shot without Precise Shots deals reduced damage and wastes Focus that could be used on a Steady Shot to regenerate Focus for the next Aimed Shot. The high Arcane Shot count combined with low Aimed Shot count suggests a pattern of casting unbuffed Arcane Shots during downtime instead of using Steady Shot to regenerate Focus.

Actionable Items

1. Increase Aimed Shot frequency — this is the single biggest DPS lever. At 6.6 CPM vs the reference's 9.5 CPM, Darthurg is missing roughly 14 Aimed Shots per 4:53 of fight time. Aimed Shot is 40.9% of total damage. Each missed Aimed Shot also means one fewer Precise Shots proc, which cascades into lower-quality Arcane Shot casts and fewer Spotter's Mark buffs on subsequent Aimed Shots. The fix: prioritize Aimed Shot over everything else. If Aimed Shot has a charge available or will cap soon, press it immediately. Use Rapid Fire to reduce Aimed Shot cooldown (Take Aim talent). Do not cast Arcane Shot or Steady Shot while Aimed Shot has a charge ready. Estimated gain: +15-20% DPS if Aimed Shot CPM reaches 9.0+.
2. Use Kill Shot on every Deathblow proc — currently zero Kill Shots fired. The Deathblow talent gives Aimed Shot a 20% chance to reset Kill Shot cooldown. With 32 Aimed Shots in this fight, roughly 6 procs were expected — all were missed. The reference dealt 4.25M damage (4.6%) from 11 Kill Shots. Kill Shot should be used immediately after any Aimed Shot that triggers the Deathblow proc — it is essentially a free high-damage ability. Check if Kill Shot is on the action bar and add a WeakAura or alert for Deathblow procs if not already tracking them. Estimated gain: +4-5% DPS.
3. Reduce dead time during movement — 53.8s of gaps in a 293s fight. MM Hunter has multiple instant-cast options for movement: Arcane Shot (with Precise Shots), Rapid Fire (mobile channel), Volley, Explosive Shot. The 15.1s gap at 2:04 and 10.8s gap at 2:48 are the worst offenders. During Crown's phase transitions and movement-heavy mechanics, queue up instant abilities. Pre-position early to minimize forced movement during Aimed Shot hardcasts. Even casting a Steady Shot is better than casting nothing. Estimated gain: +5-8% DPS from recovering 8-10 lost GCDs.
4. Stop casting Arcane Shot without Precise Shots active. Arcane Shot at 12.7 CPM vs 8.2 CPM reference means roughly 22 extra Arcane Shots in 4:53 — many of these are unbuffed casts. The rotation priority is: Aimed Shot > Arcane Shot (with Precise Shots) > Rapid Fire > Steady Shot (Focus regen). When no Precise Shots buff is active and Aimed Shot is on cooldown, use Steady Shot to regenerate Focus for the next Aimed Shot instead of spamming unbuffed Arcane Shots. This improves Focus management and ensures every Arcane Shot hits harder.
5. Cast Moonlight Chakram in every Trueshot window (Sentinel talent). Darthurg used Moonlight Chakram only 1 time vs the reference's 4 times. With the Sentinel hero talent, Moonlight Chakram becomes available after casting Trueshot and should be used as a filler late in the Trueshot window when Aimed Shot is on cooldown. The reference dealt 5.0M damage (5.4%) from Moonlight Chakram. Use it in every Trueshot window to not waste the Sentinel-granted ability.
