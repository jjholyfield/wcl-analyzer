How Resto Shaman Works

Resto Shaman healing revolves around Chain Heal as the primary AoE throughput spell. It bounces to 4–5 targets per cast, and on a fight like Mythic LBV where the raid is constantly taking damage, it should be the ability you press the most by a wide margin. The #1 RSham on this fight casts Chain Heal 17.6 times per minute — roughly every 3.4 seconds. That’s how central it is.

Riptide goes out on cooldown for the HoT and to set up Chain Heal bounces via the Tidal Waves buff. Unleash Life buffs your next heal and should be weaved in before a Chain Heal when available. Healing Stream Totem and Healing Rain are set-and-forget throughput — drop them and keep casting. Surging Totem is part of the same rotation.

Healing Wave is a slow, mana-efficient single-target filler. It heals one person. Chain Heal heals 4–5. On a fight with constant raid damage, Healing Wave should be rare — the #1 RSham only casts it 1.0 times per minute. It’s for when mana is critically low or when there genuinely isn’t raid damage going out, which on LBV is almost never.

The core priority is: Riptide on CD → Unleash Life → Chain Heal → Chain Heal → Chain Heal. Healing Stream Totem and Healing Rain whenever they drop off. Everything else is secondary. Flame Shock and Lava Burst are DPS spells. Top Resto Shamans barely touch them — the #1 casts Flame Shock 0.5 times per minute and Lava Burst zero times. On progression, those GCDs belong to healing.

How It’s Affecting Your Healing

Chain Heal at 11.1/m vs 17.6/m (63%) is the single biggest issue. Chain Heal is 60%+ of Resto Shaman healing output. It heals 4–5 targets per cast. Casting 37% fewer means roughly 37% less AoE healing going into the raid. This one gap accounts for most of the 72% HPS vs the #1.
Healing Wave overcasting at 3.5/m vs 1.0/m. Every Healing Wave is a Chain Heal that didn’t happen. Healing Wave heals one person. Chain Heal heals 4–5. On Mythic LBV where the entire raid is constantly taking damage, single-target healing is almost never the right call. The extra 2.5 Healing Waves per minute should be Chain Heals.
DPS spell overcasting — Flame Shock 3.2/m + Lava Burst 2.1/m = 5.3 DPS casts per minute. The #1 RSham does about 0.5/m in DPS spells. That’s roughly 5 extra GCDs per minute going to damage instead of healing. On progression, the raid can’t afford to lose those healing globals for 5,900 DPS.
Zero mana pots across 30 pulls. Resto Shaman is mana-hungry, especially when Chain Healing at full rate. Without mana pots, you’re forced to downrank to Healing Wave (cheaper but weaker) on longer pulls. This creates a vicious cycle: no mana pot → run low on mana → cast Healing Wave instead of Chain Heal → lower HPS. Use Algari Mana Potion every pull over 3 minutes.

What You Need to Change

1
Chain Heal Way More
Target 15+ Chain Heals per minute. This should be your primary cast on every pull. Between Riptide and Unleash Life, the rest of your GCDs should be Chain Heal. The #1 RSham casts it every 3.4 seconds — that’s the rhythm. If you’re ever wondering what to press next, the answer is Chain Heal.
2
Cut Healing Wave to Under 2/min
Healing Wave should only come out when mana is truly critical or there genuinely isn’t raid-wide damage happening, which on LBV is almost never. You’re casting it 3.5x more than the #1. Every Healing Wave cast is a Chain Heal you didn’t press — that’s 4 people who didn’t get healed.
3
Stop DPSing on Prog
Flame Shock and Lava Burst are costing you ~5 healing GCDs per minute. The best RSham in the world casts Flame Shock 0.5/m and Lava Burst 0/m on this fight. You’re doing 5,900 DPS — the raid isn’t going to notice if it drops to zero, but they will notice the extra 5 Chain Heals per minute.
4
Use Mana Pots
Algari Mana Potion every pull over 3 minutes. RSham burns mana fast when Chain Healing at the rate you should be. Zero mana pots across 30 pulls means you’re either going OOM and forced into Healing Wave, or you’re avoiding Chain Heal to conserve mana. Either way, mana pots fix it. They’re on a separate cooldown from your pre-pot.
