How Holy Paladin Works

Holy Paladin generates Holy Power through builders (Holy Shock, Judgment, Flash of Light, Holy Light) and spends it on Light of Dawn (AoE heal) and Word of Glory/Eternal Flame (single target). The spec has a very high cast rate — you’re constantly pressing buttons.

Holy Shock is your primary builder — cast it on cooldown for Holy Power and Infusion of Light procs. Flash of Light and Holy Light are your main healing spells — Flash is fast and procs instant with Infusion of Light, Holy Light is bigger but slower. Light of Dawn is your primary Holy Power spender for raid healing.

Major CDs: Avenging Wrath (2min throughput), Aura Mastery (raid-wide DR), Divine Toll (burst Holy Shocks), Lay on Hands (emergency), Blessing of Sacrifice (external).

Beacon of Light transfers healing to beacon targets — place on ranged DPS for mastery synergy.

How This Is Affecting Your Healing

HPS at 45% of top — lowest healer on the team by a significant margin. 97K average HPS vs the #1 HPal at 215K. The gap is enormous and it’s coming from spell priority, not uptime alone.
Flash of Light at 11% of top — the single biggest gap across ALL player audits. Top HPals cast FoL 10.3/min. Iconserve casts 1.1/min. Flash of Light is a primary healing spell that generates Holy Power AND heals. Nearly every GCD that should be FoL is going to Judgment, Shield of the Righteous, or Hammer of Wrath instead.
Light of Dawn at 46% of top — primary AoE heal finisher massively underused. With fewer FoL and HL casts generating Holy Power through healing, there’s less HP to spend on Light of Dawn. The problem cascades — fewer healing builders means fewer healing finishers.
Judgment at 250% and Shield of the Righteous at ~870% of top. Combined 6.6/min of DPS abilities. Top players spend 1.6/min on Judgment and ~0.3/min on SotR. On a fight where the raid is dying, these GCDs need to be healing spells. Every SotR and Judgment cast is a Flash of Light or Holy Light that didn’t happen.
31% early death rate — dies in nearly 1 in 3 pulls. Combined with 0% health potion usage and only 38% healthstone usage, survivability consumables are being left on the table.
Blessing of Sacrifice barely used (0.0/min). This external defensive should be used on tanks during big hits. It’s a 2-minute cooldown that provides meaningful damage reduction to the target.
CPM at 77% of top — not pressing buttons fast enough on top of wrong priorities. 64.7 CPM vs 83.9 for the #1 HPal. Even if spell priority were fixed, 77% CPM means ~23% of potential casts are missing. The combination of low CPM AND wrong spell priority compounds the HPS deficit.

What You Need to Change

1
More Flash of Light — 1.1/min needs to be 10+
This is the single biggest HPS gain available. FoL generates Holy Power, heals the target, and procs Infusion of Light. Every FoL you’re not casting is healing you’re not doing. Top HPals cast 10.3 Flash of Light per minute. You cast 1.1. This one change alone would dramatically close the HPS gap.
2
More Light of Dawn — 4.6/min needs to be 10+
Primary AoE finisher for spending Holy Power. With more FoL and HL generating HP, you’ll have more to spend on Light of Dawn. This is your main raid healing spender — at 46% of top, half the AoE healing is missing.
3
Less Judgment and Shield of the Righteous — cut to under 2/min total
Combined 6.6/min of DPS abilities. On progression where people are dying, every DPS GCD is a heal that didn’t happen. Cut Judgment from 4.0 to ~1.5 and SotR from 2.6 to near zero. Hammer of Wrath (1.0/min) should also be cut entirely. Replace all of these GCDs with Flash of Light and Holy Light.
4
Use healthstone and health potion
38% healthstone usage, 0% health potion, 31% death rate. These are free survivability on separate cooldowns. Use them when you take damage. Dying in 1 out of 3 pulls means the raid is 4-healing for a third of the night.
