Renewing Mist Coverage Gap

Renewing Mist at 7.3 CPM vs 11.1 CPM is 35% below reference — roughly 17 fewer casts over the fight (scaled for time). Renewing Mist is the foundation of the Mistweaver Conduit of the Celestials build: it drives Vivify cleave targeting (Vivify bounces to targets with Renewing Mist), Rising Mist HoT extension via Rushing Wind Kick, and generates procs for the talent tree. With fewer Renewing Mists out, every Vivify and every Rushing Wind Kick heals fewer targets. This single gap cascades into lower throughput on multiple abilities. The reference never lets Renewing Mist reach 2 charges — it should be cast on cooldown at all times.

Mana Tea Under-Use

Mana Tea at 1.6 CPM vs 4.7 CPM (−66%) means Senssay is consuming Mana Tea stacks far less frequently than the reference. In the current Mistweaver design, Mana Tea stacks accumulate passively and should be consumed regularly to sustain higher cast volume. The reference uses Mana Tea nearly 5 times per minute to keep mana flowing into additional Vivifies and Renewing Mists. Under-consuming Mana Tea likely contributes to the lower Vivify and Renewing Mist CPM — there may be mana pressure preventing more casts that Mana Tea would alleviate.

Enveloping Mist Usage

Enveloping Mist at 8.2 CPM vs 6.7 CPM (+22%) is above the reference. This indicates strong spot-healing and tank maintenance — Enveloping Mist provides significant single-target HoT and amplifies healing received. The higher CPM suggests Senssay is proactive with targeted healing on players taking damage. However, some of these globals might be better spent on Renewing Mist if targets are not critically low.

Rushing Wind Kick & Rising Mist

Rushing Wind Kick at 9.1 CPM vs 11.1 CPM (−18%) means fewer Rising Mist triggers to extend HoTs across the raid. Each Rushing Wind Kick heals all targets with Renewing Mist or Enveloping Mist and extends those HoTs by 4 seconds. With both Rushing Wind Kick AND Renewing Mist below reference, the compounding effect is significant — fewer HoTs out AND fewer extensions. This directly explains why Renewing Mist effective healing (10.3M vs 27.3M) scales disproportionately with the CPM gap.

Actionable Items

1. Increase Renewing Mist coverage — never let it reach 2 charges. At 7.3 CPM vs the reference's 11.1 CPM, roughly 35% fewer Renewing Mists are being applied. This is the single highest-impact improvement available. Renewing Mist is the foundation of the Conduit of the Celestials Mistweaver build: Vivify cleaves to targets with Renewing Mist, Rushing Wind Kick extends Renewing Mist via Rising Mist, and multiple talent procs trigger from Renewing Mist applications. With fewer HoTs out, every subsequent ability is less effective. The reference casts Renewing Mist every 5.4 seconds on average — it should be treated as the #1 priority button at all times. Set a weakaura to alert when Renewing Mist has 2 charges to prevent overcapping.
2. Consume Mana Tea stacks more frequently. At 1.6 CPM vs 4.7 CPM, Mana Tea is being used roughly a third as often as the reference. Mana Tea stacks passively and should be consumed on cooldown to sustain spell output. The reference uses it nearly 5 times per minute, which enables the higher Vivify and Renewing Mist casting rates. Under-consuming Mana Tea likely creates mana pressure that forces fewer casts overall. Build a habit of consuming Mana Tea as part of the core rotation cycle, not just when low on mana.
3. Increase Rushing Wind Kick usage to maximize Rising Mist extension. At 9.1 CPM vs 11.1 CPM (−18%), Rushing Wind Kick is being used less frequently than the reference. Each Rushing Wind Kick heals all targets with active HoTs AND extends those HoTs by 4 seconds via Rising Mist. Combined with the lower Renewing Mist coverage, this compounds — fewer HoTs out AND fewer extensions means the HoT blanket decays faster. Rushing Wind Kick should be cast on cooldown, even during low-damage windows, because the HoT extension value is always relevant.
4. Increase Vivify usage during damage windows. Vivify at 10.5 CPM vs 13.4 CPM (−22%) means approximately 13 fewer Vivify casts over the fight (time-adjusted). Vivify is the primary burst heal and cleaves to targets with Renewing Mist. With more Renewing Mists out (item #1) and more frequent Mana Tea (item #2), there should be both more mana and more valid targets to make each Vivify more impactful. The Enveloping Mist CPM is +22% above reference — some of those globals may be better invested in Vivify when multiple targets need healing simultaneously.
5. Time Revival for maximum effective healing. Revival at 1:45 had 47% overheal vs the reference's 15%. Revival is a 3-minute cooldown that should be reserved for the highest raid-wide damage windows. In Crown of the Cosmos, Phase 3 mechanics (Void Expulsion + escalating Echoing Darkness + Voidstalker Sting) create the most healing pressure. Consider holding Revival for Phase 3 rather than using it in Phase 1 where damage is more manageable with standard throughput.
