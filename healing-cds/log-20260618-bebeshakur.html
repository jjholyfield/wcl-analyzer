Actionable Improvements

1. Use Icebound Fortitude in P2 (Critical)

Issue: 1 IBF cast across 14 pulls while dying to sustained P2 damage every single pull.
Impact: IBF provides 30% damage reduction for 8 seconds on a 3-minute cooldown. Used proactively during Simulacrum Backlash + Voidstalker Sting overlaps (the exact combination killing Bebeshakur 8+ times), this would prevent the majority of deaths.
Fix: Plan IBF usage for the first major damage overlap window in P2 (~3:30-4:00 mark). With a 3-minute CD, a second use is available in late P2 / early P3 if needed. This alone could double P3 reach rate.

2. Increase Death Strike Usage as Emergency Self-Heal

Issue: 6 Death Strikes across 14 pulls (0.4 per pull). Death Strike heals for a significant amount based on recent damage taken.
Impact: In the death sequences, Bebeshakur is typically taking 60-120K damage over 3-5 seconds before dying. A single Death Strike in that window would heal back enough to survive the finishing blow.
Fix: When below 40% HP in P2 and multiple damage sources are ticking, spend Runic Power on Death Strike instead of Death Coil. The DPS loss from 1-2 Death Coils is negligible compared to dying and doing 0 DPS for the rest of the pull.

3. Use Anti-Magic Shell More Aggressively in P2

Issue: AMS averaging 2.9 uses per pull. On a 45-second cooldown with pulls lasting 4+ minutes, 4-5 uses are possible and warranted.
Impact: AMS absorbs magic damage -- and every ability killing Bebeshakur in P2 (Simulacrum Backlash, Cosmic Barrier, Void Barrage, Voidstalker Sting) is magic damage. AMS also generates Runic Power from absorbed damage, making it both defensive AND offensive.
Fix: Use AMS on cooldown in P2, especially before known damage overlap windows. Prioritize it before Grasp of Emptiness casts and during Simulacrum Backlash stacks. The absorbed damage converts to Runic Power for more Death Coils.

4. Use Healthstone Every Pull

Issue: 3 Healthstone uses across 14 pulls. It is a free heal on a per-pull cooldown.
Impact: Several death sequences show Bebeshakur dying to a 10-40K finishing blow after being chipped down. A Healthstone used at 50% HP would have survived those.
Fix: Add Healthstone to a keybind and use it when below 50% HP during P2 damage overlaps. Also ensure a Potion of Recklessness (or defensive potion) is available -- only 1 potion use across 14 pulls.

5. What Is Working Well

P1 execution is excellent -- 368K average DPS with zero deaths across 14 pulls. Cleave/AoE target switching on sentinels is clean.

Offensive CD alignment is correct -- Dark Transformation and Army of the Dead are consistently paired, Blood Fury aligns with burst windows, and CDs are not held unnecessarily.

CPM is good -- 48.4 CPM in P1 and 52.9 CPM in P2 on the best pull shows strong GCD usage. The rotation itself is not the problem.

Clear improvement trend -- 33.9K average in the first half vs 38.8K in the second half (+14.4%). Learning the fight mechanics through the session.

Summary

Verdict: Bebeshakur's DPS gap is survival-based, not rotation-based. The Unholy DK rotation execution is solid -- good CPM, proper CD alignment, strong P1 cleave. The entire problem is dying in P2 on every pull due to sustained rot damage from Simulacrum Backlash + Voidstalker Sting + Grasp of Emptiness overlaps. The fix is straightforward: use Icebound Fortitude, use Death Strike as an emergency heal, use AMS more aggressively, and use Healthstone. These four changes together would likely convert most P2 deaths into P3 entries, which is the progression gate for the entire raid.
