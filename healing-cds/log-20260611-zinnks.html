Augmentation Evoker — Support Spec Context

Augmentation is a support DPS spec. Personal DPS is low by design — the spec amplifies allies through Ebon Might (stat buff on 4 targets), Prescience (crit buff), and Shifting Sands (versatility). The key performance metrics are Ebon Might uptime, Prescience usage, Eruption CPM (extends Ebon Might), and empowered spell frequency (Fire Breath + Upheaval). Raw DPS numbers should not be compared against pure DPS specs.

Eruption Deficit — The Core Problem

Eruption is the engine that keeps Ebon Might running. Each Eruption cast extends the duration of Ebon Might through the Sands of Time passive. At 12.7 CPM vs 16.6 CPM, Zinnks is missing roughly 16 Eruption casts per 4 minutes of alive time (extrapolated). This directly causes lower Ebon Might uptime (61% vs 85%) because the buff expires faster without extensions. The gap is filled partially with Azure Strike (1.7 CPM vs 0.7) which does less damage and does not extend Ebon Might. Every Azure Strike that could have been an Eruption is a missed EM extension.

Living Flame Gap — 55% Below Reference

Living Flame at 4.4 CPM vs 9.8 CPM is the largest rate gap. Living Flame is the primary filler between Eruption casts when Essence Burst procs are unavailable and no empowered spell is ready. A 55% deficit suggests significant periods of not pressing buttons — either movement without Hover usage, unfamiliarity with when to cast filler, or GCD time lost to defensive/survival casts. The reference casts Hover at the same rate (3.4 vs 3.2 CPM), so movement itself is not the differentiator — it's what happens during movement. Living Flame is instant with the Leaping Flames talent, so it should be castable while moving with Hover.

Actionable Items

1. Increase Eruption CPM from 12.7 to 16+ to maintain Ebon Might uptime. This is the single most impactful improvement. Eruption extends Ebon Might through Sands of Time — every missed Eruption shortens the buff window for 4 allied DPS players. The 24% CPM gap directly causes the 24% Ebon Might uptime gap (61% vs 85%). In practical terms: cast Eruption whenever you have an Essence Burst proc or when Ebon Might needs extension, even during movement (Eruption is instant with Essence Burst). Prioritize Eruption over Azure Strike in every situation where Ebon Might is active. Estimated raid DPS gain: significant — 24% more EM uptime means 24% more time that 4 DPS allies receive your stat buff.
2. Fill dead GCDs with Living Flame — current rate is 55% below reference. Living Flame at 4.4 CPM vs 9.8 CPM indicates substantial periods of not pressing buttons. When no Eruption is available (no Essence Burst procs, Ebon Might not active), Living Flame should be the default filler. With the Leaping Flames talent, Living Flame becomes instant after empowered spells and can be cast while moving with Hover. The reference casts Living Flame 75 times in 7:38 — it should be the most common cast after Eruption. Map Living Flame to an easily accessible keybind and treat it as the "when nothing else is ready, press this" button. Estimated personal DPS gain: ~15-20% from filling dead GCDs.
3. Replace Azure Strike filler with Eruption or Living Flame. Azure Strike is at 1.7 CPM vs 0.7 CPM (+161%). Azure Strike is lower priority than both Eruption (extends Ebon Might) and Living Flame (higher damage, procs Leaping Flames). The reference uses Azure Strike sparingly (5 total casts in 7:38). Every Azure Strike that could be an Eruption is a missed Ebon Might extension. Use Azure Strike only as a last-resort instant when moving without Hover and both Eruption and Living Flame are unavailable.
4. Increase Upheaval usage to match Fire Breath cadence. Fire Breath is at a good rate (2.0 CPM, near reference 2.1), but Upheaval is lower at 1.7 CPM vs 2.1 CPM (−18%). Both empowered spells extend Ebon Might through Sands of Time and grant Shifting Sands to nearby allies. They should be used on cooldown with similar frequency. Missing Upheaval casts means fewer EM extensions and fewer Shifting Sands buff applications. On a ~12 second cooldown, Upheaval should be cast roughly every 2 GCD windows.
5. Keep Prescience on cooldown — avoid capping charges. Prescience at 4.6 CPM vs 5.6 CPM (−18%) means fewer crit buffs distributed to DPS allies. Prescience is a two-charge system that should never cap at 2 charges. Between Eruption casts and empowered spells, weave Prescience applications to keep the charges rolling. Target the highest-DPS players in the raid (check your Details/WCL for who is doing the most damage). Even during prog movement, Prescience is instant and costs no movement time.
What's Already Good

Cooldown management: Breath of Eons, Fire Breath, and Tip the Scales are all used at correct rates. Fire Breath at 2.0 CPM nearly matches the reference's 2.1 — empowered spell timing is solid.

Ebon Might recast awareness: Ebon Might itself was recast 7 times (1.7 CPM), slightly above the reference's 1.6 CPM. Zinnks is aware of when EM falls off and recasts it — the problem is maintaining it between recasts via Eruption extensions, not forgetting to recast.

Defensive awareness: 4 Obsidian Scales uses and 3 Emerald Blossom self-heals show active survival play during progression. 2 Zephyr casts contributed raid-wide damage reduction. This defensive awareness is valuable during prog even if it costs some GCDs.

Best pulls showed strong burst: On the three 0.01% boss pulls (F8: 280K, F17: 256K, F21: 262K), Zinnks put up strong numbers during burst windows, indicating good Breath of Eons and empowered spell alignment during cooldown windows.

Progression Context

This analysis compares wipe prog data against kill data from the #1 ranked Aug Evoker. Prog pulls are inherently messier: more deaths cause panic movement, healers go into survival mode, DPS drop, and the Aug Evoker's buff targets die mid-buff. However, the Eruption and Living Flame CPM gaps are rate-based metrics independent of ally deaths — these represent genuine rotation improvement opportunities that will carry over once the team secures a kill. Closing the Eruption gap alone would bring Ebon Might uptime closer to 75-80%, which translates to meaningful DPS gains for the entire raid.
