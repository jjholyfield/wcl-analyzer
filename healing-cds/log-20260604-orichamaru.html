How Demonology Warlock Works

Demonology Warlock is about summoning and empowering demons. The core loop is: generate Soul Shards by casting Shadow Bolt or Demonbolt → spend shards on Hand of Gul’dan (summons imps) and Call Dreadstalkers → when you have enough demons out, cast Summon Demonic Tyrant to empower all active demons and extend their duration.

Hand of Gul’dan is both your main shard spender and your primary damage source through the imps it spawns. Demonbolt is an empowered version of Shadow Bolt that generates more shards — you get Demonbolt procs from summoning demons. Prioritizing these procs means faster shard generation means more Hand of Gul’dan casts.

The key to Demo Lock DPS is maximizing Hand of Gul’dan casts (more imps = more damage), getting full Tyrant windows with as many demons active as possible, and keeping Dreadstalkers on cooldown. Implosion detonates your imps for AoE damage during multi-target windows. Ruination is your big burst cooldown.

How It’s Affecting Your Damage

Hand of Gul’dan at 74% of #1 is the biggest DPS gap. 12.3/m vs 16.7/m means roughly 4.4 fewer imp-summoning casts every minute. Imps are your primary passive damage source, and having fewer imps out when Tyrant hits means weaker burst windows too. This single gap is the largest contributor to the 54K DPS difference.
Demonbolt at 73% of #1 is starving your shard generation. 9.1/m vs 12.4/m means Demonbolt procs are being missed or deprioritized. Every Demonbolt generates more shards than Shadow Bolt — fewer Demonbolts means slower shard income means fewer Hand of Gul’dan casts. This directly feeds the HoG gap.
Ruination and Infernal Bolt at 75-76% of #1. These are your burst cooldowns. Missing casts here means lost burst damage during key windows. Combined with the lower imp count from fewer HoG casts, burst phases are significantly weaker than they should be.
4 early deaths (13%) across 4 different mechanics despite having a strong defensive kit (Dark Pact, Unending Resolve, Mortal Coil). Healthstone at 33% and Health Potion at 10% means the defensive toolkit isn’t being fully utilized when damage spikes hit.

What You Need to Change

1
More Hand of Gul’dan
Target 15+ Hand of Gul’dan per minute. You’re at 12.3, the #1 is at 16.7. Cast HoG as soon as you have 3 shards — don’t sit on them. Every delayed HoG is imps not spawning, damage not happening, and a weaker Tyrant window. This is the single biggest lever for closing the DPS gap.
2
Prioritize Demonbolt Procs
When you get a Demonbolt proc, cast it immediately over Shadow Bolt. You’re at 9.1/m vs 12.4/m for the #1. Each Demonbolt generates more shards than Shadow Bolt, which means faster HoG casts. Shadow Bolt is already at 97% of #1 — the issue isn’t casting too few fillers, it’s casting the wrong filler when procs are available.
3
Use Healthstones and Health Potions
10% Health Potion usage and 33% Healthstone usage is too low, especially for a Warlock who creates their own healthstones. You have Dark Pact, Unending Resolve, Mortal Coil, Singe Magic, Healthstone, AND Health Potion — that’s one of the deepest defensive kits in the raid. Use it all. The 4 early deaths to 4 different mechanics suggest damage intake that any of these could have survived.
4
Keep Pre-Pot Perfect
30/30 Light’s Potential is exactly right. Keep doing this.
