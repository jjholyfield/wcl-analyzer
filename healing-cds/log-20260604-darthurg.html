How Marksmanship Hunter Works

Marksmanship Hunter is a ranged DPS spec built around a rhythm of building Focus with Steady Shot and spending it on hard-hitting abilities — particularly Aimed Shot, the spec’s signature move.

The core loop is: use Aimed Shot on cooldown (highest priority, hardest hitting ability), fill with Arcane Shot (instant Focus spender that procs Precise Shots), and weave Steady Shot when you need Focus. Rapid Fire is a channeled burst that generates Focus and should be used on CD.

The rhythm is: Aimed Shot → instant Arcane Shots during Precise Shots procs → Steady Shot to regen → repeat. Trueshot is your major burst cooldown that speeds up Aimed Shot and resets its CD. Aimed Shot is always the priority — it hits the hardest and everything else revolves around its cast windows.

How It’s Affecting Your Damage

Arcane Shot at 29% of #1 is the biggest red flag. 5.1/m vs 17.8/m — that’s nearly 13 missing Arcane Shots per minute. Darthurg appears to be using Multi-Shot (4.5/m) instead of Arcane Shot as the primary filler. The top MM Hunters don’t use Multi-Shot at all on this fight. This may be a talent issue, a keybind issue, or a rotation priority issue. With only 3 pulls, it’s hard to say if this is consistent long-term, but the pattern held across all 3 pulls.
28.3% dead time means more than 1 in 4 seconds doing nothing. For a ranged spec with no melee uptime requirements, this is extremely high. Even after accounting for movement on mechanics, a ranged DPS should be under 15%. Marksmanship can cast on the move with Arcane Shot — there’s almost never a reason to stop pressing buttons.
Aimed Shot at 63% of #1. 9.5/m vs 15.1/m — missing nearly 6 Aimed Shots per minute. This is the hardest-hitting ability in the kit. Every missed Aimed Shot is the single biggest DPS loss possible. Aimed Shot should be pressed on cooldown, every cooldown, without exception.
DPS at 48% of #1 — 130K vs 269K. With only 3 pulls and limited play time, part of this gap is explained by shorter exposure and less optimization time. But the rotation gaps above (Arcane Shot, dead time, Aimed Shot) are real and consistent across all 3 active pulls. The underlying issues exist independent of sample size.

What You Need to Change

1
More Arcane Shot, Less Multi-Shot
The top MM Hunters use Arcane Shot as the primary instant spender, not Multi-Shot. 5.1/m vs 17.8/m is a 71% deficit. Check your talents and rotation priority — on a single-target fight, Arcane Shot should be your go-to filler between Aimed Shots. Multi-Shot is for sustained AoE, not single-target.
2
Reduce Dead Time
28.3% means over a quarter of the fight is wasted. Target under 15%. Marksmanship has instant-cast options (Arcane Shot, Kill Shot) that can be used while moving. There should never be a point where you’re standing still with nothing queued. Always be casting — if you can’t Aimed Shot, Arcane Shot. If you can’t Arcane Shot, Steady Shot.
3
More Aimed Shot
Target 14+ Aimed Shots per minute. Currently at 9.5. This is the highest damage ability in the entire kit and should be cast on cooldown without exception. During Trueshot windows, Aimed Shot resets and should be spammed. Outside of Trueshot, it should still be the first thing you press when it comes off cooldown.
4
More Play Time Needed
3 pulls is not enough data to draw definitive conclusions, but the patterns above are consistent across all 3 pulls. The rotation gaps (Arcane Shot usage, dead time, Aimed Shot frequency) need to be addressed before the next raid night so the next set of data tells a clearer story.
