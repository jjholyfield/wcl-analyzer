How Feral Druid Works

Feral Druid is a melee spec built around maintaining bleeds (damage-over-time effects) while spending combo points on finishers. The core loop is: apply Rake (bleed, generates combo points) → Shred (builder, generates combo points) → at 5 combo points, spend on Ferocious Bite (single target finisher) or Primal Wrath (AoE finisher that also applies Rip to all targets).

Rake must be kept active on targets at all times — it deals strong damage per tick and generates combo points. Shred is your primary builder between Rake refreshes. Tiger’s Fury is a short CD that buffs your damage and restores energy — use on cooldown. Berserk and Convoke the Spirits are your major burst CDs.

The key to Feral is bleed uptime. If Rake falls off, you lose both the bleed damage AND the combo points from reapplying. Feral Druids who let bleeds drop lose DPS disproportionately because the entire rotation cascades from bleed management.

How It’s Affecting Your Damage

Rake at 52% of #1 is catastrophic. Rake is the foundational ability of Feral Druid. Half the Rake casts means half the bleed damage, which means fewer combo points generated, which means fewer finishers. The entire rotation cascades from Rake — when it falls off targets, everything downstream suffers. This alone explains a massive chunk of the DPS gap.
Shred at 40% of #1 is the biggest gap on the sheet. Shred is the primary combo point builder between Rake refreshes. Moistbear is casting 3.4/m vs 8.4/m — less than half the rate. If you’re not pressing Shred, you’re not generating combo points, and your finishers dry up.
Swipe overuse is where the missing globals are going. 14.9/m vs 11.0/m — Moistbear is pressing Swipe 35% more than the #1. Swipe is the weakest button in the Feral toolkit. Every Swipe press that should have been a Shred or Rake is direct DPS left on the table.
Primal Wrath at 52% compounds the finisher problem. Primal Wrath is the AoE finisher that applies Rip to all targets. At 3.4/m vs 6.5/m, Moistbear is missing nearly half the AoE finisher casts, which means Rip is falling off targets constantly during multi-target windows.
CPM at 73% of #1 is the team’s biggest gap. 43.1 vs 59.1 casts per minute. Even if every ability were the right one, pressing 27% fewer buttons means 27% less damage. Combined with the wrong ability priorities, this is a compounding problem.

What You Need to Change

1
More Rake — Target 8+ Per Minute
Currently at 4.5/m vs 8.6/m for the #1. Rake must be active on all targets at all times. This is the single highest priority. Every Rake tick is damage, and every Rake application is a combo point. If Rake is falling off, everything else in the rotation breaks down.
2
More Shred — Target 8+ Per Minute
Currently at 3.4/m vs 8.4/m for the #1. Shred is the primary builder, not Swipe. On single target and cleave, Shred should be your go-to button between Rake refreshes. It generates combo points and deals more damage than Swipe.
3
Less Swipe — Swipe is for 3+ Targets Only
Currently at 14.9/m vs 11.0/m for the #1. You’re pressing Swipe when you should be pressing Shred. Swipe is the weakest AoE filler in the Feral kit. Use it only when there are 3 or more targets stacked together. On 1-2 targets, Shred is always better.
4
More Primal Wrath — Target 6+ Per Minute
Currently at 3.4/m vs 6.5/m for the #1. Primal Wrath is the AoE finisher that applies Rip to all targets. When there are 2+ targets, Primal Wrath replaces Ferocious Bite as your finisher. Missing Primal Wraths means Rip is falling off targets during AoE windows.
5
Use Healthstones More
12/30 (40%) needs to be higher. You’re pre-potting every pull which is great, but Healthstones are free healing on a separate cooldown. Use them when you take big damage — they can be the difference between dying and staying alive for another 30 seconds of DPS.
