Glazedwhole is performing as a reliable survivability anchor on Crown of the Cosmos progression. Across 19 usable pulls, only 1 actionable death (fight 14, Intermission at 2:35 -- first death in the raid, to Orbiting Matter during Stellar Emission). The other 18 deaths were all cascade (death #3 or later), meaning the raid collapsed around him, not because of him. More Heart Strikes = more Runic Power = more Death Strikes = more Blood Shields and self-healing. Actionable Items

1. Increase Death Strike CPM -- 10.4 vs 14.8 Reference (30% deficit)
Death Strike is the primary self-heal and Blood Shield generator. At 10.4 CPM, Glazedwhole is pressing Death Strike 30% less than Akavinny (14.8 CPM). This means fewer Blood Shields, less self-healing, and more reliance on external healing. The fix is chaining more Heart Strikes to generate Runic Power, then spending it on Death Strike. Heart Strike CPM is also low (7.1 vs 12.4) -- these are linked. Prioritize Heart Strike as the primary rune spender to fuel Death Strike.
2. Increase Total CPM -- 53.1 vs 69.8 Reference (24% deficit)
A 24% gap in total abilities pressed per minute means significant rotational downtime. On a 433-second fight, this represents approximately 120 missed button presses. Focus on maintaining GCD uptime -- always have something queued. Use Blood Boil and Death's Caress as fillers when runes are regenerating and Runic Power is too low for Death Strike. During Gift of the San'layn windows, chain Vampiric Strike and Death Strike rapidly to maximize the burst window.
3. Use AMS for Magic-Heavy Mechanics
Simulacrum Backlash (4.1% of total damage) had only 3% absorption, and Cosmic Radiation (1.0%) had 1% absorption. Both are magic damage that Anti-Magic Shell mitigates. AMS usage is already 3 casts per long pull (good frequency), but timing it for Simulacrum Backlash in P2 or Cosmic Radiation in P3 would reduce magic damage intake. Consider banking AMS specifically for Cosmic Radiation in P3, where incoming damage is highest.
4. Intermission Survival -- Pre-Cast a Defensive Before Stellar Emission
The one actionable death was during Intermission (fight 14 at 2:35) to Orbiting Matter + Stellar Emission with no major defensive active. DRW was not used until 2:56 and Vampiric Blood until 2:58 -- both came after the death. Pre-casting a defensive (IBF or AMS) before the Intermission Stellar Emission channel would prevent this. The Intermission starts on a fixed timer at 2:14 -- plan the defensive window.
5. Strength: Defensive CD Discipline Is Excellent
Vampiric Blood used in every long pull (6.3 avg), DRW in every pull (2.9 avg), AMS in every pull (3.0 avg). Lichborne used proactively for leech in 11/11 pulls. IBF used in 10/11 pulls. This is above-reference defensive usage -- Glazedwhole is pressing defensive buttons more often than kill references, which is correct behavior for prog.
6. Strength: Exceptional Survivability -- Last Player Standing in Wipes
Only 1 actionable death in 19 pulls. In the other 18 deaths, Glazedwhole was death #3 to #22 -- the raid collapsed around them while they stayed alive. This is the ideal profile for a prog tank: staying alive while others die, giving the raid the best possible chance to recover or learn more of the fight. Multiple P3 pulls (6:58-7:12) demonstrate extended survival under the hardest mechanics.
