Actionable Items

1. Press Death Strike more. 12.3/min vs reference 17.5/min is a 30% deficit on the single most important Blood DK button. Death Strike generates Blood Shield absorbs, heals, and deals damage. More Death Strikes means more mitigation, more self-healing, and more DPS. This is the #1 priority regardless of build.
2. Use Vampiric Blood aggressively, not defensively. 4 uses in 7:58 vs reference's 14 uses in 8:20. Top Blood DKs treat VB as a rotational Death Strike amplifier (+30% healing for 10s), pressing it on cooldown. Glazedwhole saves it for emergencies, leaving massive uptime on the table. With Improved Vampiric Blood reducing the cooldown, it should be pressed every 35-40 seconds.
3. Reduce Blood Boil and Marrowrend casts. Blood Boil at 11.3/min (+52% over reference) and Marrowrend at 3.8/min (+75%) suggest GCDs are being spent on maintenance when they should be spent on Death Strike and Heart Strike. Bone Shield is already 99.4% uptime — Marrowrend can be pressed less. Blood Boil should only be used for Blood Plague application and when it's free from Crimson Scourge procs.
4. Increase Death and Decay usage. 3.0/min vs reference 5.0/min (-40%). Death and Decay empowers Heart Strike to hit additional targets and is a core part of the AoE rotation, especially on Rotmire with frequent add spawns.
5. Close the CPM gap. 59.2 total CPM vs 84.8 is a 25.6 cast-per-minute deficit. Some of this is build-dependent (San'layn has more proc-based casts), but even accounting for that, there's significant GCD downtime. Focus on always being casting — if nothing else is available, press Heart Strike or Death Strike.
6. Consider whether Rider is the right build. All top 5 Blood DKs on this boss run San'layn. San'layn provides more self-healing (Vampiric Strike), more passive damage (Infliction of Sorrow), and more casts per minute (proc-based abilities). The Rider build may be better for other content, but the mythic ranking data heavily favors San'layn for progression.
