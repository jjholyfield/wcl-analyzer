Actionable Items

1. Survive P1 mechanics — 44% of deaths happen before Intermission

Bursting Emptiness (5 deaths), Grasp of Emptiness (3 deaths), and Void Expulsion (3 deaths) are the top P1 killers. These are all avoidable: Bursting Emptiness requires soaking void droplets before they burst, Grasp of Emptiness requires moving out of the grip zone, and Void Expulsion is a frontal cone to dodge. Consider mapping Divine Protection to specific P1 events: DP at 0:27 for the first Void Expulsion window, DP at 1:30 for the second wave. Currently DP timing varies from 0:25 to 1:08 across pulls — consistency would help survival.

2. Save Lay on Hands — used at 0:12 in every single pull

Lay on Hands is a 10-minute emergency cooldown being spent in the first 12 seconds of every qualifying pull. At pull start, healing demand is at its lowest. Holding LoH for a genuine emergency in P2 or P3 — a tank about to die during Rift Slash, or self-healing during P3 Cosmic Radiation overlap — adds a critical safety net when damage is highest. This single change could prevent some of the P2/P3 deaths.

3. Coordinate P1 CDs with the healing team to stop stacking at pull

In F23, Dueche AW + Brewtote Asc + Silencio Convoke/Tranq all fire within 15 seconds of the pull. P1 damage is manageable and does not require 3 healing CDs simultaneously. Stagger: one major CD at pull (AW), one at 0:25-0:30 for Null Corona/Grasp overlap, one at 1:00 for Void Expulsion. Save at least one CD for the Intermission Stellar Emission channel (2:22-2:52).

4. Map Divine Protection to known P1 damage events

DP is used 17/19 pulls (good rate) but timing varies significantly (0:25-2:35 range). With Crown of the Cosmos having fixed boss timers, DP should be planned: ~0:27 for first Void Expulsion, ~1:30 for second Void Expulsion/Grasp overlap, and ~2:22 for Intermission Stellar Emission start. Proactive DP timing would directly address the P1 and Intermission death problem.

5. Target Holy Shock on injured players to reduce 52.9% overheal

Holy Shock must be pressed on cooldown for Holy Power generation and Dawnlight procs. However, over half of its healing is wasted on full-health targets. Using health-deficit-sorted raid frames to pick Holy Shock targets would improve effective healing output, especially in P2/P3 where every heal matters. This is a minor optimization compared to survival.
