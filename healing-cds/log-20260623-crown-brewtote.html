Bottom Line

Brewtote died in all 20 Crown pulls. Average survival time was 210 seconds, meaning most deaths occur late in P2 or early P3 as damage ramps up. The most common death causes are Cosmic Barrier (4), Grasp of Emptiness (3), and Void Remnants (3), with 7 deaths having no single killing blow (accumulated damage). HPS while alive is solid at 156K average with healthy 26.6% overheal, and CD discipline is excellent -- Spirit Link Totem is deployed at exactly ~26.5s into every pull with near-zero variance. The core issue is survival, not throughput. Brewtote needs to use personal defensives (Astral Shift, Spiritwalker's Grace) more aggressively during high-damage windows and prioritize positioning to avoid Grasp of Emptiness and Cosmic Barrier overlap kills.

Actionable Items

1. Survival is the #1 priority -- 0/20 pulls survived

Brewtote died in every single Crown pull. Average survival time is 210 seconds (3:30), with most deaths occurring in P2. The throughput numbers are solid (156K HPS, 26.6% overheal) -- this is not a healing performance problem, it is a personal survival problem. Use Astral Shift more aggressively during Cosmic Barrier overlap and Grasp of Emptiness windows. In fight 25, Astral Shift was used 3 times in 6:11 -- the ~90s cooldown allows 4 uses. Pre-cast Astral Shift before known damage spikes rather than reactively.

2. Cosmic Barrier kills (4 deaths) -- position and defensive before the wall

Cosmic Barrier was the single deadliest named ability, killing Brewtote 4 times in P2/P3. This is the barrier wall that appears during phase transitions. Brewtote needs to: (a) pre-position closer to the safe side of the barrier, (b) have Astral Shift available for the barrier window, and (c) top personal health before the barrier spawns. Consider using Gust of Wind to quickly reposition when the barrier appears.

3. Grasp of Emptiness awareness (3 deaths) -- save Gust of Wind

Grasp of Emptiness is a void grip/pull mechanic that killed Brewtote 3 times (2 in P3, 1 in P2). This is avoidable with positioning and movement cooldowns. Gust of Wind (3 uses in F25) should be held specifically for Grasp windows when possible. Ghost Wolf can also help break free. In P3 where Grasp overlaps with other mechanics, defensive + movement CDs are essential.

4. Consider holding Ascendance for P2 instead of P1 opener

Currently, Ascendance is used at ~7s in 18/20 pulls (P1 opener). P1 damage is relatively manageable -- the team has 4 healers alive and boss adds are dying. P2 is where 71% of Brewtote's deaths occur. Holding Ascendance for the start of P2 (~175s) would provide a major throughput CD during the deadliest phase and might improve survival by having stronger self-sustain when damage ramps up. The first Ascendance use could shift from 7s to ~45-60s (after Null Corona) or directly to P2 entry.

5. CD discipline is excellent -- maintain this consistency

Spirit Link Totem at 26.6s average with 0.4s standard deviation across 20 pulls is outstanding. Ascendance timing is also very consistent. Both CDs are used in every pull (100% usage rate). This level of CD discipline shows Brewtote has internalized the fight timing well. Once survival improves and pulls consistently reach P3, a third Ascendance use should become possible.
