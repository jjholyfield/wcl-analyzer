Bottom Line — Progression Context

This is progression data from 26 wipes on Mythic Crown of the Cosmos — no kill yet. On the longest pull (Fight 23, 4:52), Nurnyx dealt 163.7K DPS but died at 4:14 to Void Remnants. While alive (254 seconds), effective DPS was ~208K. Compared to the #4 ranked reference Fabx (104.7K DPS over a 9:03 kill with 0 talent differences), Nurnyx's alive-time DPS is actually strong — but the comparison is heavily skewed because prog pulls involve shorter windows of burst-biased damage vs a full-length kill. The core issues are rotational CPM (34.0 vs 38.8), a -17% Mind Blast CPM gap, -15% Shadow Word: Madness CPM gap, and -25% Mind Flay CPM gap. The biggest actionable improvement is reducing dead GCD time — every uncapped GCD on a Shadow Priest is a lost Shadowy Apparition, which compounds into significant damage. On a full-length kill, these CPM gaps would widen into a meaningful DPS deficit.

Mind Blast Gap (−17% CPM)

Mind Blast is the highest-priority rotational ability for Shadow Priest — it generates significant Insanity, triggers Shadowy Apparitions via the Archon hero talent tree, and deals heavy direct damage. At 8.0 CPM vs 9.7 CPM, Nurnyx is missing roughly 7 Mind Blast casts per 4 minutes of active time. Each missed Mind Blast cascades: less Insanity generated means fewer Shadow Word: Madness casts, fewer Shadowy Apparitions spawned, and less overall proc value. The reference treats Mind Blast as an absolute priority — it should be pressed on cooldown, never held unless Voidform is about to come up.

Mind Flay Filler Gap (−25% CPM)

Mind Flay at 5.0 CPM vs 6.6 CPM is the biggest percentage gap. In Shadow Priest's rotation, Mind Flay is the default filler when nothing else is available — it generates Insanity and deals damage. A low Mind Flay CPM typically indicates dead GCD time: moments where nothing is being cast. This is the primary driver of the overall 12.4% rotational CPM gap. During movement-heavy Crown mechanics (Grasp of Emptiness, Void Expulsion), instant-cast options like Shadow Word: Death and Shadow Word: Pain should be used instead of losing casts entirely.

Power Word: Shield Usage

Fabx casts Power Word: Shield 25 times (2.8 CPM) compared to Nurnyx's 1 cast. In current Shadow Priest gameplay, PW:S is off the GCD and provides a significant absorb shield on a very short cooldown. It costs no DPS to press and provides survivability. On a fight like Crown with constant ticking damage (Echoing Darkness, Void Expulsion), habitual PW:S usage directly contributes to survival — especially during Phase 2/3 mechanics that apply heavy sustained damage.

Actionable Items

1. Fill every GCD — close the 12.4% rotational CPM gap. Nurnyx's rotational CPM is 34.0 vs 38.8 for the reference — roughly 1 empty GCD every 12 seconds. Over a full kill (~9 minutes), that compounds to approximately 43 lost casts. The gap appears across multiple abilities (Mind Flay −25%, Mind Blast −17%, SW:Madness −15%) rather than being concentrated in one spell, which points to dead GCD time during movement. During Crown's movement mechanics (Grasp of Emptiness repositioning, Void Expulsion puddle placement, platform transitions), use instant-cast abilities: Shadow Word: Death, Shadow Word: Pain refreshes, Halo, and Angelic Feather. Mind Flay should be the default action whenever nothing else is available — if nothing is being cast, Mind Flay fills the gap. Estimated gain on a kill pull: +8-12K DPS.
2. Press Mind Blast on cooldown — it is the #1 priority. Mind Blast at 8.0 CPM vs 9.7 CPM means ~7 missed casts in 4 minutes of active time. Mind Blast is Shadow Priest's highest-priority rotational ability: it generates large Insanity (for more Shadow Word: Madness), triggers Shadowy Apparition spawns via the Archon talent tree, and hits hard (14.1% of total damage). Each missed Mind Blast cascades — less Insanity means fewer SW:Madness casts (already −15% vs reference), fewer Apparitions (33.5 CPM vs 37.3), and less Void Apparition value. The reference has a 2-charge Mind Blast from Thought Harvester talent (identical talent build), meaning charges should rarely cap. Estimated gain: +5-8K DPS.
3. Use Power Word: Shield habitually for free survivability. Fabx casts PW:S 25 times (2.8 CPM) vs Nurnyx's 1 cast. PW:S is off the GCD in Shadow's current build — it costs zero DPS to press. On Crown of the Cosmos with persistent Echoing Darkness ticks (118 hits, 2.4M damage) plus phase-specific burst, a regular PW:S absorb shield directly prevents damage that healers would otherwise need to cover. Nurnyx died at 4:14 to Void Remnants — an extra 100-150K absorb from a recent PW:S may have bought the extra second needed to survive. Build a habit of pressing PW:S every 15-20 seconds during non-channel moments.
4. Maintain Shadow Word: Pain uptime more aggressively on multi-target. SW:P at 1.9 CPM vs 3.1 CPM (−39%) is the largest percentage gap among rotational abilities. Crown of the Cosmos is a multi-target fight with Sentinels (Phase 1), Rift Simulacrum + adds (Phase 2), and Sentinels + Simulacrums (Phase 3). Shadow Word: Pain should be active on every target that will live long enough for the DoT to tick — each SW:P tick feeds Psychic Link damage (which was 4.0% for Nurnyx vs 15.5% for the reference). Tentacle Slam applies VT to 6 targets, but SW:P must be manually applied. On transition to new targets, lead with SW:P before starting your Mind Blast rotation.
5. On kill attempts: target Versatility in gear for Crown survival. Nurnyx has 27 Versatility vs Fabx's 160. While the DPS difference is marginal, the damage reduction from Versatility matters on a fight with heavy sustained damage. Crown's Phase 2 and Phase 3 involve overlapping damage sources (Void Remnants, Simulacrum Backlash, Echoing Darkness) where an extra 2-3% DR from Versatility can be the difference between living and dying. If any gear options provide a Vers upgrade without sacrificing Haste/Mastery, they are worth taking for prog survivability.
