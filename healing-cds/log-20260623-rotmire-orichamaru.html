Actionable Improvements

1 Maximize Hand of Gul'dan during Tyrant Windows
Average 5.6 HoG per Tyrant window vs reference's 7.3. Two windows were critically low: T4 at 3:16 (4 HoG, ref had 6) and T7 at 6:29 (3 HoG, ref had 8). Pool Soul Shards to 4-5 before Tyrant. During the 20-second Tyrant window, prioritize Demonbolt (instant, generates shards fast) into HoG dumps. The goal is 7+ HoG per Tyrant.
Impact: This single change addresses the Mind Sear gap (6.3x less damage) and is the largest DPS lever. More imps during Tyrant = exponentially more Mind Sear procs.
2 Consume Demonic Core Procs Faster
Demonbolt CPM is 10.6 vs reference's 11.9 (-11%), while Shadow Bolt is 9.2 vs 8.0 (+15%). This means Demonic Core stacks are sitting unclaimed while Shadow Bolt is being hard-cast. Demonbolt is instant and generates shards faster than Shadow Bolt. Always check for Demonic Core stacks before starting a Shadow Bolt cast. Consume Demonbolt procs at 2+ stacks, and always before Tyrant windows.
Impact: ~14 additional Demonbolt casts over the fight, converting ~14 Shadow Bolts. Each Demonbolt generates shards faster, enabling more HoG casts.
3 Use Potion of Recklessness (DPS Potion)
No DPS potion was used -- only a Silvermoon Health Potion. The reference player used Potion of Recklessness twice (pre-pot at -2s and second pot during a later Tyrant window). Pre-pot 2 seconds before the pull timer hits 0, and use the second charge during a Tyrant window in the second half of the fight.
Impact: Two pots during Tyrant windows = ~3-5% overall DPS increase for free.
4 Maintain Uptime During Festering Vines #3 (2:59)
The 2:59 Vines window had only 4 casts (ref had 11) with a 6.4-second dead zone. This was the worst single window in the fight. Pre-position before Vines to minimize movement. Use Demonic Circle placement before Vines if available, or use instant casts (Demonbolt with Demonic Core, Implosion) during forced movement. The cascade effect is significant: poor Vines #3 handling led directly to a weak Tyrant T4 at 3:16.
Impact: Recovering 7 casts in this window and a stronger T4 Tyrant could recover ~1-2% total DPS.
5 Align Tyrant with Mechanic Gaps
Orichamaru used Tyrant nearly on cooldown (64s intervals), which is good for total Tyrant count (8 vs ref's 7). However, some Tyrant windows overlapped with add spawns and Vines, causing forced movement mid-burst. The reference player sometimes holds Tyrant an extra 5-10 seconds to land it in a clean window. On this fight, Tyrant T4 (3:16) overlapped with Awaken Fungi at 3:20 -- a 4-second delay to 3:21 would have allowed a clean window.
Impact: Cleaner Tyrant windows = more casts during the burst = more Mind Sear procs. Quality over quantity.

Summary

The 31% DPS gap is execution-based, not gear or talent

At identical item level (291) and nearly identical talents (4 differences), the gap is driven by three factors:

1. Tyrant window efficiency (primary): 5.6 HoG per Tyrant vs 7.3, causing 6.3x less Mind Sear damage (the single largest delta).

2. Demonic Core management: More Shadow Bolts, fewer Demonbolts means slower shard generation and fewer HoG opportunities.

3. No DPS potion: Missing ~3-5% from two Potion of Recklessness uses.

The reference also received 50% more Ebon Might applications (15 vs 10), which accounts for ~3-5% of the gap.
