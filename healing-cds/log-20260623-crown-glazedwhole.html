Glazedwhole did not die a single time across 20 pulls. That is the single most important metric for a tank on prog, and it is perfect. Self-healing averaged 63.2K HPS (66% of damage taken), driven by Death Strike, Blood Shield absorbs, and San'layn leech. DTPS averaged 95.5K across all pulls, with a notable downward trend through the session as the team improved execution. The main growth areas are rotational throughput — total CPM of 56.0 vs the rank #3 reference at 68.2 means roughly 18% fewer buttons pressed per minute, translating to fewer Death Strikes (10.0 vs 15.1 CPM, a 34% deficit), fewer Heart Strikes (6.8 vs 11.2 CPM, 39% deficit), and fewer Death and Decay casts. These gaps reduce both DPS contribution and self-healing potential, though clearly not to the point of dying. Anti-Magic Shell usage is also low at 0.4 CPM vs 0.9 CPM from reference — this is free magic mitigation being left on the table. On a fight with heavy magic damage (Voidstalker Sting, Echoing Darkness, Void Expulsion), more AMS casts directly reduce healer burden.

Defensive Insights

Vampiric Blood is used consistently and well-timed 4.9 avg uses per fight across all phases. In P1 (highest DTPS phase at 129K), VB is pressed 2.8 times per fight on a 1.5-minute CD in a 2:08 phase — near maximum possible. This is the signature of a tank who knows the damage pattern.
Anti-Magic Shell drops off sharply after P1 AMS at 1.4/fight in P1 drops to 0.3/fight in P2 and 0.2/fight in P3. This is a 1-minute cooldown that absorbs magic damage and generates Runic Power. With Voidstalker Sting, Echoing Darkness, and Cosmic Radiation all being magic damage, AMS should be used on cooldown in P2 and P3.
Icebound Fortitude used mostly in P1, rarely in P2/P3 IBF is a 3-minute 30% DR. Using it early in P1 means it may not be available for the harder damage in late P2 or P3. Consider saving IBF for specific high-damage overlaps in P2/P3 as the team gets cleaner at P1.

Actionable Items

#1: Death Strike CPM — 10.0 vs 15.1 reference (34% deficit) Death Strike is the core Blood DK survival button. Every Death Strike heals for at least 25% of recent damage taken AND applies a Blood Shield absorb (Mastery). At 10.0 CPM, Glazedwhole is leaving 5 Death Strikes per minute on the table. More Heart Strikes (the primary Runic Power generator) will fund more Death Strikes. This is the single highest-leverage improvement: more DS = more healing + more absorbs + more DPS.
#2: Heart Strike CPM — 6.8 vs 11.2 reference (39% deficit) Heart Strike is the Rune-spending filler that generates Runic Power for Death Strike. At 6.8 CPM vs 11.2, roughly 4.4 Heart Strikes per minute are missing. Each Heart Strike generates 10 Runic Power, meaning ~44 RP per minute is being lost. That is nearly one Death Strike every 30 seconds that is not happening. This and #1 are linked — fixing Heart Strike automatically helps Death Strike.
#3: Anti-Magic Shell underuse — 0.4 CPM vs 0.9 reference (56% deficit) AMS is a ~1-minute cooldown that absorbs magic damage and generates Runic Power. Crown of the Cosmos is a magic-heavy fight (Voidstalker Sting, Echoing Darkness, Void Expulsion, Cosmic Radiation, Simulacrum Backlash). In fight 25, 3 AMS casts absorbed 1.25M damage. The reference used 8 in a comparable timeframe. More AMS usage directly reduces DTPS, generates RP (enabling more Death Strikes), and is especially impactful in P2/P3 where current usage drops to 0.3/fight and 0.2/fight.
#4: Total CPM gap — 56.0 vs 68.2 (18% fewer buttons pressed) The overall GCD utilization is 18% lower than the reference. Blood Boil is overcasted (+51%), suggesting GCDs are going to Blood Boil when Heart Strike or Death Strike should be pressed. Blood Boil is useful for maintaining Blood Plague and spending Boiling Point procs, but should not displace core rotational buttons. Prioritize: Death Strike > Heart Strike > Death and Decay > Blood Boil.
#5: Dancing Rune Weapon usage — 0.4 CPM vs 0.7 reference (43% deficit) DRW is both offensive (generates extra Runes/RP from duplicated casts) and defensive (20% parry). 3 casts in 6:44 vs 6 casts in 8:28. On a 2-minute cooldown in a ~7-minute fight, 3 uses is possible but 4 should be the target. Aligning DRW with Gift of the San'layn windows maximizes the San'layn synergy — every duplicated Vampiric Strike during the Gift window amplifies self-healing.
#6: Death and Decay uptime — 3.1 CPM vs 4.8 reference (35% deficit) Death and Decay buffs Heart Strike damage and triggers Crimson Scourge procs. Keeping DnD down consistently improves both DPS and resource generation. Also provides Sanguine Ground healing when talented. On a fight with frequent tank positioning requirements, some downtime is expected, but improving from 3.1 to 4+ CPM would be impactful.
Strength: Zero deaths in 20 pulls This is the most important metric for a tank on prog and it is perfect. The survivability foundation is excellent — improving rotational throughput will layer DPS and additional self-healing on top of an already solid defensive baseline.
