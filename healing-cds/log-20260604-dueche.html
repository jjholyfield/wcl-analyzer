How Holy Paladin Works

Holy Paladin healing revolves around Reclamation procs that fire off Holy Shock crits, Holy Shock on cooldown for holy power generation, and then spending that holy power on Light of Dawn for AoE healing or Eternal Flame for a single-target HoT. These are your core rotational abilities and they make up the backbone of your throughput.

Between those procs and cooldowns, you need to be casting filler heals. Flash of Light is your fast triage heal — it’s what you cast when someone is low and needs healing right now. Holy Light is slower but bigger, and it’s what you cast when raid damage is moderate and nobody is in immediate danger. The critical thing is that top Holy Paladins cast Flash of Light at 10–11 per minute. This is their primary filler and triage heal. If you’re not casting Flash of Light, there are long windows where damaged players simply aren’t getting healed.

Avenging Wrath is a 2-minute throughput cooldown — use it on cooldown every time it’s available, don’t save it for a “perfect” moment. Aura Mastery is a 3-minute raid-wide damage reduction that should be coordinated with other healers so your CDs are spread across damage events. Divine Toll is rotational holy power generation, not a healing CD.

How It’s Affecting Your Healing

Flash of Light at 1.8/m vs 10.9/m (17%) — this is the single biggest issue. Top Holy Paladins use Flash of Light as their primary triage heal, casting it almost 11 times per minute. Dueche is casting it fewer than twice per minute. Without FoL, there are long windows where damaged players simply aren’t getting healed between Holy Shock cooldowns. This single gap accounts for most of the 60K HPS difference between Dueche (156K) and the #1 (215K).
Holy Light at 1.8/m vs 5.6/m (32%) — both filler heals are severely undercast. Combined with the Flash of Light gap, Dueche is casting roughly 3.6 filler heals per minute where top players cast 16.5 per minute. That’s nearly 13 missing heals every 60 seconds — healing that simply doesn’t happen. The Reclamation and Holy Shock numbers are actually strong, but those procs alone can’t carry the throughput without filler heals filling every gap.
7 early deaths (23% of pulls) — dying this often removes a healer from the fight. Every time Dueche dies, the remaining 3–4 healers have to cover the healing gap for the rest of the pull. On progression where pulls already end in wipes, losing a healer early makes it harder for the raid to practice later phases. 3 of these deaths were to Divine Toll, which suggests a positioning or defensive timing issue around that mechanic.
0/30 pre-pots — free throughput left on the table every pull. Pre-potting costs nothing and gives a burst of HPS or DPS at the start of every pull. Whether it’s a healing pot or a DPS pot, it’s free output that adds up across 30 pulls.

What You Need to Change

1
Cast Flash of Light Way More — Target 8+/min
Between Holy Shock cooldowns and Reclamation procs, you should be filling every global with Flash of Light. If someone in the raid is below 80% health, FoL them. It’s fast, it’s efficient, and it’s what separates 156K HPS from 215K HPS. You should never have a global where you’re not pressing something — if nobody needs a heal right now, cast Holy Light instead. The goal is zero dead GCDs.
2
Cast Holy Light as a Backup Filler — Target 4+/min
When raid damage is low and nobody is in danger, Holy Light is your steady-state filler. It’s slower than Flash of Light but heals for more. Together with FoL, your filler casts should total 12+ per minute. Right now you’re at 3.6 combined. That means roughly 9 seconds out of every minute where you’re not casting a heal at all.
3
Pre-Pot Every Pull
Potion of Recklessness or Light’s Potential — whichever fits your role that pull. It’s free damage or healing at the start of every pull and it costs nothing except remembering to press it. 0/30 is the lowest on the team.
4
Use Defensives Before Dangerous Mechanics
3 deaths to Divine Toll means the same mechanic is killing you repeatedly. Hit Divine Protection before known damage windows — you’re using it at 0.7/m vs the #1’s 1.1/m, so there’s room to press it more often. Use Healthstone and Health Potion more aggressively too — they’re on separate cooldowns from your mana pot, so there’s no conflict. Going from 8/30 HS and 5/30 HP to 20+/30 on both would prevent several of these deaths.
