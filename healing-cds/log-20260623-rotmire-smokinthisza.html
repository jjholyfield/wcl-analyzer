Actionable Improvements

1. Fill Every GCD Between Mechanics with Steady Shot or Arcane Shot
Impact: ~24-30 additional casts, closing the 2.3 CPM filler gap
Arcane Shot CPM is 4.3 vs reference's 6.0, and Steady Shot is 4.1 vs 6.3. Combined, that is 3.9 fewer filler casts per minute. Over 8 minutes, that is roughly 31 missed GCDs. These are the bread-and-butter casts that keep the rotation moving between Aimed Shot charges. When nothing else is available, Steady Shot must fill every gap. The reference has zero 8+ second gaps; Smokinthisza has one. Fix: Treat any GCD where Aimed Shot and Rapid Fire are both on CD as a mandatory Steady Shot. If Precise Shots is active, use Arcane Shot first. Never let a GCD pass empty.
2. Reduce Dead Time During Movement (Vines and Pre-Bloom Positioning)
Impact: ~10-15 casts recoverable from movement windows
4 of 6 Festering Vines windows show CPS below reference. As a ranged class with several instant-cast abilities, MM Hunter should maintain high uptime during movement. Black Arrow (instant), Arcane Shot with Precise Shots (instant), and Explosive Shot (instant) are all usable while repositioning for Vines or Bloom. The quiet window at 2:51–2:59 drops to 0.38 CPS (half the reference's 0.75), and the 6:16–6:32 pre-Bloom #3 window is 0.38 vs 0.50. Fix: Pre-position for Vines so movement is minimal. Queue up instant casts during any forced movement. Plan Black Arrow and Arcane Shot usage to coincide with movement windows rather than using them during stationary periods.
3. Minimize Turtle Downtime at 6:28
Impact: 3-5 recoverable casts from tighter Turtle usage
The largest single gap (8.8s at 6:24–6:33) is partially caused by Aspect of the Turtle at 6:28. The gap starts 4 seconds before Turtle and ends 5 seconds after. Cast right up until Turtle activates, and resume casting the instant it drops. Also evaluate whether Turtle is necessary here — if a defensive (Survival of the Fittest) could replace it, that eliminates the forced attack lockout entirely. Fix: If Turtle is needed, cast an instant ability (Black Arrow) at 6:24 instead of stopping, activate Turtle, and have Volley/Aimed Shot queued for the instant it drops.
4. Use Multi-Shot to Maintain Trick Shots on Add Spawns
Impact: Quality improvement on add cleave efficiency
The reference uses Multi-Shot 4 times during the fight to activate Trick Shots before Aimed Shot on multi-target windows. Smokinthisza uses zero Multi-Shots, relying entirely on Volley for Trick Shots activation. While Volley does activate Trick Shots, Multi-Shot is a faster, on-demand activation. During add spawns where Volley is on cooldown, a Multi-Shot before Aimed Shot ensures cleave damage hits all targets. Fix: When adds spawn and Volley is unavailable, use one Multi-Shot to activate Trick Shots before casting Aimed Shot into the add pack.
5. Sustain Filler Rotation Consistency in Mid-Fight (2:00–4:00)
Impact: Addresses the worst quiet window cluster
The 2:16–2:59 range has the worst quiet window performance (0.38–0.47 CPS vs 0.60–0.75 reference). This is the period after Bloom #1 / Trueshot #1 expires and before the next set of add spawns. It is the classic post-burst-window lull where focus drops. The reference maintains 0.60+ CPS through this zone. Fix: After Trueshot expires, immediately shift mental focus to keeping GCDs rolling. The rotation outside of Trueshot is simpler: Aimed Shot on CD, Rapid Fire on CD, Black Arrow procs, Steady Shot as filler. Internalize the non-Trueshot priority so there is zero hesitation between casts.

The 13.6K raw DPS gap (174.9K vs 188.5K) becomes an estimated 28-38K effective gap when accounting for the ~10-15% external buff advantage.

  • Trueshot + PI alignment — every cooldown stacked perfectly with Fungal Bloom windows
  • Add switching — faster first hits than reference, more total add damage
