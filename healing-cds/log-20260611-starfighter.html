Prog Assessment

Starfighter is running Slayer Arms on Mythic Crown of the Cosmos progression. Across 26 pulls, the raid reached 0.01% boss HP on two attempts (F8, F17) but never closed the kill. On the longest pull (Fight 23, 4:52), Starfighter dealt 132.6K DPS before dying at 4:01 to Void Remnants during what appears to be the P2 transition. Compared to the #2 ranked Arms Warrior kill (90.8K DPS over 8:14), Starfighter's CPM-adjusted damage output is reasonable for prog — the reference has a full kill's worth of execute phase and consistent uptime that a wipe pull cannot match. The key differences: Mortal Strike CPM is 15% below reference (12.6 vs 14.9), Execute usage is 42% lower (5.7 vs 9.8 CPM — expected since wipes cut short the sub-35% execute window), Rend uptime is severely low (0.6 vs 3.0 CPM), and Slam is overcasted (+39%) at the expense of higher-priority abilities. Cooldown alignment is clean — all 3 Avatar uses synced with Colossus Smash and Bladestorm. The rotational CPM gap (44.5 vs 52.6) accounts for roughly 8 missed GCDs per minute, partially explained by intermission downtime and early death.

Rend Uptime Is Nearly Zero

Starfighter cast Rend only 2 times across 4 minutes of combat vs the reference's 22 casts (3.0 CPM). Rend is a high-priority DoT in the Arms rotation — it should be maintained on all active targets at all times. With the Bloodletting talent, Rend auto-applies during execute phase, but outside execute, it must be manually refreshed every 15 seconds. Rend contributed 3.2% of reference damage (2.79M over the kill). Over 190s of active combat, Starfighter should have cast Rend approximately 6 times at minimum (initial application + refreshes). The 2-cast count suggests Rend is simply not in the priority rotation, which is a significant damage loss.

Slam Over-Use Displacing Higher-Priority Abilities

Slam at 6.6 CPM (21 casts) vs the reference's 4.8 CPM (35 casts) means Slam is being pressed 39% more often per minute. Slam is the lowest-priority filler in the Arms rotation — it should only be used when Mortal Strike, Overpower, Execute (Sudden Death), and Rend are all unavailable. The overcasting suggests GCDs are going to Slam when Rend refreshes or Mortal Strike casts should be happening instead. Each Slam that replaces a Mortal Strike costs roughly 150-200K damage per GCD.

Execute Delta: Prog Context Matters

The −42% Execute CPM gap (5.7 vs 9.8) is largely expected. The reference killed the boss and had a full execute phase (sub-35% HP for several minutes). Starfighter's wipe at 77.31% boss HP means most Executes were Sudden Death procs only, not sustained sub-35% spam. The 18 Execute casts from 15 Sudden Death procs are actually reasonable usage — Starfighter is consuming procs when they appear. This gap will naturally close when the boss reaches execute phase on longer pulls.

Spell Reflection Underuse

The reference used Spell Reflection 9 times across 8:14 vs Starfighter's 1 use in 4:01. On Crown of the Cosmos, Echoing Darkness deals constant magic damage ticks (118 hits on Starfighter totaling 3M damage). Each Spell Reflection window can reflect multiple ticks for free damage with no GCD cost. At roughly one use per minute, Starfighter should have gotten 3-4 uses in 4 minutes. Spell Reflection also provides a brief 20% magic DR, which helps survivability on a fight with heavy magic damage. This is a free button that provides both DPS and survival.

Actionable Items

1. Maintain Rend at all times outside execute phase. Starfighter cast Rend only 2 times in 4 minutes of combat vs the reference's 22 casts (3.0 CPM). Rend is a high-priority DoT that should be refreshed before expiration (15s duration, refresh at <5s remaining). With Bloodletting, it auto-applies during execute phase, but in P1/P2 phases (which is all prog pulls right now), it must be manually maintained. Rend contributed 3.2% of reference total damage — extrapolated to Starfighter's 190s of active time, that's roughly 1.3M missing damage. Add it to the priority: after Mortal Strike, before Slam.
2. Reduce Slam usage — fill with higher-priority abilities. Slam at 6.6 CPM is 39% above the reference's 4.8 CPM. Slam is the lowest-priority filler in the Arms rotation. Each Slam GCD that could have been a Mortal Strike loses ~150K damage; each one that could have been an Overpower loses ~80K. The rotation priority above Slam: Mortal Strike > Execute (Sudden Death) > Overpower > Rend refresh > Heroic Strike > Slam. If Slam is coming up frequently, Mortal Strike or Overpower are likely being delayed.
3. Use Spell Reflection on cooldown for free damage and DR. The reference used it 9 times in 8:14 (~1.1 CPM). Starfighter used it once. On Crown of the Cosmos, Echoing Darkness deals constant magic ticks (Starfighter took 3M from 118 hits). Each Spell Reflection reflects damage back and provides a brief magic damage reduction. This is off-GCD and free — no rotation disruption. Aim for 1 use per minute. Over 4 minutes, that's 3-4 missed uses worth of free damage and survivability. This could have helped survive the Void Remnants that killed Starfighter at 4:01.
4. Minimize casting gaps during P1 movement mechanics. Starfighter had ~37 seconds of dead time in 190 seconds of active combat. The largest gaps (15.9s at 1:21, 8.2s at 0:54) correspond to P1 mechanics. Crown P1 has Grasp of Emptiness beams and Void Expulsion puddles that force melee movement. Use Charge (already at 6 casts) and Heroic Leap (only 1 cast — should be more) to return to melee range faster. Wrecking Throw and Heroic Throw can fill ranged GCDs during forced movement. Each recovered second is roughly 1 additional GCD.
5. Consider Storm Bolt over Impending Victory. The 3 talent differences include Storm Bolt (reference) vs Impending Victory (Starfighter). Storm Bolt is a 30s CD stun that deals damage and fires inside every Avatar window. The reference used it 8 times, always synced with burst. Impending Victory is a self-heal GCD — it costs a global that could go to damage. If survivability is covered by healers and Spell Reflection usage improves, Storm Bolt adds more DPS. However, if the self-heal is keeping Starfighter alive through mechanics, keep Impending Victory until the raid's healing stabilizes.

Summary

This is progression data, not a kill — raw DPS comparison against ranked kills is misleading. The meaningful gaps are rotational: Rend uptime is near-zero (biggest single fix), Slam overcasting displaces higher-priority abilities, and Spell Reflection is essentially unused. Cooldown alignment is already excellent. The CPM gap (44.5 vs 52.6) is partially intermission/downtime and partially the casting gaps during P1 movement. Fixing Rend uptime alone would add an estimated 1-2M damage per pull. Reducing Slam in favor of Mortal Strike adds another 1-2M. Adding consistent Spell Reflection usage provides free damage plus survivability that may prevent the 4:01 death. Combined, these changes could add 15-25K DPS on a full pull — meaningful on a boss the team has already brought to 0.01%.
