Zero Smite Problem

Smite is the primary Atonement filler — the spell you cast between cooldowns to maintain steady Atonement healing. Without it, there is no sustained throughput between ramp windows. Espéon casts Smite 77 times (11.2/min) — it accounts for more casts than any other ability. Tiareynna cast it zero times. This single missing button explains a massive portion of the healing gap.

Over-Casting Pattern

Where Smite should go, Tiareynna is filling with SW:Pain (6.4/min vs 2.0), PW:Shield (5.1/min vs 2.0), and Plea (4.8/min vs 1.7). These are all single-target, low-throughput abilities. SW:Pain is a maintenance DoT (apply once, refresh occasionally), not a filler. PW:Shield and Plea apply one Atonement each — Smite instead would deal damage and heal through all active Atonements simultaneously.

Actionable Items

1. Press Smite. This is the #1 priority. Smite is the bread-and-butter Atonement filler — every global not spent on a cooldown or ramp ability should be a Smite. It deals damage, which heals through every active Atonement simultaneously. The reference casts 77 Smites in 6:54 (11.2/min). Zero Smites means zero sustained Atonement healing between ramp windows. Where Smite should go, Tiareynna is instead casting SW:Pain (6.4/min), PW:Shield (5.1/min), and Plea (4.8/min) — all single-target, low-throughput filler.
2. Pre-ramp for Bloom. Blooms fire on a fixed 136-second timer. The ramp sequence (3× PW:Radiance → Evangelism) must start 5-6 seconds before the Bloom, not after. With Blooms at 2:00, 4:16, and 6:32, start the first PW:Radiance at 1:55, 4:11, and 6:27 respectively. Currently ramping 5.6s late means the healing window is completely missed.
3. Use Shadow Word: Death as Atonement damage. SW:Death deals massive instant damage, translating to huge Atonement healing. Reference uses it 3.6/min — on cooldown, any time a target is below 20% or using the execute talent. Tiareynna used it 0.6/min (2 total casts). During ramp windows, SW:Death should be queued right after Evangelism alongside Mind Blast and Penance.
4. Reduce SW:Pain over-casting. Shadow Word: Pain is a maintenance DoT — apply it once and it refreshes on its own through Void Bolt / other interactions. At 6.4/min vs the reference's 2.0/min, Tiareynna is re-applying SW:Pain 3× as often as necessary, wasting globals that should be Smite casts.
5. Position to avoid Festering Vines. Died at 3:08 to a root mechanic. Six Angelic Feather casts (vs reference's 1) suggest repeated positioning scrambles throughout the fight. Feather grants speed — it does not remove roots. The key is not getting targeted in the first place through better positioning relative to the vine spawn locations.
