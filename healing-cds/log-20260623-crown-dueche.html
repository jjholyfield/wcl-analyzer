Actionable Items

1. Survive P2 Mechanics — Cosmic Barrier and Void Remnants

9 of 19 deaths occurred in Phase 2, with Cosmic Barrier (3 kills) and Void Remnants (3 kills) as the primary causes. Cosmic Barrier is persistent ticking damage that requires active mitigation — Divine Protection should be used on CD during P2 when Cosmic Barrier is active. Void Remnants are ground zones that must be dodged immediately. Map out a P2 personal CD plan: Divine Protection for the first Cosmic Barrier window, Divine Shield as emergency when Cosmic Barrier overlaps with Rift Slash or Voidstalker Sting. On the best pull (F13, only survival), both AW and AM were used at 52s and 270s — the P2 CD usage on that pull may hold the key.

2. Establish Consistent Aura Mastery Timing

Aura Mastery was only used in 11/20 pulls with a timing stddev of 98.1 seconds. Compare to Avenging Wrath at 1.3s stddev — the opening AW is locked in, but AM has no established pattern. This is Holy Paladin's most impactful raid CD. Work with the healing team to assign AM to a specific boss ability window: either the first Grasp of Emptiness (around 0:25) or the first Null Corona (around 0:20) in P1, and then again for a P2 mechanic overlap. A 3-minute CD used predictably at the same boss event every pull gives the raid more reliable damage reduction than a reactive AM that sometimes never gets pressed.

3. Reduce P1 Deaths — Clean Mechanics Before Healing Pressure

6 deaths in P1 where healing demand is lowest (136.8K HPS, 37.8% overheal). Silverstrike Arrow killed Dueche twice — this is a targeted ability requiring pre-positioning before it fires. Echoing Darkness (Vorelus) is ground damage that must be dodged. One Melee death at 2:02 suggests being too close to an enemy. P1 is the "free" phase with low pressure — dying here means the team loses a healer for the hard parts. Focus on personal positioning during Vorelus and Morium mechanics, and be at range from adds.

4. Plan Team CD Coverage for Intermission and Early P2

On Fight 25 (longest pull), the Intermission (Stellar Emission, 2:14-2:53) had zero healer CDs deployed. Early P2 (2:53-4:20) also had no healer CDs until Brewtote's SLT at 4:23. The team survived, but if intermission damage increases or early P2 gets harder, there is no CD safety net for 2+ minutes. Coordinate with the healing team to place at least one healer CD in early P2 (Dueche's second AW is naturally available around 2:08-2:30, align with P2 start).

5. Core Rotation is Solid

Holy Shock at 16.1 CPM, Light of Dawn at 8.8 CPM, Divine Toll at 1.4 CPM — the core Holy Paladin rotation is well-executed on the longest pull. 24.5% overheal on Fight 25 is excellent for prog. The Herald of the Sun build is performing well with Dawnlight, Sun Sear, and Litany of Light contributing meaningful passive healing. The throughput fundamentals are not the issue — survival is the bottleneck. When Dueche stays alive (F13), the output is there to support the team.
