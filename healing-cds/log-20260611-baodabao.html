Bottom Line — Progression Context

Baodabao dealt 175.7K DPS on the longest pull (Fight 23, 4:52) across 26 wipes on Mythic Crown of the Cosmos — this is progression data, not a kill. The #1 ranked Frost Mage Asimage dealt 108.2K DPS on a 9:05 kill with only 2 talent differences. Direct DPS comparison is misleading here: Baodabao's pull covered only the first ~5 minutes (through early Phase 2), while Asimage's kill includes all three phases over 9+ minutes. Longer fights naturally have lower DPS as burst windows thin out. The key finding is rotational CPM: 36.9 active CPM vs Asimage's 43.4 — a 15% gap in how many GCDs are being used per minute of active combat. The biggest contributors are Flurry at 3.5 CPM vs 7.6 CPM (−54%) and Ice Lance at 17.4 vs 20.7 CPM (−16%). These are the core of the Frost Mage Shatter combo — Flurry applies Freezing stacks and Brain Freeze procs, Ice Lance consumes them for Shatter damage. With only 17 Flurries vs 69 (adjusted: 3.5 vs 7.6 CPM), Baodabao is losing massive Shatter and Frost Splinter throughput.

Critical: Flurry Usage −49%

Flurry is the #1 gap. At 4.2 CPM vs 8.3 CPM, Baodabao is casting Flurry at roughly half the rate of the reference. In the current Frost Mage rotation (Spellslinger hero talent), Flurry is cast on every Brain Freeze proc and also has baseline charges available without Brain Freeze. With 17 Flurries vs 69 (CPM-adjusted), Baodabao is generating far fewer Shatter windows and Freezing stacks, directly reducing both Ice Lance and Frost Splinter damage. This single gap cascades through the entire rotation.

Glacial Spike −38%

Glacial Spike at 1.5 CPM vs 2.4 CPM means fewer Icicle dump windows. Glacial Spike triggers Fingers of Frost (via Flash Freeze talent) and generates Frost Splinters, making it both a direct damage ability and a proc enabler. The lower Glacial Spike rate is partially caused by the lower Flurry/Frostbolt throughput — fewer Icicle-generating casts means fewer Glacial Spikes become available.

Actionable Items

1. Double Flurry usage — cast on every Brain Freeze AND on available charges. This is the single biggest improvement available. At 4.2 CPM vs 8.3 CPM, Baodabao is casting Flurry at half the reference rate. In the current Spellslinger Frost Mage rotation, Flurry should be cast whenever Brain Freeze procs (priority #2 after Frozen Orb), and also whenever a charge is available without Brain Freeze (lower priority but still above filler). Each Flurry applies Thermal Void (extending Icy Veins), generates Freezing stacks on the target, and does meaningful direct damage (5.3% of Baodabao's damage vs 9.7% for the reference). More Flurries means more Shatter-empowered Ice Lances, more Frost Splinters, and more Glacial Spikes becoming available. Estimated gain: +15-20% overall throughput — this is the rotation's core engine.
2. Cast Frozen Orb on cooldown — treat it as priority #1. Frozen Orb has a ~30s cooldown but Baodabao averaged 40.5s between casts vs Asimage's 31.8s. The first Orb was delayed to 7.5s (Asimage casts it at 0.1s pre-pull), and the second didn't come until 52.2s (a 44.8s gap). Frozen Orb generates Brain Freeze procs (via Wintertide talent), Fingers of Frost procs, and deals sustained AoE damage. Every delayed Frozen Orb cascades into fewer Brain Freeze procs, fewer Flurries, fewer Shatter windows, and fewer Glacial Spikes. On pull: cast Frozen Orb at 0s before anything else. During combat: as soon as Frozen Orb is off cooldown, it should be the very next button pressed. Estimated gain: ~1-2 extra Frozen Orbs per 5 minutes = 4-6 additional Brain Freeze procs.
3. Fill Frostbolt cast windows with instant casts when procs are available. Many of the 2.7s gaps between instant casts correspond to Frostbolt casts. This is correct when no procs are available (Frostbolt is the filler). But with 24 Fingers of Frost procs and 15 Brain Freeze procs recorded, some of these gaps likely had procs available that were being held instead of immediately consumed. The Spellslinger rotation priority is: Frozen Orb > Flurry (Brain Freeze) > Ice Lance (Fingers of Frost or 6+ Freezing stacks) > Glacial Spike > Frostbolt. Procs should be used immediately, not banked.
4. Use Alter Time and Greater Invisibility for survival. Asimage used Alter Time twice and Greater Invisibility twice over a 9-minute kill. Baodabao used neither over 4:52. Alter Time is a 10s position reset that doubles as a damage immunity when you snap back — critical for Dimensional Slash positioning and platform transitions. Greater Invisibility provides 60% damage reduction for 3 seconds — use it during high damage windows like Simulacrum Backlash pulses. These defensives extend survival during progression, giving more learning time per pull.
5. Align 2nd Potion with Heroism in Phase 2. Baodabao used Potion of Recklessness at 0:02 (pull) but did not use a second potion when Heroism was cast at 2:59. Asimage used a second potion at 2:53 to stack with Time Warp. For future pulls that consistently reach Phase 2, saving the first potion for Heroism timing (or using one at pull AND one at Heroism if two are available) would amplify the strongest burst window of the fight.
What's Already Good

Survivability: Baodabao survived until the final wipe call at 4:24 while the raid collapsed from 20 alive to 7 in the preceding 40 seconds. Dying to the final Bursting Emptiness with the rest of the remaining raid is not a personal error.

Frostbolt CPM: At 6.7 CPM, Frostbolt usage exactly matches the reference (6.7 CPM). The filler spell is being cast at the right rate — the issue is above it in the priority (Flurry and Frozen Orb).

Ray of Frost: At 1.7 CPM vs 1.6 CPM, Ray of Frost usage is slightly above the reference. This is a ~60s cooldown that converts to Comet Storm, and it's being used on schedule.

Interrupts: 4 Counterspells vs Asimage's 1. Active interrupt participation during progression is valuable for the raid team.

Damage taken profile: Mostly unavoidable damage. No excessive avoidable damage instances. The 377K from Falling during intermission is the only truly avoidable source — use Slow Fall for the platform transition.

Damage distribution: Shatter at 38.1% of total damage (vs 37.0% for reference) confirms the fundamental Shatter gameplay loop is working — Ice Lance into Freezing targets is happening at the right proportion when casts are made. The gap is purely in how many total casts are generated per minute.

Summary

The 15% active CPM gap (36.9 vs 43.4) is the single finding that matters. It breaks down to: ~60% Flurry under-use (4.2 vs 8.3 CPM — fewer Brain Freeze consumptions and fewer charge uses driving fewer Shatter windows and Freezing stacks), ~25% Frozen Orb delays (40.5s avg interval vs 31.8s — fewer Brain Freeze procs generated), and ~15% Glacial Spike cascade (fewer Icicles building from fewer Flurries/Frostbolts means fewer Glacial Spike casts). The good news: this is entirely execution-based. Gear is nearly identical (289.1 vs 289.8 ilvl), talents are 2 diffs apart, and stats are closely matched. Fixing items #1 and #2 (Flurry priority and Frozen Orb on cooldown) would likely close the majority of the CPM gap and bring sustained DPS in line with reference kills. This is a spec where the rotation is mechanically simple (most casts are instant), so the fix is primarily about priority discipline — Brain Freeze procs and Frozen Orb cooldown tracking.
