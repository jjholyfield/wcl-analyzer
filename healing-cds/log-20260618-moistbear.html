Actionable Improvements

  • #1: Survive Phase 2 — Bursting Emptiness Awareness

    The problem: Bursting Emptiness killed Moistbear 4 times (F18, F22, F23, F28). This AoE fires when a Rift Simulacrum dies. Moistbear is consistently too close to the dying Simulacrum. Combined with chip damage from Voidstalker Sting and Simulacrum Backlash, health is too low to absorb the burst.

    The fix: When Rift Simulacrums are low health, move to max melee range or briefly disengage. Use Barkskin or Bear Form before the Simulacrum dies. Prioritize positioning over the last few GCDs of damage on the add.

    Impact: Surviving 30-60 more seconds per pull = ~1-2M more damage dealt per fight. Fixing this alone could convert 3-4 sub-20K pulls into 30K+ pulls.

  • #2: Survive Phase 2 — Simulacrum Backlash Stacking

    The problem: Simulacrum Backlash killed Moistbear 3 times (F16, F20, F24). The death sequences show repeated 33K Backlash ticks stacking with Voidstalker Sting ticks, slowly grinding health to zero. This is a positioning issue -- Backlash damage increases with proximity or number of Simulacrums being cleaved.

    The fix: Ensure proper spread from other players during Rift Simulacrum phases. Avoid standing between multiple Simulacrums. Use Survival Instincts (if talented) or Barkskin during high-stack periods. Consider using Regrowth during Cat Form lulls if health is critical.

    Impact: Eliminating these deaths extends P2 uptime by 30-90 seconds. The difference between dying at 4:26 (F24, 25.7K DPS) and dying at 5:42 (F23, 38.3K DPS) is 49% more DPS.

  • #3: Use Survival Instincts and Frenzied Regeneration

    The problem: Neither Survival Instincts (50% DR, 3 min CD) nor Frenzied Regeneration (heal over time) appeared in the Fight 23 cast log. These are two of Feral Druid's strongest defensive tools. Barkskin (20% DR, 1 min CD) was used 4 times, but the bigger DR cooldown was seemingly not used at all.

    The fix: If Survival Instincts is talented, use it during the first P2 Bursting Emptiness window and again in early P3. If it is not talented, strongly consider picking it up -- 50% DR for 6 seconds can be the difference between living and dying through Bursting Emptiness. Frenzied Regeneration can be used in Bear Form during movement windows to recover health.

    Impact: One Survival Instincts usage during a Bursting Emptiness window would reduce 265K damage to ~133K -- easily survivable. This is the single highest-impact talent/usage change available.

  • #4: Void Remnants and Grasp of Emptiness — Dodge Mechanics

    The problem: Void Remnants killed Moistbear twice (F15, F21) and Grasp of Emptiness once (F19). These are avoidable ground effects in P2. The Void Remnants hits show 192-210K damage per hit, which is lethal when health is already low from ambient damage.

    The fix: Watch for Void Remnants swirls and move immediately. For Grasp of Emptiness, move out of the void zone before the second tick. Wild Charge (available, used 4 times in F23) is an excellent mobility tool for dodging these -- pre-plan an escape target.

    Impact: Avoiding these deaths extends 3 pulls by 30-90 seconds each, adding significant damage contribution.

  • #5: Tiger's Fury Frequency

    The problem: Tiger's Fury was cast 9 times in 342 seconds (~38s average gap). The ability has a 30-second cooldown. This means ~26 seconds of Tiger's Fury uptime was lost across the fight.

    The fix: Tiger's Fury should be pressed on cooldown (every 30 seconds) unless you are sitting at near-full energy. The 15% damage buff and 50-energy refund are too valuable to delay. The gap from 1:41 to 2:57 covers Intermission (unavoidable), but earlier P1 gaps between casts (35s, 31s, 35s) suggest slightly late usage.

    Impact: ~8-10% more time under Tiger's Fury buff = ~1-1.5% overall DPS increase. Small compared to survival gains but worth cleaning up.

Summary

Verdict: Survival is the only priority

Moistbear's Feral Druid rotation and cooldown management are fundamentally sound. P1 execution is clean. Berserk/TF/Convoke alignment is correct. Bleed uptime is maintained. The 28.5K average DPS is entirely explained by dying in every pull. The 59% DPS drop from P1 to P2 is not a rotation problem -- it is a survival problem. Every death is in P2 or early P3, and the kills come from a combination of: (1) Bursting Emptiness proximity damage, (2) Simulacrum Backlash stacking, and (3) standing in avoidable ground effects while already at low HP.

The single highest-impact improvement is using Survival Instincts before known burst damage in P2, followed by better positioning around dying Rift Simulacrums. If Moistbear can survive to the end of the fight on even half of the pulls, average DPS should jump from 28K to 35K+ immediately, with no rotation changes needed.
