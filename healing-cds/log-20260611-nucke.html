Bottom Line

Nucke is progressing Crown of the Cosmos with 26 wipe pulls, reaching 0.01% boss HP twice (pulls 8 and 17) but no kill yet. Analyzing the longest pull (fight 23, 4:52) against the #1 ranked Vengeance DH Melvabeam (53.5K DPS, 8:25 kill), the biggest concerns are rotational throughput: Nucke's total CPM is 96.0 vs 130.0 — a 26% deficit meaning roughly one in four GCDs is being missed or delayed. Core spender Soul Cleave is at 7.4 CPM vs 13.5 CPM (−45%), and Demon Spikes active mitigation is at 47.7% uptime which leaves large unmitigated windows. Metamorphosis usage is critically low at 3 casts vs 16 (−68% CPM), which is expected since the Annihilator hero talent tree generates additional Metamorphosis procs through Untethered Rage — but those procs only trigger when Soul Cleave and Spirit Bomb are being used at full rate, which they are not. DPS of 53.3K on a 4:52 wipe is reasonable for prog context, but the rotation efficiency gap suggests 15-25% more throughput is available through tighter GCD usage alone. Self-healing HPS of 37.1K matches the reference's 36.2K, which is a strong baseline. This is prog data compared to a kill — survival pressure is different — but the fundamental GCD and ability priority gaps will carry into the kill.

Core Spender Deficit: Soul Cleave −45%

Soul Cleave is the primary Fury spender for Vengeance DH. At 7.4 CPM vs 13.5 CPM, Nucke is pressing Soul Cleave barely half as often as the reference. Each Soul Cleave consumes Soul Fragments for healing and damage, and with the Annihilator tree, consuming fragments has an increasing chance to proc Untethered Rage which grants a bonus Metamorphosis window. Fewer Soul Cleaves = fewer fragment consumes = fewer Meta procs = fewer Voidfall Meteors. This single deficit cascades through the entire Annihilator damage loop.

Throw Glaive Over-Use: +720% CPM

Throw Glaive at 4.1 CPM (20 casts) vs the reference's 0.5 CPM (4 casts) is being used as a frequent filler. Throw Glaive is the lowest-priority ability in the Vengeance rotation — it should only be pressed when nothing else is available and the target is out of melee range. These 16 extra Throw Glaive casts are GCDs that should be Fracture, Soul Cleave, or Immolation Aura instead. Each displaced GCD loses both damage and Soul Fragment generation.

Where the Annihilator Loop Breaks Down

The Annihilator hero tree creates a damage loop: Fracture builds Soul Fragments → Soul Cleave/Spirit Bomb consume them → Voidfall stacks build → Untethered Rage triggers bonus Metamorphosis → Metamorphosis drops Voidfall Meteors + Meteor Shower + Catastrophe. Nucke's loop breaks at step 2 — Soul Cleave is pressed at half the reference rate (−45%). This means Voidfall Meteors are only 36% of reference damage (3.1M vs 8.7M), Meteor Shower is 29% of reference (1.2M vs 4.0M), and Catastrophe is 37% of reference (1.4M vs 3.8M). These three abilities alone account for 10.9M of the 26M damage gap.

Actionable Items

1. Increase Soul Cleave usage — the entire Annihilator loop depends on it. Soul Cleave is at 7.4 CPM vs 13.5 CPM (−45%). This is the single most impactful change. Soul Cleave consumes Soul Fragments, which triggers Voidfall stacks and has an increasing chance to proc Untethered Rage (bonus Metamorphosis). Melvabeam had 16 Metamorphosis activations to Nucke's 3 — that gap is almost entirely driven by Soul Cleave frequency. The Annihilator damage loop is: Fracture → Soul Cleave (consume fragments) → Voidfall stacks → Metamorphosis proc → Voidfall Meteors/Meteor Shower/Catastrophe. More Soul Cleaves = more Meta procs = more damage AND more survivability (Meta provides max HP increase and damage reduction). Target: 12+ Soul Cleave CPM.
2. Reduce GCD gaps — 96 CPM vs 130 CPM is a 26% deficit. Across 4:52, Nucke pressed 469 buttons vs the reference's projected equivalent of ~634 buttons at the same fight length. That is ~165 missing GCDs — roughly 2.5 minutes of dead time across a 4:52 fight. This could be from movement, hesitation, or downtime between phases. Every empty GCD is a missed Fracture (Soul Fragment), Soul Cleave (heal + damage + proc chance), or Immolation Aura (passive damage + fragments). On prog, when unsure what to do, the answer is always Fracture → Soul Cleave. Fill every GCD.
3. Maintain higher Demon Spikes uptime — 47.7% is dangerously low. Demon Spikes is the primary active mitigation and should have 70%+ uptime on prog. The Feed the Demon talent refunds Demon Spikes charge cooldown every time a Soul Fragment is consumed — so higher Soul Cleave usage (item 1) will naturally increase Demon Spikes availability. Three significant gaps were identified: 25 seconds (1:11–1:36), 45 seconds (2:15–3:00), and 29 seconds (3:28–3:57) with no Demon Spikes or major defensive active. Boss melee hits during these windows landed for 250–356K unmitigated. With Demon Spikes active, those melees would be significantly parried or reduced.
4. Stop using Throw Glaive as a filler — 20 casts vs reference's 4. Throw Glaive is the absolute lowest priority ability in the Vengeance rotation. At 4.1 CPM (20 casts), it is being pressed more than Immolation Aura (2.7 CPM) and almost as much as Demon Spikes (3.5 CPM). Each Throw Glaive displaces a Fracture or Soul Cleave cast that would generate and consume Soul Fragments, feed the Annihilator loop, and contribute to self-healing. Throw Glaive should only be pressed when the boss is completely out of melee range and nothing else is available. Target: under 1.0 CPM.
5. Use Immolation Aura and Sigil of Flame on cooldown. Immolation Aura at 2.7 CPM vs 4.2 CPM (−36%) and Sigil of Flame at 1.0 CPM vs 1.7 CPM (−41%) are both being delayed. Immolation Aura generates Soul Fragments passively (via the Fallout talent) and deals damage — it is both a builder and a DoT. Sigil of Flame is free damage on the ground. Both should be pressed essentially on cooldown. These are low-effort gains: they do not compete with other GCDs because they are off-GCD or have independent cooldowns.
6. Align Fel Devastation with Fiery Brand for extension. Nucke does use Fel Devastation during Fiery Brand windows (confirmed in timeline), which is correct. Continue this pattern. Fiery Brand reduces all damage taken by 40% — extending it with Fel Devastation and Soul Carver maximizes the mitigation window. With better overall CPM, Fiery Brand's uptime will naturally improve from the current 30.0% toward the reference's level.
What's Already Good

Self-Healing HPS: 37.1K matches the #1 reference's 36.2K. Even with fewer spender casts, Nucke's self-sustain is competitive. Soul Barrier is being used proactively (1.7M absorbed, 0% wasted), and Consume Soul is generating substantial healing (3.7M).

Fiery Brand + Fel Devastation Synergy: When Nucke does use Fiery Brand, the follow-up with Fel Devastation for extension is consistently applied. This is the correct priority for maximizing the 40% damage reduction window.

Tank Swap Pattern: 9 taunts from Nucke, 8 from Glazedwhole — clean, even split. No extended periods where one tank held the boss too long. The swap rhythm is healthy.

Near-Kills Exist: The raid reached 0.01% boss HP twice (pulls 8 and 17) and 0.22% on pull 21. The damage is there raid-wide. The kill will come from surviving longer, not from raw DPS increases.

Prog Context: This is wipe data compared against kill references. On prog, tanks take more damage from unfamiliar mechanics, spend more GCDs repositioning, and die to learning. The 53K DPS on a 4:52 wipe is not directly comparable to 53K DPS on an 8:25 kill. The CPM and rotation priority gaps, however, are persistent issues that carry into any fight length.

Summary

The primary gap is rotational throughput. At 96 CPM vs 130 CPM, roughly one in four GCDs is empty. This cascades through the Annihilator talent tree: fewer Soul Cleaves (−45%) → fewer Voidfall/Untethered Rage procs → fewer Metamorphosis windows (3 vs 16) → less damage AND less survivability. The fix is straightforward: press more buttons. Soul Cleave whenever at 3+ Soul Fragments, Fracture to build fragments, Immolation Aura and Sigil of Flame on cooldown, Demon Spikes whenever available. Stop filling GCDs with Throw Glaive (20 casts → target 4 or fewer). On the defensive side, the self-healing is already strong (37K HPS) and tank swaps are clean — but Demon Spikes uptime at 47.7% leaves large unmitigated windows. Fixing the Soul Cleave rate will simultaneously fix Demon Spikes uptime (via Feed the Demon), Metamorphosis uptime (via Untethered Rage), and overall DPS. It is one change with three benefits.
