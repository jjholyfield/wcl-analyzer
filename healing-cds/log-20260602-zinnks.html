How Augmentation Evoker Works

Augmentation Evoker is a support-damage hybrid. Your job is to keep Ebon Might active on your allies while dealing damage yourself through Eruption and empowered abilities. You’re not just a buffer — you deal real personal damage — but your biggest value comes from the primary stat boost Ebon Might gives to the entire raid.

The gameplay revolves around 30-second cycles. You cast Ebon Might to start the buff, then spend the next 30 seconds extending it by casting Eruption (your main filler), Fire Breath, and Upheaval. Every Eruption and every empowered cast adds time to Ebon Might through the Sands of Time talent. If you stop casting, Ebon Might falls off and your raid loses the stat buff.

Prescience is your other key buff — a 2-charge ability that gives Critical Strike to allies. Use it on cooldown, never overcap charges. It’s rotational, not something you save.

Your big cooldown is Breath of Eons (2 min). It debuffs enemies to store 30% of all raid damage dealt, then explodes for that stored damage when it expires. It also extends Ebon Might and triggers the Duplicate passive, which doubles your Eruption, Fire Breath, and Upheaval casts. Press it on cooldown every time. Holding it for “better windows” is weaker than just pressing it.

Between cooldowns, you fill with Eruption whenever Ebon Might is active, and Living Flame when you need a basic filler. The difference between a good Aug Evoker and a great one is Ebon Might uptime and Eruption volume. Top players cast Eruption 20+ times per minute. If you’re below that, you’re either letting Ebon Might drop or not filling GCDs.

How This Is Affecting Your Damage

Living Flame filler at 6.8/min vs top’s 11.3/min — the biggest gap. Top players fill every empty GCD with Living Flame between Eruptions. 4.5 fewer Living Flames per minute means dead GCDs where nothing is happening. Every empty GCD is damage gone, and less Ebon Might extension through the Sands of Time passive.
Eruption at 15.7/min vs top’s 21.0/min. This cascades from the low filler rate. Fewer Living Flames means less Essence generation, which means fewer Eruptions available. 5.3 fewer Eruptions per minute means less Ebon Might extension, fewer Bombardment procs, and less personal damage.
Ebon Might refreshed 1.8/min vs top’s 1.4/min. You’re pressing Ebon Might more often than top players, which means the buff is falling off and needing manual refresh. Top players extend it so well through Eruption and Empowers that they barely need to recast it. More Eruptions = longer Ebon Might = fewer manual refreshes = more GCDs for damage.
Healthstone at 92% and health potion at 69% is solid. Best consumable discipline on the team aside from pre-pots. The defensive buttons are getting pressed when they need to be.
23% early death rate. Dying in almost 1 in 4 pulls. When an Aug Evoker dies, the entire raid loses Ebon Might — the damage loss is multiplied across every player who had the buff.
