Bottom Line

Nucke contributes 82.6K DPS vs the reference Rampire's 106.4K — a 22% gap that is primarily execution-driven given only 6 talent diffs and near-identical item levels (290 vs 259). The core rotation issue: Soul Cleave CPM is 11.7 vs 17.2 — a 32% deficit on the primary soul fragment consumer and self-heal button. Felblade usage at 0.3/min vs 3.4/min is the single biggest rotational gap — this is a resource generator and gap-closer that should be pressed on cooldown. Fracture CPM is 50.9 vs 57.7, suggesting some GCD downtime. On the defensive side, Nucke uses Demon Spikes well (41 casts) but died at 7:40 — 17 seconds before the kill — after a Last Resort proc followed by a 754K Putrid Fist with insufficient mitigation. Fel Devastation is over-used as a rotational ability (9 casts vs Rampire's 1) while Metamorphosis is used aggressively (13 casts). The DPS gap comes from lower Voidfall Meteor damage (7.0M vs 11.8M, −41%) which is directly tied to the Soul Cleave and Felblade CPM deficit — fewer consumers means fewer procs of the trinket/tier interactions that drive Vengeance damage.

Felblade — The Biggest Gap

Felblade is a 15-second cooldown that generates Fury and functions as a gap-closer. Rampire presses it 28 times in 8:13 (3.4/min). Nucke pressed it twice in 7:57. This is not a minor rotation miss — it is functionally absent from the rotation.

Nucke — 2 Uses

Only 2 Felblade casts in nearly 8 minutes. This means ~30 Felblade casts were missed. Each missed Felblade is a lost GCD, lost Fury generation, and a lost gap-closer opportunity.

26 missed Felblades × ~40 Fury each = ~1,040 lost Fury. That translates to roughly 15+ additional Soul Cleaves that never happened — which compounds into less self-healing, less damage, and fewer Soul Fragment procs.

Rampire — 28 Uses

28 uses in 8:13 = one every ~17.6 seconds. Nearly on cooldown (15s). This feeds the Fury economy that enables frequent Soul Cleave presses. Felblade is both a rotational DPS ability AND a resource generator.

Combined with 474 Fractures (vs Nucke's 405) and 141 Soul Cleaves (vs 93), Rampire's entire Fury economy runs significantly hotter — more generation, more spending, more procs.

Actionable Items

1. Press Felblade on cooldown. 2 uses in 7:57 vs Rampire's 28 in 8:13. This is a 15-second cooldown that generates Fury and closes gaps. Missing it ~26 times costs approximately 1,040 Fury, which translates to roughly 15 additional Soul Cleaves. This single fix would close a large portion of both the DPS and survivability gap. Bind it prominently and treat it as a rotational priority, not an optional button.
2. Increase Soul Cleave frequency. 11.7/min vs 17.2/min is a 32% deficit on the primary soul fragment consumer. Soul Cleave is simultaneously damage, self-healing, and fragment consumption. More Soul Cleaves directly feeds the Voidfall Meteor proc chain (which accounts for the largest single damage gap: 7.0M vs 11.8M). The Felblade fix will naturally increase available Fury for more Soul Cleaves, but also ensure Soul Cleave is always being pressed when Fury is available and fragments are up.
3. Reduce Fel Devastation to emergency-only use. 9 casts (1.1/min) vs Rampire's 1. With 74% overheal, most Fel Devastation healing is wasted. The GCDs spent on 8 extra Fel Devastation casts could instead be Soul Cleave or Fracture presses. Use Fel Devastation as a major defensive CD to survive dangerous moments, not as a rotational filler.
4. Increase Demon Spikes coverage on Putrid Fist. 5 of 14 Putrid Fist hits (36%) had zero absorb, meaning Demon Spikes was down. While 41 total uses is solid, timing them to ensure coverage on Putrid Fist windows would smooth out the damage spikes. The 7:38 hit (754K, 628K net) that preceded the death had only 126K absorbed — better Demon Spikes alignment here could have prevented the death.
5. Close the Fracture CPM gap. 50.9/min vs 57.7/min is a 12% deficit on the primary GCD filler. While this is the smallest gap per-ability, Fracture generates Soul Fragments which fuel the entire damage and healing engine. More Fractures = more fragments = more Soul Cleave value = more Voidfall Meteor procs. Combined with Felblade and Soul Cleave improvements, this completes the Fury economy fix.
6. Survive the kill fight. Dying at 7:40 in a 7:57 kill means the raid had to finish without a tank for 17 seconds. The 7:38 Putrid Fist (754K) was the killing blow sequence. With Last Resort already consumed earlier in the fight, there was no safety net. Better defensive CD alignment in the final 30 seconds (Demon Spikes + Fiery Brand on the 7:38 fist, or holding Metamorphosis for an emergency heal) could prevent this.
