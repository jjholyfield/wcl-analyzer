Aura Mastery Timing Consistency
P1 Aura Mastery lands at 33s, 54-65s range. The 8 pulls without AM are short P1 wipes (F3, F5, F19, F22 at <1:50) or pulls where it was not pressed (F2, F23). In P3, AM lands at 336-396s on the deepest pulls.

Zero P2 AM uses across all 11 P2-reaching pulls. With a 3-minute CD and first use at ~60s, AM is available again at ~240s (4:00) which is mid-P2. This is a gap in the team's CD coverage.
Lay on Hands as Opener
LoH is cast within the first 14 seconds of every pull (avg 8.6s). This appears to be a planned P1 tank-save rather than an emergency triage.

On a 10-minute CD, LoH is never available for P2 or P3. On 7+ minute pulls, it would become available again near the end of P3 -- but those pulls are rare. The team should decide whether this planned P1 usage outweighs having LoH available as a P3 emergency tool.

Actionable Items

1. Reduce P1 Overheal: Replace Holy Light with DPS Filler

P1 overheal at 48% means nearly half of all healing output in the first 2 minutes is wasted. Holy Light is the primary offender: 73% overheal, with 79 of 109 total casts in P1. In P1, raid damage is manageable -- Alleria + Sentinels do not require hard casting Holy Light on non-tank targets. Replace those GCDs with Judgment (HP + damage) or Hammer of Wrath (damage). This preserves mana for P2/P3 where overheal drops to 35% and 31%, proving the spell selection corrects when damage is real.

2. Fill the P2 Aura Mastery Gap

Zero Aura Mastery was used in P2 across all 11 P2-reaching pulls. With a 3-minute CD and first use at ~60s in P1, AM should be available again by ~240s (4:00), which is mid-P2. P2 has significant raid damage from Rift Simulacrum and Undying Voidspawn. Coordinate with the healing team to assign AM to a specific P2 damage window -- this is 12% DR for the entire raid during the Rift phase.

3. Evaluate Lay on Hands as P1 Opener

LoH is cast within the first 14 seconds of every pull (avg 8.6s). This is a 10-minute cooldown consumed before the fight demands it. On a 7-minute prog pull, LoH never comes back. The team should consciously decide: does P1 truly require LoH for the tank swap or initial burst, or could it be saved for a P3 emergency tank-save? If the answer is "P1 needs it," the current usage is correct. If not, holding it gives a powerful emergency tool for the phase where everything overlaps.

4. Bursting Emptiness Positioning (P1)

Two of Dueche's 3 actionable deaths (F12 at 1:55, F21 at 1:05) were to Bursting Emptiness -- the Void Droplet detonation. Both deaths are in the late P1 window when Droplets are active. Review positioning relative to Droplet spawns in the 1:00-2:00 window. As a Holy Paladin with plate armor and Divine Shield available, surviving P1 mechanics should be achievable with better positioning.

5. Avenging Wrath Discipline is Excellent

AW used in 19/19 pulls, always within the first 6 seconds, with a second use in 10/11 P2-reaching pulls and a third in 3 P3-reaching pulls. This is textbook CD discipline -- using AW on cooldown and maximizing total uses per pull. Continue this pattern. Light of Dawn volume also scales correctly, with more P2 casts (282) than P1 (266) despite shorter P2 time.
