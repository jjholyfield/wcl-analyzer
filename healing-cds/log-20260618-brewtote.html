Died in 26 of 27 pulls. Brewtote's healing throughput (145.8K avg HPS) and overheal discipline (24.3%) are solid for progression, but none of that matters when the survival rate is 3.7%. The raid reached P3 in only 6 of 27 pulls, and Brewtote was alive for P3 in just 5 of those. Eleven deaths in P1 alone (before 2:14) represent fundamental mechanic failures early in the fight. When Brewtote survives deep into fights (F28: 5:41, 157.6K HPS, #2 healer), the healing output is strong. The core issue is staying alive through P1 mechanics (Echoing Darkness, Void Remnants, Interrupting Tremor) and P2 mechanics (Simulacrum Backlash, Voidstalker Sting, Gravity Collapse). CD discipline is excellent — Spirit Link Totem deploys at 0:29 every pull with sub-1s standard deviation. Fix survival and the healing performance is already there.

Spirit Link Totem — Excellent Discipline
Used in every single pull. P1 timing is remarkably consistent at 0:29 with only 0.86s standard deviation. This aligns with Null Corona damage at ~0:20-0:25 — SLT lands right after the first major raid damage event. Second use appears in longer pulls (P2) at ~4:08-4:24, showing CD is tracked and re-used when available. 5 fights had a second SLT use — all in pulls that reached deep P2.
Astral Shift — Critical Underuse
Only used in 8 of 27 pulls (30% usage rate) for a 1.5-minute CD. Zero uses in P1 (where 11 deaths happened) and zero uses in P3 (where all 5 entries resulted in death). This is the single highest-impact improvement available. On a 90-second cooldown, Astral Shift should be available 2-3 times per pull on longer fights. Death forensics show Voidstalker Sting (100K/tick) and Gravity Collapse (161K) regularly appearing in the death sequence — a 40-60% DR for 12 seconds directly counters these.
Ascendance — Too Early in P1
Ascendance fires at 0:06-0:07 in most pulls — within the first 7 seconds of the fight. No significant raid damage occurs this early in P1 (Null Corona is at ~0:20, Grasp of Emptiness at ~0:25). The first meaningful healing check is around 0:20-0:30. Using Ascendance this early wastes its 12s window on essentially full-health targets. Consider delaying to ~0:20 to align with the first damage spike, or saving for later in P1 when Vorelus abilities start overlapping. The 2nd Ascendance in P2 (~3:37 avg) aligns well with P2 damage patterns.

Actionable Items

1. Use Astral Shift Proactively — Especially in P1 and P3
The problem: Astral Shift was used in only 8 of 27 pulls (30% usage rate). Zero uses in P1 (11 deaths) and zero uses in P3 (5 deaths, 100% death rate). It is a 90-second cooldown with 40-60% DR for 12 seconds.

What to change: Use Astral Shift at the first sign of danger in each phase: in P1 when Echoing Darkness or Void Remnants appear, during Intermission for Stellar Emission (already happening — 4 uses), and in P3 immediately when Voidstalker Sting or Gravity Collapse start. On a 90s CD, it should be available 2-3 times per 6-minute pull.

Expected impact: The death forensics for F27 and F28 show 100K+ per tick from Voidstalker Sting and 161K from Gravity Collapse. Astral Shift at 40% DR reduces these to 60K and 97K respectively — survivable with normal healing. This single change could cut deaths by 30-50%.
2. P1 Mechanic Survival — Echoing Darkness, Void Remnants, Interrupting Tremor
The problem: 11 of 27 deaths happen in P1, before the fight even reaches the 2:14 Intermission mark. The top P1 killers are Echoing Darkness (Vorelus ability), Void Remnants (ground damage), and Interrupting Tremor.

What to change: These are positioning/awareness mechanics, not healing checks. Echoing Darkness requires staying out of Vorelus's frontal. Void Remnants requires dodging ground effects. Interrupting Tremor requires movement. Ghost Wolf (used at 2.6 CPM in Inter but rarely in P1) and Gust of Wind (22 total casts) should be used more aggressively in P1 for repositioning. Spiritwalker's Grace allows healing while moving — already being used (79 casts) but should be prioritized during mechanic overlap windows.

Expected impact: Eliminating even half the P1 deaths means 5-6 more pulls reaching Intermission and P2 with a full healing team. Each early healer death cascades into a wipe within 30-60 seconds.
3. Delay Ascendance from 0:06 to 0:20-0:25
The problem: Ascendance fires within 6-7 seconds of the pull in 23 of 25 P1 uses. No significant raid damage occurs until Null Corona at ~0:20 and Grasp of Emptiness at ~0:25. The first 15 seconds of the fight are primarily tank damage from Sentinels.

What to change: Delay Ascendance to 0:20-0:25 to align with the first wave of raid-wide damage. Ascendance's 12-second window will then cover 0:20-0:32, which includes the first Null Corona hit and Grasp of Emptiness. This also means the 2nd Ascendance use (3 min cooldown) would be available at 3:20-3:25 in P2, which is a better P2 timing than the current ~3:37 average.

Expected impact: More effective healing during the first damage spike. Currently Ascendance heals into full-health targets for most of its window.
4. Deploy SLT and Ascendance in P3
The problem: Brewtote died in all 5 P3 entries. SLT was used 0 times in P3. The 2nd SLT use typically lands at 4:08-4:24 (mid P2), which means it is back off cooldown around 7:08-7:24 — too late for P3 entries at 5:09. Ascendance's 2nd use at ~3:13 comes back at ~6:13 — also late for P3.

What to change: Consider shifting the 2nd SLT use earlier in P2 (3:30-3:45 range) so it is available again by ~6:30-6:45 in P3. Alternatively, save the 2nd SLT specifically for P3 entry and use other tools in mid-P2. Ascendance timing in P2 (~3:13) is already early enough that the 3rd use at 6:13 catches early P3 — this is fine if Brewtote survives that long.

Expected impact: P3 is the hardest phase with multiple damage overlaps. Having SLT available for P3 gives the entire raid a 10% DR + HP redistribution during the most lethal damage windows.
