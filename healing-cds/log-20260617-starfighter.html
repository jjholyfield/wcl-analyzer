Bottom Line

Starfighter survived the kill and dealt 165K DPS across 7:58 — a 25.7% gap behind the #1 reference Carloseljet (222K DPS in 7:15). Rotational CPM is nearly identical (66.7 vs 65.5) — the gap is not from dead time or missed GCDs. The difference comes from ability priority: Starfighter over-casts Thunder Clap (+96% CPM) and Raging Blow (+27%) at the expense of Rampage (−10%), Bloodthirst (−13%), and Thunder Blast (−21%). Odyn's Fury damage is 47% lower than reference despite more casts, indicating poor cooldown alignment with Avatar/Recklessness. With only 3 talent differences and similar ilvl (289.5 vs 259.1), the gap is pure execution and priority tuning.

Priority Problem: Rampage & Bloodthirst Down

Rampage is the #1 priority ability — it enrages you, hits hard, and enables the rest of the rotation. At 17.2 CPM vs 19.2 CPM, Starfighter is missing roughly 16 Rampage casts over the fight. Bloodthirst is the primary Rage generator and Rampage enabler at 11.2 vs 12.8 CPM — about 13 missed casts. Together, these two abilities account for ~32% of reference total damage. The lost casts are being filled with lower-priority Thunder Clap and Raging Blow instead.

Thunder Clap Over-Use

Thunder Clap at 5.4 CPM (43 casts) vs 2.8 CPM (20 casts) is being used more than double the reference rate. Thunder Clap is a low-priority filler that generates Thunder Blast procs, but pressing it too often displaces higher-value Bloodthirst and Rampage casts. The reference gets more Thunder Blasts (67 vs 58) with fewer Thunder Claps (20 vs 43) — suggesting better proc management through Bloodthirst-first priority.

Why This Matters

Avatar grants 20% damage amp and Recklessness grants crit bonus. Odyn's Fury under both CDs does roughly 2x the damage of an unbuffed cast. Carloseljet fires Odyn's Fury 10 times, always inside CD windows — every cast is amplified. Starfighter fires it 15 times but many land outside CD windows, resulting in 47% less total damage from the ability. Holding Odyn's Fury for 5-6 seconds to sync with Avatar/Recklessness would be the single biggest DPS gain available.

Actionable Items

1. Sync Odyn's Fury with Avatar + Recklessness. This is the single biggest DPS gain available. Currently Odyn's Fury fires 15 times but many casts land outside cooldown windows, producing 47% less total damage (−4.8M) compared to Carloseljet who casts it 10 times all inside CD windows. Hold Odyn's Fury for 5-6 seconds after pressing Avatar + Recklessness. Every OF cast should be amplified by at least one CD. An unbuffed OF at 0:01 (pre-pull) is nearly wasted compared to a buffed OF at 0:30 during the Avatar/Recklessness window. Estimated gain: +3-4M damage (∼7-9K DPS).
2. Prioritize Rampage over Thunder Clap and Raging Blow. Rampage is at 17.2 CPM vs 19.2 — roughly 16 missed casts over the fight. Thunder Clap is being pressed at 5.4 CPM (43 casts) vs the reference's 2.8 CPM (20 casts). Each Rampage replaces a Thunder Clap or Raging Blow GCD and deals substantially more damage while maintaining Enrage uptime. The reference manages to get more Thunder Blast procs (67 vs 58) with fewer Thunder Clap inputs (20 vs 43) because Bloodthirst-generated Rage feeds more Rampages, which trigger more proc chances. Estimated gain: +3-5M damage (∼7-10K DPS).
3. Use Spell Reflection on cooldown for free damage. Carloseljet used Spell Reflection 13 times vs Starfighter's 2. On Rotmire, Rotting Pustules are constant magic ticks that can be reflected for bonus damage. This is essentially a free DPS button with no GCD cost. With 467 Pustules ticks taken, each Spell Reflection window could reflect multiple ticks for additional damage with zero rotation disruption.
4. Increase Bloodthirst usage to fuel Rage generation. Bloodthirst at 11.2 CPM vs 12.8 CPM means 13 fewer casts. Bloodthirst is the primary Rage generator — fewer Bloodthirsts means less Rage, which means fewer Rampages. The 11% Haste gap partially explains this (∼1 CPM), but the remaining delta suggests Bloodthirst is being deprioritized in favor of Raging Blow (27% higher CPM than reference). When Rampage is not ready, Bloodthirst should be the next button, not Raging Blow.
5. Target Haste and Mastery in gear upgrades. Starfighter has −11% Haste and −19% Mastery vs the reference, with +37% more Crit. Fury Warrior stat priority favors Haste and Mastery over Crit for sustained damage. Shifting itemization toward Haste/Mastery pieces would naturally increase GCD speed (more casts per minute) and Enrage/Rampage damage. This is a longer-term improvement but would passively close part of the Rampage CPM gap.

Uptime: 66.7 rotational CPM matches the reference's 65.5 — there is essentially zero dead time. Only 1 casting gap >2.5s across 8 minutes (a 2.8s gap after a Charge at 6:46). The GCD usage is extremely tight.

Kill pull was best pull: 165K on the kill was Starfighter's highest sustained DPS all night, showing focused execution when it mattered.

Summary

The 25.7% DPS gap vs #1 ranked Carloseljet breaks down to: ~40% cooldown alignment (Odyn's Fury outside CD windows), ~35% ability priority (Thunder Clap and Raging Blow displacing Rampage and Bloodthirst), ~15% Spell Reflection free damage, and ~10% stat distribution (less Haste reducing natural CPM ceiling). The good news: none of this is gear-gated. Fixing Odyn's Fury alignment alone would close roughly 7-9K DPS of the gap. Adding proper Rampage prioritization closes another 7-10K. Together that would put Starfighter in the 180-185K range — competitive with the #3-#5 references on the leaderboard.
