Bottom Line

Sìlencio survived the full 7:58 kill and delivered 154.5K HPS with solid CD timing on all 3 Blooms. Actionable Items

1. Deploy major CDs AT Bloom, not after. Across all 3 Fungal Blooms, Sìlencio's Convoke/Tranquility arrived 3-17 seconds after the damage window. Blooms are on a fixed 136-second timer (2:00, 4:16, 6:32). The reference fires Convoke 0.9-1.8s before Bloom hits and chains Tranquility immediately after. Start Convoke at 1:58, 4:14, and 6:30. If Convoke is on cooldown, start Tranquility at the Bloom second. The first 2-3 seconds of a Bloom contain the most damage — every second of delay means healing lands on targets that are already topped by other healers.
2. Learn Cat Form weaving for DPS windows. The reference deals 6,550 DPS vs Sìlencio's 2,582 DPS. The gap is entirely Cat Form abilities: Feral Frenzy (1,212K damage), Rip (687K), and Rake (274K). During the 15-25s gaps between damage events, shift to Cat Form, apply Rake and Rip, then shift back to heal. This is the single biggest HPS + DPS improvement available — Cat Form damage generates Symbiotic Bloom healing, so it contributes to both metrics simultaneously.
3. Increase Rejuvenation and Regrowth CPM. Rejuvenation is 14.3 CPM vs 18.2 (−21%) and Regrowth is 15.1 CPM vs 18.4 (−18%). Over the full fight, that is ~31 fewer Rejuvenations and ~26 fewer Regrowths. Each additional HoT active means more Mastery (Harmony) stacking, which amplifies all other healing. The GCD downtime that could fill these casts is where the Cat Form weaving from item #2 should go — either Cat damage or more HoTs, but never idle globals.
4. Swiftmend on cooldown. 3.3 CPM vs reference's 4.5 CPM (−27%, 10 fewer casts). Swiftmend is instant, costs no mana, triggers key talents (Soul of the Forest/Verdancy), and removes a HoT to create an Efflorescence puddle. Using it on cooldown during damage windows maximizes passive throughput from talent procs.
5. Reduce Tranquility overheal. Tranquility overheal is 57% vs the reference's 49%. The late deployment timing (3.1s after Bloom #1) is the direct cause — by the time Tranquility is channeling, other healers have already handled the spike. Deploying Tranquility immediately at or within 1 second of Bloom will ensure it heals into active damage rather than topping already-full targets.
