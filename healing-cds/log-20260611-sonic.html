Bottom Line — Progression Context

This is prog data from 26 wipes on Mythic Crown of the Cosmos — no kill yet. On the longest pull (Fight 23, 4:52), Sonic dealt 141K DPS before dying at 3:46 to Voidstalker Sting. The reference kills come from full-duration fights (~8:00+), so the comparison is inherently unequal — reference players had full Tyrant cycles and execute phases that prog wipes never reach. That said, the core rotation gaps are real and fixable now: Hand of Gul'dan CPM is 9.8 vs 12.6 reference (−22%), Demonbolt is 7.2 vs 10.1 (−29%), and Summon Demonic Tyrant was cast only 3 times in 3:46 vs the reference's rate of ~1 every 62 seconds. Total rotational CPM of 32.9 vs 38.4 means roughly 5.5 fewer ability casts per minute. With similar ilvl (289.7 vs 288.9) and only 5 talent differences, the gap is execution-based and will carry into kill attempts once the raid gets there.

Core Spender Gap: Hand of Gul'dan & Demonbolt

Hand of Gul'dan is the primary Soul Shard spender and the #1 DPS driver during Tyrant windows — the current Demonology meta revolves around maximizing HoG casts during Tyrant. At 9.8 CPM vs 12.6 CPM (−22%), Sonic is missing roughly 10-11 Hand of Gul'dan casts per fight-minute-equivalent. Demonbolt (the Demonic Core spender that generates shards and procs) is even further behind at 7.2 vs 10.1 (−29%). Meanwhile, Shadow Bolt (the lowest-priority filler) is 22% over-represented — a clear sign of spending GCDs on the weakest shard generator when Demonic Core procs should be consumed instead.

Dreadstalker Cadence

Call Dreadstalkers at 2.1 CPM vs 3.0 CPM (−29%) means Sonic is casting Dreadstalkers every ~28.2 seconds instead of every ~20 seconds. Dreadstalkers are a core part of the Tyrant setup — they must be active when Tyrant is cast to be extended. Missing Dreadstalker casts means fewer demons for Tyrant to empower, and fewer Demonic Core procs from the Dreadstalker talent chain. The reference casts Dreadstalkers almost exactly on their ~20s cooldown.

Why This Matters

Demonology's damage is heavily front-loaded into Tyrant windows. Tyrant dynamically scales damage based on how many Hand of Gul'dan casts happen during its 20-second duration. The reference casts Tyrant every ~62 seconds — nearly on cooldown. Sonic's gap between Tyrant #2 and #3 was 111 seconds (nearly 2 minutes), which represents a massive lost burst window. Additionally, Grimoire: Imp Lord should be cast just before or just after Tyrant so the Imp Lord is extended by Tyrant — Sonic's single Imp Lord cast at 2:58 was 3 seconds before Tyrant #3, which is close but suboptimal compared to the reference who consistently pairs them. With only 1 Imp Lord cast in 3:46 vs the reference's rate of ~1 per 2.5 minutes, there are likely 1-2 missed Imp Lord casts.

Actionable Items

1. Increase Hand of Gul'dan and Demonbolt casts per minute. This is the single biggest DPS improvement available. Hand of Gul'dan at 9.8 CPM vs 12.6 (−22%) and Demonbolt at 7.2 vs 10.1 (−29%) represent the core of Demonology's damage engine. Shadow Bolt (the weakest filler) at 6.9 CPM vs 5.7 is being over-used — Demonic Core procs should be consumed via Demonbolt before falling back to Shadow Bolt. Every Demonbolt generates more procs and shards than Shadow Bolt, which feeds more Hand of Gul'dan casts. Fix: Always check for Demonic Core stacks before casting Shadow Bolt. At 2+ stacks, Demonbolt should be the priority. This alone could close 2-3 CPM of the gap. Estimated gain: 8-12% DPS increase.
2. Cast Demonic Tyrant on cooldown with proper setup. Only 3 Tyrant casts in 3:46 (one every ~75 seconds) vs the reference's rate of ~1 every 62 seconds. The gap between Tyrant #2 (1:10) and #3 (3:01) was 111 seconds — nearly 2x the cooldown. A missed Tyrant window during prog is a massive DPS loss because Tyrant dynamically scales damage based on Hand of Gul'dan casts during its 20-second duration. Fix: Track Tyrant cooldown and begin preparing (saving Dreadstalkers + Imp Lord) 5-10 seconds before it's ready. Cast Dreadstalkers → Imp Lord → Tyrant → spam Hand of Gul'dan during the window. Estimated gain: 10-15% DPS increase from the extra Tyrant window alone.
3. Use Grimoire: Imp Lord on cooldown, synced with Tyrant. Only 1 Imp Lord cast in 3:46 vs the reference's ~1 every 2.5 minutes. Imp Lord summons a powerful demon that should be extended by Tyrant. The reference pairs every Imp Lord with a Tyrant window. Fix: When Tyrant is coming off cooldown, cast Dreadstalkers and Imp Lord in the 2-3 seconds before Tyrant so both get extended. This is the standard Tyrant setup sequence. Estimated gain: 3-5% DPS increase.
4. Use Dark Pact aggressively to survive dangerous mechanics. Dark Pact was used only 1 time in 3:46 despite being a 1-minute cooldown. The reference used it 7 times in 8:19 — essentially on cooldown for every high-damage window. Sonic died to Voidstalker Sting, which is predictable debuff damage that Dark Pact could absorb. Dead DPS does zero DPS. Fix: Use Dark Pact proactively before known damage events (Void Expulsion, Voidstalker Sting, Silverstrike Barrage). It costs health to activate but the shield more than compensates. On Crown specifically, use it during every Sting application and every Barrage.
5. Eliminate falling damage. Two instances of Falling damage totaling 411K during the fight. Crown of the Cosmos has platform mechanics requiring careful positioning. Falling off platforms is fully avoidable and contributes to both damage taken and potential death risk. Fix: Stay centered on platforms during transitions, use Demonic Gateway and Demonic Circle: Teleport for emergency repositioning instead of running off edges.

Summary — Progression Context

This is prog data from 26 wipes — no kill yet. The comparison to reference kills is inherently unfair (references had full fight duration, execute phases, and optimized strategies). However, the core rotation gaps identified here — Hand of Gul'dan under-casting (−22%), Demonbolt displacement by Shadow Bolt (−29%), missed Tyrant windows, and under-using Imp Lord — are execution patterns that carry into every pull regardless of fight length. The defensive gap (Dark Pact and Unending Resolve under-use) is equally critical: surviving deeper into the fight is the #1 priority on prog. Fixing rotation fundamentals + defensive usage would increase both DPS output and survivability, directly contributing to the raid's ability to progress past Phase 2 mechanics. With 289.7 ilvl and a well-allocated stat profile, the gear is not the bottleneck — this is execution and cooldown discipline.
