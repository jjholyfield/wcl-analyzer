Phase-Specific Insights

P1 Sentinels (101K avg) is strong. Multi-target cleave on Sentinels + Void Droplets is where Demonology Warlock shines -- Implosion, Hand of Gul'dan, and Wild Imp splash all scale well with multiple targets. Best pull hit 136K (fight 17). The two deaths in P1 (fights 11 and 22) were to specific mechanics (Silverstrike Arrow, Bursting Emptiness), not DPS issues.

Intermission is the survivability wall. Near-zero DPS is expected (it is a survival/movement phase with Stellar Emission channel + Silverstrike Barrage dodging). But Sonic died in 2 of 14 pulls here, and both deaths were early first-2 deaths that compromised the entire pull. Burning Rush self-damage (120-140K per death) is a significant contributor -- turning it off during Intermission would help survival substantially.

P2 Rift (44K avg) drops significantly from P1. Expected -- the transition from multi-target Sentinels to Rift phase mechanics with more movement and targeted abilities naturally reduces DPS. Best P2 pull was 66K (fight 12). Fight 11's 5K P2 DPS is an outlier from being dead since intermission. Excluding that, P2 avg is 47K -- reasonable for a movement-heavy phase with fewer cleave targets.

P3 Cosmos (20K avg) is where the team is still learning. Only reached P3 in 11/14 pulls. Best P3 DPS was 34K (fight 17 -- the deepest pull at 7:48). Several P3 pulls show very low DPS (6-7K) on early P3 entries, suggesting Sonic is frequently dying or compromised early in P3. As the team survives deeper into P3 and Sonic gets more complete Tyrant cycles, this number should climb. The gap between best (34K) and worst (6K) P3 shows how much room there is to improve with more experience in the phase.

Actionable Improvements

Priority 1 -- Survival

Turn Off Burning Rush During Intermission

In both Intermission deaths (fights 15 and 25), Burning Rush self-damage contributed 120-140K damage in the 10 seconds before death. Intermission has constant heavy raid damage from Stellar Emission and Silverstrike Barrage. The movement speed from Burning Rush is not worth the health drain when the entire raid is under sustained damage pressure. Toggle it off before Silversunder Catastrophe ends and the Intermission begins (~2:14).

Impact: Eliminating 2 first-2 deaths would raise survival rate from 64% to 79% and improve average DPS by ~3-4K (less time dead).

Priority 1 -- Survival

Avoid Bursting Emptiness (Void Droplet Explosions)

Two first-2 deaths (fights 9 and 22) came from Bursting Emptiness -- the AoE explosion when Void Droplets reach their target or die. In fight 22, Sonic took 576K from two Bursting Emptiness hits within seconds, plus 183K from Echoing Darkness. Stay at range from Void Droplets when they are close to dying or about to burst. Use Demonic Gateway or Soulburn: Demonic Circle for emergency repositioning.

Impact: Eliminating these 2 deaths would bring first-2 death count from 5 to 3, and survival rate to 79%.

Priority 2 -- Consistency

P3 Performance Stabilization

P3 DPS ranges from 6K (fight 6, very short P3 with cascade death) to 34K (fight 17, deepest pull). This 5x variance is partly explained by varying P3 duration (short P3s have fewer Tyrant windows), but some of it is execution. In the three longest P3 entries (F17: 34K, F25: 31K, F12: 28K), DPS is more consistent. Focus on maintaining Shadow Bolt/Demonbolt casting during P3 mechanics -- movement is the biggest enemy of cast-time dependent specs.

Impact: As the team survives deeper into P3, Sonic's DPS should naturally improve with more complete Tyrant cycles. Best P3 DPS (34K on fight 17) shows improvement is happening.

Priority 3 -- Optimization

Review Talent Build Against Top References

All 5 comp-matched references have 8-13 talent differences from Sonic's current build. While 8+ diffs suggests a meaningfully different talent configuration (possibly different key talent choices in the Demonology or Diabolist trees), Sonic's ilvl (291.7) is actually higher than all references (286-290). The talent differences could represent an alternative viable build, or they could indicate optimization opportunities. Compare talent trees side-by-side on Wowhead's talent calculator to identify which specific nodes differ.

Impact: Potentially meaningful DPS gain if key talents are suboptimal, but survival improvements (#1 and #2) should take priority during progression.

Priority 3 -- Awareness

Fight 1 Cold Start

Fight 1 (37.5K DPS) was the lowest qualifying pull and was the night's opener with only 19 players. This is 29% below average. While first-pull cold starts are normal, being 29% below suggests either incomplete pre-pull setup (missing pre-pot, delayed Tyrant ramp) or the missing 20th player causing mechanics to hit harder. Not actionable beyond general awareness.

What's Working

P1 Sentinel cleave is a standout strength. 101K average with a 136K best shows strong multi-target output. Demonology's demon army (Implosion, Hand of Gul'dan, Wild Imp cleave) scales well on Sentinels + Droplets, and Sonic is executing this phase consistently across all 14 pulls.

Session improvement trend is positive. First half averaged 51.8K, second half averaged 54.2K -- a 4.7% improvement over the night. The three best pulls (F17, F25, F12) all came in the second half of the session, suggesting learning and adaptation.

Best-pull DPS ceiling is promising. Three pulls at 66-67K DPS shows a repeatable ceiling. When surviving deep into P3 (fights 17 and 25 are the two deepest pulls this night at 7:48 and 7:56), Sonic maintains strong output. The gap between survival pulls (54.8K avg) and death pulls (48.5K avg) is only 11.5% -- most of the DPS loss is from dying, not from inconsistent rotation.
