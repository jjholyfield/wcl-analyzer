How Marksmanship Hunter Works

Marksmanship Hunter is a ranged spec built around a two-ability core: Aimed Shot and Arcane Shot. Aimed Shot is your hardest-hitting ability and the one you want to press as often as possible. Arcane Shot is your filler — you use it between Aimed Shots to keep generating damage and spending focus.

Rapid Fire is a channeled ability that deals damage and regenerates focus. You fire it on cooldown because it feeds directly into your next Aimed Shot. Steady Shot is your focus generator — you use it when you’re low on focus and nothing else is available. The less you need Steady Shot, the better, because it’s your lowest-damage button.

Your major cooldown is Trueshot (2 min). During Trueshot, Aimed Shot has no cast time and your damage spikes significantly. You want to dump as many Aimed Shots as possible during every Trueshot window. Volley is your AoE cooldown for multi-target windows.

The single most important thing for MM Hunter is always be casting. Every global cooldown you spend doing nothing is damage lost forever. If Aimed Shot is on cooldown and Rapid Fire is on cooldown, press Arcane Shot. If you’re low on focus, press Steady Shot. There is never a reason to stand still doing nothing.

How It’s Affecting Your Damage

25% dead time is costing ~30K+ DPS. A quarter of each fight is spent not pressing any buttons. The #1 MM Hunter has about 15% dead time. Cutting Mortwo’s dead time to match would likely close a significant portion of the 86K DPS gap (183K vs 269K) on its own.
Aimed Shot deficit is the biggest single DPS loss. At 11.4 casts/min vs 15.1 for the #1, that’s 3.7 fewer Aimed Shots every minute. Over a 5-minute pull, that’s roughly 18 missing Aimed Shots — each one hitting for tens of thousands of damage. This is the primary reason for the DPS gap.
More Steady Shot usage than top players. 4.2/min vs 3.6/min means spending more globals on low-damage focus generation. This suggests running out of focus more often, likely due to the dead time gaps breaking the flow of Aimed Shot → Arcane Shot cycling.

What You Need to Change

1
Eliminate Dead Time
Your dead time is 25% vs 15% for the best MM Hunters. That means roughly 1 out of every 4 seconds you’re not pressing anything. Always be casting. If Aimed Shot is on cooldown and Rapid Fire is on cooldown, press Arcane Shot. If you’re out of focus, press Steady Shot. There is no point in MM Hunter’s rotation where standing still is correct.
2
Get More Aimed Shots Off
You’re casting 11.4 Aimed Shots per minute vs 15.1 for the #1. This is the highest-damage ability in your kit — every one you miss is a large chunk of damage lost. Make sure you’re pressing Aimed Shot the instant it comes off cooldown, especially during Trueshot windows where it becomes instant-cast.
3
Press Healthstone
1 healthstone used in 14 pulls is unacceptable. It’s a free heal with its own cooldown — costs nothing and can save you from dying. Press it any time your health drops below 50%.
