How Frost Mage Works

Frost Mage is a proc-based spec built around one core loop: build Freezing stacks on the target, then spend them with Ice Lance for burst damage. Everything you do feeds into this cycle.

Your main filler is Frostbolt. Every time you cast Frostbolt, it applies Freezing stacks to the target. You keep casting Frostbolt until one of two things happens: you get a Brain Freeze proc (which lights up Flurry), or you get a Fingers of Frost proc (which lights up Ice Lance). When either of those procs, you stop Frostbolting and press the glowing button immediately.

The big combo works like this: Frostbolt builds stacks → Brain Freeze procs → you press Flurry (which dumps more stacks and sets up the target) → then you fire Ice Lance to consume those stacks for a fat hit. That Frostbolt → Flurry → Ice Lance chain is the bread and butter. You’ll do it dozens of times per fight.

On top of that, you have two cooldowns on roughly 60-second timers: Frozen Orb and Ray of Frost. Frozen Orb fires out and generates a ton of procs — press it on cooldown. Ray of Frost is a 4-second channel that deals damage and builds Freezing stacks fast — but you want to spend your existing stacks before channeling it, so the new stacks it generates don’t go to waste.

When your Icicle bar fills up, Glacial Spike becomes available — it transforms your Frostbolt into a big hit. Press it immediately when it lights up. Don’t sit on it.

The one rule that matters more than anything else: always be casting. If nothing is proc’d, cast Frostbolt. If something is proc’d, press it. If a cooldown is up, use it. Every second you spend not pressing a button is DPS you’re leaving on the floor. The difference between a good Frost Mage and a great one isn’t knowing the rotation — it’s never stopping.

How This Is Affecting Your Damage

37% dead time = 148 seconds of a 400-second fight with no casts. That includes a single 104-second gap starting at 3:21. You were either dead, disconnected, or running without casting. Even excluding that one gap, you still have 44 seconds of dead time spread across 10+ smaller gaps — moments where you stopped casting to move, look around, or wait for something.
Flurry at 2.8/min means you’re pressing it less than 40% as often as top players. This is the core of the DPS gap. Every Flurry you don’t press means you miss the Flurry → Ice Lance shatter combo. At 7.4/min, top players are chaining that combo roughly every 8 seconds. You’re doing it every 21 seconds. That’s 2.5x less burst damage from your most important combo.
Low Frostbolt (3.6/min vs 6.2/min) is starving your proc engine. Frostbolt is what generates Brain Freeze procs for Flurry and builds Icicles for Glacial Spike. When you cast fewer Frostbolts, you get fewer procs, which means fewer Flurries, fewer Ice Lances, and fewer Glacial Spikes. It’s a cascade — low Frostbolt drags everything else down with it.
Frozen Orb and Ray of Frost are being held too long. Both are ~60s CDs. Top players get 2.0 Frozen Orbs and 1.8 Rays per minute. You’re getting 1.3 of each. That’s 2-3 missed uses over the fight — each one is a chunk of damage and a wave of procs you never generated.
Ice Barrier at 1.8/min vs top players at 0.2-1.0/min. You’re pressing a defensive almost twice a minute. It doesn’t cost a GCD, but it suggests you’re taking avoidable damage that’s pulling your attention away from casting. The more you’re worried about surviving, the less you’re pressing damage buttons.

What You Need to Change

1
Never stop pressing buttons
Your #1 problem isn’t rotation — it’s uptime. 37% dead time means over a third of the fight you’re not doing anything. The goal is to always have a cast going. If you’re moving, queue an Ice Lance (instant). If you’re standing still and nothing is proc’d, Frostbolt. If something is glowing, press it. The target is under 20% dead time — that alone would add ~50K DPS without changing anything else about your rotation.

Practice this: In your next pull, focus only on this — never let your cast bar be empty. Don’t worry about pressing the perfect button. Just press something. Frostbolt is always the right answer when nothing else is available.
2
Press Flurry the instant Brain Freeze procs
You’re getting 2.8 Flurries per minute. Top players get 7.4. The reason your Flurry count is low is a combination of two things: you’re not casting enough Frostbolt to generate Brain Freeze procs, and when you do get the proc, you’re probably not pressing it fast enough before it’s wasted by another Frostbolt overwriting it.

The combo is simple: Brain Freeze procs → Flurry (instant) → Ice Lance (instant) → back to Frostbolt. You should be able to fire that combo in under 2 seconds. If Brain Freeze lights up and you wait, you’re losing your highest-value window.
3
Press Frozen Orb and Ray of Frost on cooldown
Both are ~60-second CDs. In a 6:40 fight, you should get 7+ uses of each. You got 9 Frozen Orbs (fine) but the gaps between uses varied wildly — consistent pressing would add 1-2 more uses and smooth out your proc generation. Ray of Frost at 9 uses is also OK but could be tighter.

Rule of thumb: If either CD has been off cooldown for more than 5 seconds and you haven’t pressed it, you’re holding too long. The only exception is if you need to spend existing Freezing stacks before channeling Ray of Frost.
4
Stay alive without stopping your casts
12 Ice Barriers in 6:40 suggests you’re taking a lot of avoidable damage. Top players press it 1-6 times. Ice Barrier is off the GCD so it’s not directly costing you casts, but whatever’s hitting you is probably also making you move and stop casting. Better positioning = less damage taken = less panic = more time casting Frostbolt.

If the 104-second gap at 3:21 was a death, figure out what killed you and address that specifically. Being dead for nearly 2 minutes is the single biggest DPS loss in this log.
