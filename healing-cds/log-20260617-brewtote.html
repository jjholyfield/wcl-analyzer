Bottom Line

Brewtote survived the kill with 163.8K HPS and an excellent 18.4% overheal rate (reference: 11.9%). Led all healers in raw throughput. CD deployment had a notable gap: Ascendance used at 1:58 for Bloom #1 but then no major CDs for Bloom #2 at 4:16, followed by Spirit Link + Ascendance both used on Bloom #3 at 6:32. The reference (Jollintotems) uses SLT+Ascendance on Bloom #1 and SLT+Ascendance again on Bloom #2 — a tighter cadence. Flame Shock overcasting at 6.5/min vs reference's 1.4/min is consuming globals that could be Riptide or Chain Heal. Strong overall performance with room to tighten CD timing and DPS-filler balance.

CD Cadence Issue

The reference (Jollintotems) uses SLT + Ascendance on Bloom #1 and SLT + Ascendance on Bloom #2 (5:04 + 5:06, which is their fight's Bloom #2 timing). This is the optimal cadence — both CDs cover 2 of 3 Blooms. Brewtote used Ascendance on Bloom #1 but saved SLT until Bloom #3, leaving Bloom #2 with zero major CDs. Since Ascendance was on cooldown from the 1:58 use, neither CD was available for Bloom #2 at 4:16. Shifting SLT to Bloom #2 and Ascendance to Bloom #3 (or vice versa) would ensure every Bloom has at least one major CD.

Flame Shock Overcasting

Flame Shock is a maintenance DoT — apply it once and maintain it. Brewtote cast it 52 times (6.5/min) vs the reference's 10 casts (1.4/min). This is 42 extra globals spent refreshing a DoT that lasts 18 seconds. Those globals would be better spent on Riptide (currently 27% below reference) or Downpour (71% below). Each Flame Shock global replaces a potential 50-80K healing event.

Riptide & Downpour Deficit

Riptide at 8.2/min vs 11.2/min means fewer Tidal Waves procs (which buff the next Healing Wave or Chain Heal) and fewer HoTs ticking on the raid before damage events. Downpour at 1.0/min vs 3.5/min is the bigger gap — the reference uses Downpour 3.5x more often, generating significant burst AoE healing. Together, these abilities account for most of the HPS gap.

Actionable Items

1. Deploy a major CD for every Bloom. Bloom #2 at 4:16 had zero CDs from Brewtote. Spirit Link Totem was available (never used on Bloom #1) but was held until Bloom #3. Assign SLT to Bloom #2 and Ascendance to Blooms #1 and #3. Alternatively, use Ascendance on Bloom #1 and SLT on Bloom #2, then Ascendance again on Bloom #3 (3-min CD allows this). Either way, ensure every Bloom has at least one CD deployed.
2. Reduce Flame Shock casts from 52 to ~10. Flame Shock is a maintenance DoT that lasts 18 seconds. At 6.5 casts/min, Brewtote is re-applying it 4.7x more than the reference (1.4/min). This is 42 wasted globals across the fight. Apply Flame Shock once, refresh only when it falls off or on a new target, and redirect those globals to Riptide and Downpour. Expected gain: ~20-30K additional HPS from the redirected globals.
3. Increase Riptide usage from 8.2/min to 11+/min. Riptide is the core rotational button for Resto Shaman — it feeds Tidal Waves procs (which buff Chain Heal and Healing Wave), provides a HoT for Chain Heal bounce targeting, and has a short 6-second cooldown. The reference casts 82 Riptides vs Brewtote's 65. Those 17 missing Riptides represent lost Tidal Waves procs, weaker Chain Heal bounces, and 1,889K less Riptide healing.
4. Increase Downpour usage from 1.0/min to 3+/min. Downpour is a burst AoE heal on a short cooldown. The reference casts it 26 times (3.5/min) for 5,752K healing vs Brewtote's 8 casts for 2,497K. This is the single largest healing gap by ability (3,255K delta). Press it on cooldown during damage windows — the 8.8% overheal on Brewtote's Downpours shows it is landing efficiently when used.
5. Pre-spread more Riptides before Bloom. On Bloom #1, Brewtote had 1 Riptide out before the damage hit. The reference had 3 Riptides pre-spread. More Riptides = more Tidal Waves procs entering the Bloom window = stronger Chain Heals during peak damage. Start spreading Riptides 10-12 seconds before each Bloom instead of DPSing with Flame Shock.
