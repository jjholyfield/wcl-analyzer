How Survival Hunter Works

Survival Hunter is the only melee Hunter spec. It’s built around a simple but punishing priority system: Kill Command is your highest priority ability that generates Focus and deals strong damage. You press it on cooldown, always.

Raptor Strike and Raptor Swipe are your Focus spenders — Strike for single target, Swipe for cleave. Wildfire Bomb is a short-cooldown AoE that you weave in between other abilities.

The spec is straightforward compared to most: keep Kill Command on cooldown, spend Focus with Raptor Strike/Swipe, use Wildfire Bomb on CD, and press Boomstick and Aspect of the Eagle for burst windows. Takedown is a proc-based ability that becomes available when targets drop below a threshold.

What makes Survival work is consistency — the rotation is simple enough that execution should be near-perfect. The gap between an average and a great Survival Hunter is almost entirely about reducing dead time and pressing Kill Command on CD without fail.

How It’s Affecting Your Damage

6 early deaths are the #1 DPS problem. Mßaku’s rotation is within 5–10% of the top player on every core ability. But dying in 20% of pulls is devastating — each death means zero damage for the remaining fight duration, which drags the average DPS from what could be 225K+ down to 210K. Staying alive is worth more than any rotation improvement.
Raptor Strike and Raptor Swipe are the only rotation gaps. At 89% and 90% of the #1 respectively, these are the only abilities with meaningful room for improvement. Both are Focus spenders, so the gap likely comes from dead time — moments where Focus is available but no button is being pressed. Tightening these up is a small but real gain.
Kill Command at 96% and Wildfire Bomb at 95% are excellent. These are the two most important buttons in the Survival rotation, and Mßaku is pressing them almost exactly as often as the top player. The rotation fundamentals are solid.

What You Need to Change

1
Stop Dying
6 out of 30 early deaths to 5 different mechanics is the single biggest thing holding you back. The rotation is at 92%+ of the #1 — you don’t need to press buttons better, you need to stay alive longer. Use Aspect of the Turtle and Survival of the Fittest more aggressively during dangerous mechanics. Turtle is at 0.6/pull — it should be closer to 1.0. When in doubt, pop a defensive early rather than waiting until you’re already low.
2
Health Pot Every Pull
13 out of 30 needs to be 30 out of 30. Health pots are on a separate cooldown from your DPS pot — there’s no tradeoff. With 6 deaths, any one of those health pots could have saved a pull. Make it automatic: whenever you drop below 40%, hit the health pot.
3
Keep Doing What You’re Doing Rotationally
Kill Command at 96%, Wildfire Bomb at 95%, Raptor Strike at 89%, Raptor Swipe at 90%. These are strong numbers. Don’t change the rotation — just survive longer and the DPS will follow. The small gap on Raptor Strike/Swipe will close naturally as dead time decreases.
