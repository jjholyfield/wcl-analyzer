Actionable Improvements

Ranked by estimated impact. The DPS gap is primarily execution and filler priority, not talent or gear.

1 Increase Aimed Shot casts — Steady Shot as primary filler
Darthurg cast Aimed Shot 52 times (6.4 CPM). The reference cast it 72 times (9.6 CPM). Aimed Shot is the highest-damage ability in the Marksmanship kit, representing 36.3% of reference damage but only 16.8% of Darthurg's. The root cause is filler choice: Darthurg uses Arcane Shot (60 casts) as the primary filler, which spends Focus. The reference uses Steady Shot (72 casts) as filler, which generates Focus. This Focus starvation cycle means fewer Aimed Shots can be cast.
Fix: Replace Arcane Shot filler usage with Steady Shot. Only use Arcane Shot to spend Precise Shots procs (immediately after Aimed Shot or Rapid Fire). Outside of Precise Shots, the default filler should always be Steady Shot.
Impact: 20 additional Aimed Shots would add approximately 2.7M damage (+6.5% overall DPS).
2 Add Volley to the rotation (talent or usage fix)
Darthurg never cast Volley (0 uses vs reference's 9). Volley provides a Double Tap proc that empowers the next Aimed Shot or Rapid Fire, and the ability itself did 6.6M damage (7.4%) for the reference. If Volley is not in Darthurg's talent build, it should be added. If it is talented but not used, it needs to be integrated into the rotation on cooldown (every 45 seconds or so).
Impact: 9 Volley casts would contribute roughly 3-4M damage plus enhanced Aimed Shots from Double Tap procs.
3 Reduce casting gaps — always have the next button queued
75 gaps >2.5 seconds were detected, totaling 282 seconds of reduced casting. 73.4% of these gaps follow Black Arrow or Arcane Shot — both instant GCD abilities. After pressing an instant ability, the next GCD should already be queued. The pattern suggests either decision-making hesitation (not knowing what to press next) or Focus starvation from too many Arcane Shots.
Fix: Practice the priority list until it becomes automatic: Trueshot > Rapid Fire > Aimed Shot (2 charges) > Arcane/Kill Shot (Precise Shots only) > Steady Shot (default). The "what do I press next?" answer is almost always Steady Shot.
Impact: Halving dead time would add approximately 70 casts, potentially 8-12% DPS increase.
4 Improve opener sequence — 31% CPS gap in first 15 seconds
The opener (0:00-0:15) shows 0.600 CPS vs reference 0.867 CPS. Trueshot was cast at 0:07 (reference: 0:00). With Time Warp on pull, the first 15 seconds are the highest-value DPS window of the entire fight. Every GCD missed here is amplified by lust + Trueshot.
Fix: Pre-cast Aimed Shot at 2s on pull timer. Trueshot at 0:00. Rapid Fire immediately. Burn Aimed Shot charges during Trueshot + lust window. Practice the opener on a target dummy until it is consistent.
Impact: 4 extra casts in the lust window are worth ~2x normal due to haste + Trueshot amplification.
5 Address mid-fight survival pressure (4:00-5:30 window)
Between 4:00 and 5:30, Darthurg used: Survival of the Fittest (4:15), Exhilaration (4:29), Healthstone (4:46), Health Potion (5:05), Aspect of the Cheetah (5:20), Aspect of the Turtle (5:22). This is every defensive cooldown in 90 seconds, indicating severe health pressure. CPS during Vines #5 (5:15) and #6 (6:04) dropped to 0.167 — nearly zero output. The 12 melee hits taken suggest add proximity is the cause.
Fix: Maintain 15+ yard distance from add spawn locations. Use Disengage proactively when adds approach rather than waiting until health is critical. Position further from Bursting Shroom detonation zones. This would eliminate the need for the healing potion, freeing it for a DPS potion instead.
Impact: Recovering uptime in the 4:00-5:30 window and using a DPS pot instead of healing pot could add 3-5% DPS.

Summary

The 55% DPS gap is primarily execution-based, not gear or talent. The 4-ilvl difference (287 vs 291) is marginal. The 17 talent differences may contribute 5-10% but both builds use the same hero talent tree (Dark Ranger). The remaining gap breaks down as:
  • Filler priority (Arcane Shot vs Steady Shot): ~20-25% of the gap. This is the single most impactful fix.
  • Aimed Shot volume (52 vs 72): ~15-20% of the gap. Directly caused by Focus starvation from wrong filler.
  • Missing Volley: ~5-8% of the gap. Either talent or usage gap.
  • Casting gaps / dead time: ~10-15% of the gap. Decision hesitation and Focus-starved waits.
  • Kill time difference: Darthurg's raid killed in 8:09 vs reference's 7:31. Longer fights mean more soft enrage damage and proportionally less burst-phase DPS.
What Darthurg does well:
  • Black Arrow usage is near-reference (7.2 vs 7.5 CPM) — Dark Ranger proc management is solid
  • Wailing Arrow casts match reference exactly (4 each)
  • Survived the kill fight with zero deaths after dying in both wipes — clear learning
  • Vines handling in the first half (Vines #1-4) matches or exceeds reference CPS
  • Damage split (59% boss / 41% adds) matches reference (58% / 42%) — good target prioritization
  • Counter Shot usage (7 interrupts) shows active mechanic participation
  • Defensive cooldown rotation is well-managed (5 SotF uses, 4 Exhilaration)
