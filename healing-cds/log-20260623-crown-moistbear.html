Actionable Improvements

1

Map Survival Instincts to Known Damage Windows

Survival Instincts was used only 6 times across 11 qualifying pulls (0.5 per pull). With 2 charges and a 3-minute CD, Moistbear should use SI 2-3 times per pull. Pre-plan usage:

Charge 1: Void Expulsion at ~0:53 or first heavy damage event in P1.
Charge 2: Intermission Stellar Emission (2:22) — this is killing Moistbear in 15% of pulls.
Charge 1 (back off CD): P2 Void Barrage or Call of the Void at ~3:00+.
Charge 2 (back off CD): P3 entry if the pull gets that far.

Impact: Could prevent 5-8 of the 22 deaths. This is the #1 change.
2

Survive P1 Consistently — Echoing Darkness Awareness

6 deaths in P1 (30% of all deaths). Echoing Darkness from Vorelus killed Moistbear at 0:33 and 0:57. This ability fires every ~3 seconds starting around 0:10 — it is a persistent ground effect or targeted ability. As a melee on Vorelus, Moistbear needs to:

• Use Barkskin proactively when Vorelus casts Echoing Darkness (not reactively after taking 3+ ticks)
• Bear Form shift if HP drops below 50% during P1 — the HP buffer is worth the GCD loss
• Watch for Echoing Darkness pools and move out immediately rather than finishing a cast

Impact: Eliminating P1 deaths adds ~2:14 of DPS time per affected pull, increasing phase reach from 70% to 90%+.
3

Intermission Survival Plan

3 deaths during Intermission (2 to Silverstrike Barrage, 1 to Falling). The intermission is a 39-second survival check with no targets to hit. Moistbear should:

• Pre-shift to Bear Form at 2:10 (before Silversunder Catastrophe at 2:14)
• Barkskin + Frenzied Regeneration during Stellar Emission channel (2:22-2:52)
• Position center of platform to avoid being knocked off (addresses the Falling death)
• Use Survival Instincts if HP drops below 40% during the channel

Impact: 3 fewer deaths. Intermission survival is free DPS time in P2 that Moistbear is currently losing.
4

P2 Cosmic Barrier Awareness

3 deaths to Cosmic Barrier (the Rift Simulacrum shield explosion). This is a learnable mechanic — when the Rift Simulacrum casts Cosmic Barrier, it creates a shield that explodes for massive damage when it expires or is broken.

• Stop DPS on the Simulacrum when Cosmic Barrier is cast — switch to Alleria or adds
• If the shield is about to pop, use Survival Instincts or Bear Form + Barkskin
• This is a coordination mechanic — communicate with raid about when to stop hitting the Simulacrum

Impact: 3 fewer deaths. Cosmic Barrier is the #1 named killer and it is entirely manageable.
5

DPS Optimization is Secondary — But Watch Feral Frenzy Alignment

The damage profile shows Feral Frenzy contribution is 2.1% below reference (9.2% vs 11.3%). Feral Frenzy should always be synced with Tiger's Fury for the damage buff snapshot. During prog, ensure Feral Frenzy is used on cooldown and aligned with Tiger's Fury windows rather than holding it for a "better" moment.

Additionally, Rake contribution is 2.7% below reference — ensure Rake is refreshed within pandemic range (30% remaining, ~4.5s) and ideally during Tiger's Fury for the snapshot. These are minor optimizations compared to the survival issues above.

Impact: ~2-3% DPS improvement. Valuable but secondary to staying alive.

Summary

Moistbear's rotation and talent build are correct — the damage profile matches the top-ranked Feral almost exactly. The entire performance gap is survivability. Dying in 20/20 pulls with an average survival of 3:20 means Moistbear is losing 2-4 minutes of DPS time every pull and never getting meaningful P3 practice.

The fix is straightforward: use Survival Instincts (currently at 15% of expected usage), plan defensive cooldowns for known damage windows (Echoing Darkness in P1, Stellar Emission in Intermission, Cosmic Barrier in P2), and prioritize staying alive over maximizing DPS. A living Feral doing 35K is worth more than a dead Feral who did 40K for the first 3 minutes.
