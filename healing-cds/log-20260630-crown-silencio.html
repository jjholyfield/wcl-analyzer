Ramp Quality (Fight 25)

Resto Druid ramp sequence: spread Rejuvenations (+ Germination) -> Swiftmend -> Wild Growth -> Tranquility/Convoke for burst. Tranquility extends all HoTs via Flourish passive.

P1 Ramp (0:06-0:14)

Silencio opens with Convoke at 0:06, then Tranquility channel at 0:10. This is a very aggressive opener -- deploying both major CDs within the first 14 seconds of the fight. While this ensures the raid is topped for the initial Sentinel adds, it means both CDs are unavailable until ~3:00. On a fight where P1 damage is manageable, holding Tranquility for the Silversunder Catastrophe at ~2:14 or P2 entry would be higher value.

P2 Ramp (3:24-3:41)

Convoke at 3:24 followed by Tranquility at 3:36. This is well-timed for P2 healing demands. P2 starts at ~2:53 with Empowering Darkness and ramps up with Rift Simulacrum/Undying Voidspawn adds. Having both CDs available for the P2 healing check is correct. The 12-second gap between Convoke and Tranquility provides sustained throughput through the peak damage window.

P3 Ramp (5:54-6:45)

Convoke at 5:54 (45 seconds into P3), then another Convoke at 6:58, with Tranquility at 6:40. Good CD density in P3 where everything overlaps. The Tranquility at 6:40 aligns with Dueche's Aura Mastery at 6:56, creating a strong combo where the damage reduction amplifies effective healing. Final Convoke at 6:58 provides burst heading into the final minute of the pull.

Ramp Quality Assessment

The CD pattern shows Silencio has learned the fight's rhythm -- CDs land in P2 and P3 where they matter most. The main improvement is the P1 opener: saving the first Tranquility for a more impactful moment (Intermission or early P2) would reduce P1 overheal and provide more mana for later phases. The Swiftmend -> Wild Growth pairing is under-utilized throughout, which weakens the ramp foundation that Tranquility is meant to extend.

Actionable Items

1. Increase Wild Growth CPM (3.3 -> 5.0+)

Wild Growth is 38% below reference and is the single highest-impact change available. Cast Wild Growth on cooldown during any incoming damage window. Pair it with Swiftmend for the Soul of the Forest 75% buff. Every missed Wild Growth CD cycle is 5+ targets of free HoT healing lost. In the deepest pull, that gap equates to approximately 7-8M effective healing over the full fight.

2. Increase Swiftmend CPM (2.9 -> 4.5+)

Swiftmend at 2.9 CPM vs 4.6 reference. Swiftmend should be treated as rotational -- cast it on cooldown to proc Soul of the Forest, then immediately follow with Wild Growth. The Swiftmend -> Wild Growth combo is the core of Resto Druid's sustained throughput. Missing one means missing both the direct heal AND the amplified Wild Growth. This directly addresses both the HPS gap and the team ranking position.

3. Delay First Tranquility to Intermission or P2

The first Tranquility at 0:10 lands in P1 where 65% of healing is overheal. P1 damage (Sentinels + Void Droplets) is manageable with HoTs alone. Hold Tranquility for the Silversunder Catastrophe at ~2:14 or use it to stabilize at the start of P2 (~2:53). This preserves mana and ensures the Flourish HoT extension applies when HoTs will actually be consumed by incoming damage.

4. Fill GCDs with Wrath in P1

With 65% overheal in P1, many healing GCDs are producing zero effective healing. Replace excess Rejuvenation casts on full-health targets with Wrath damage filler. The reference casts Wrath at 1.1 CPM vs Silencio's 0.3 CPM. Even 4-5 more Wraths per pull adds meaningful boss damage and reduces mana waste from overhealing.

5. Survival is excellent -- maintain the standard

0 actionable deaths across 20 pulls is outstanding. Silencio is never the reason the pull ends. The focus should be entirely on throughput optimization (Wild Growth, Swiftmend CPM) and efficiency (reducing P1 overheal) rather than any survival concerns.
