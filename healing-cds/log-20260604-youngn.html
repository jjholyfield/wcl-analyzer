How Windwalker Monk Works

Windwalker Monk is a melee spec built around a unique Mastery: Combo Strikes mechanic — you deal more damage when you don’t repeat the same ability twice in a row. This means the entire rotation is about weaving different abilities in sequence rather than spamming one button.

The core loop is: Tiger Palm (chi builder) → Rising Sun Kick (priority spender, highest single-target damage) → Blackout Kick (secondary spender) → Fists of Fury (channeled burst, big damage) → weave in Spinning Crane Kick for AoE and Mastery procs. You alternate between builders and spenders while never pressing the same button twice.

Rising Sun Kick is the highest priority — it hits hard and has a short cooldown that should be pressed on CD. Fists of Fury is your channeled burst ability and should also be used on cooldown. Tiger Palm generates Chi and should fill every gap. Blackout Kick is your dump when RSK and FoF are on cooldown.

The key metric for Windwalker is CPM. Because of the Mastery mechanic requiring constant ability weaving, any downtime is magnified. Dead time directly translates to missed damage across every ability. A Windwalker who stops pressing buttons for even a second is losing damage on a spec that demands constant motion.

How It’s Affecting Your Damage

CPM at 82% of #1 is the root cause of everything. 38.9 vs 47.7 — that’s ~9 fewer casts per minute. Over a 5-minute fight, that’s 45 missing casts. Every single core ability is proportionally low because globals are being wasted on dead time, not on wrong abilities.
Dead time at 19.1% — nearly 1 in 5 seconds doing nothing. For a spec that relies on constant ability weaving through Mastery: Combo Strikes, this is devastating. Every idle global is magnified because Windwalker’s Mastery requires you to never repeat — which means you need to always be pressing something different. Stopping breaks the flow.
Blackout Kick at 75% is the biggest individual gap. This is the Chi dump ability — it should be filling every gap where RSK and FoF are on cooldown. At 5.6/m vs 7.5/m, Youngn is leaving Chi unspent or simply not pressing buttons between cooldowns.
4 early deaths with almost no survivability consumable usage. 5/27 Healthstones and 1/27 Health Pots means Youngn is essentially going into every pull with only defensive CDs (Karma + Fort Brew) and nothing else. Three deaths to the same mechanic (Divine Toll) while sitting on unused Healthstones is free survival left on the table.

What You Need to Change

1
Reduce Dead Time — Target Under 12%
Currently at 19.1%. This is the #1 priority and the root cause of every other gap. Every ability is proportionally low because globals are being wasted. For Windwalker, the GCD should never be empty — there is always a Tiger Palm, Blackout Kick, or Spinning Crane Kick available to fill. If nothing else is up, press Tiger Palm.
2
More Tiger Palm — Target 9+ Per Minute
Currently at 7.3/m vs 9.0/m. Tiger Palm is the Chi generator that drives everything. More Tiger Palms = more Chi = more Rising Sun Kicks and Blackout Kicks. This is the button you press when nothing else is available — it should never be delayed.
3
More Rising Sun Kick — Target 8+ Per Minute
Currently at 6.8/m vs 8.7/m. Rising Sun Kick is the highest priority spender — it hits the hardest and has a short cooldown. It should be pressed on cooldown every single time. Missing RSK casts is the highest-impact DPS loss on the spec.
4
Use Healthstones and Health Pots
5/27 HS (19%) and 1/27 HP (4%) is unacceptable. These are free survival tools that cost nothing and don’t interfere with the rotation. With 3 deaths to Divine Toll, popping a Healthstone during that mechanic window could be the difference between dying and surviving the pull. Target 80%+ usage on both.
