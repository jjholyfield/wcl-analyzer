How Survival Hunter Works

Survival Hunter is a melee spec built around three core abilities. Kill Command is your hardest-hitting single ability and generates focus — press it on cooldown. Raptor Strike is your focus spender for single target, and Raptor Swipe is the AoE equivalent when multiple targets are stacked. Wildfire Bomb deals strong AoE damage on a charge system — use charges on cooldown but don’t overcap.

The priority is: Kill Command > Wildfire Bomb (if charges capping) > Raptor Strike/Swipe > Boomstick. Aspect of the Eagle is your major 2min CD that empowers your Kill Commands. Berserk extends your cooldown windows.

Survival Hunter’s strength is that the rotation is straightforward — the spec rewards consistent button pressing and cooldown tracking more than complex decision-making.

How It’s Affecting Your Damage

Rotation is excellent. Critters is within 2–6% of the #1 ranked Survival Hunter on every major ability. Kill Command at 98% means the highest-priority button is being pressed almost perfectly on cooldown. Raptor Strike, Raptor Swipe, and Wildfire Bomb are all 94–96%. This is a well-played rotation.
Dead time at 5.5% is one of the lowest on the team. Critters is pressing buttons consistently and not standing idle. Combined with the strong per-minute casts, this shows good melee uptime on the boss.
Boomstick at 85% is the only rotation gap. At 1.7/m vs 2.0/m, Critters is missing roughly one Boomstick every 3 minutes. It’s not a major DPS loss, but it’s the one ability where there’s room to tighten up.
Healthstone and Health Potion neglect is a liability. Using Healthstone in 2/30 pulls and Health Potion in 3/30 pulls is the single biggest problem in this audit. The rotation is strong, the damage is solid — but ignoring free defensives means that on the pulls where it matters most, Critters is relying entirely on healers to keep him alive. Those two deaths at 5:15 and 4:09 may have been preventable.

What You Need to Change

1
Use Healthstones
2/30 is unacceptable. Healthstone is a free heal on its own cooldown. Press it when you drop below 50% HP. It costs zero focus, zero globals from your rotation. There is no excuse not to use it. Bind it somewhere accessible and build the habit.
2
Use Health Potions
3/30 is equally bad. Health Potion is on a separate cooldown from your pre-pot — using one does not lock out the other. It’s another free heal available every pull. Same rule: below 50% HP, press it. Between Healthstone and Health Potion, you have two emergency heals per pull that you’re currently throwing away.
3
Keep Doing What You’re Doing
The rotation is solid. 94% of #1 DPS, 93% CPM, every ability within 2–6% of the best. The minor Boomstick gap (85%) is worth tightening but it’s not a major issue. The DPS gap will close naturally as fight experience increases. Don’t change the rotation — fix the defensives.
