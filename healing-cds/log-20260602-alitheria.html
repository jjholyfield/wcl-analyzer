How Retribution Paladin Works

Retribution Paladin is a Holy Power builder-spender spec with a very high cast rate. You generate Holy Power through builders (Blade of Justice, Judgment, Crusading Strikes or Hammer abilities depending on build) and spend it on finishers (Divine Storm for AoE, Templar’s Verdict/Final Verdict for single target).

The gameplay is fast-paced — Ret has one of the highest CPM rates in the game because many abilities are instant or proc-based. You’re constantly pressing buttons: builders to generate Holy Power, finishers to spend it, and weaving in CDs like Wake of Ashes, Avenging Wrath, and Execution Sentence.

Avenging Wrath is your major throughput CD — use it on cooldown. Wake of Ashes generates Holy Power and deals damage. Divine Toll and Execution Sentence are additional burst tools.

On a council fight like LBV with 3 bosses, Divine Storm (AoE finisher) takes priority over single-target finishers because it hits all 3 bosses. Top players cast Divine Storm 26+/min — it’s their most-used finisher by a huge margin.

The Templar hero talent (which Alitheria runs) replaces Crusading Strikes with Empyrean Hammer and Divine Hammer, changing the builder rotation. But the finisher priority stays the same — Divine Storm should still dominate on multi-target fights.

How This Is Affecting Your Damage

CPM at 93.3 vs 94.3 — nearly identical to #1. Uptime is NOT the issue. Dead time at 6.5% is excellent. The rotation speed is there — the issue is which abilities are being pressed, not how fast.
Divine Storm at 8.3/min vs top’s 26.5/min (31%). This is the primary DPS gap. On a 3-boss council fight, Divine Storm should be the dominant finisher because it hits all 3 targets. Top players cast it 26.5/min. At 8.3/min, the majority of Holy Power is being spent on single-target finishers instead of the AoE that would hit all 3 bosses simultaneously.
Running Templar hero talent — top rankings happen to be Crusading Strikes builds. Both Templar and Crusading Strikes are viable hero talent choices. The top rankings on this specific fight are dominated by Crusading Strikes, which generates 35-38 auto-attack builders per minute, while Templar uses Empyrean Hammer (13.5/min) and Divine Hammer (11.7/min). Some of the DPS gap is explained by this build difference rather than execution issues. The finisher priority (Divine Storm on multi-target) applies to both builds equally.
23% early death rate with low consumable usage. 6 out of 26 pulls ended in an early death, but healthstone was only used in 50% of pulls and health potion in 38%. More consistent use of survival tools would reduce deaths and increase overall damage output by staying alive longer.

What You Need to Change

1
Use Divine Storm as primary finisher on council fights
8.3/min needs to be 25+. With 3 bosses active, Divine Storm hits all of them and massively outvalues single-target finishers like Final Verdict. Every Holy Power spend on this fight should be Divine Storm unless only one target is alive. This is the single biggest DPS gain available.
2
Use healthstone and health potion consistently
23% death rate with only 50% healthstone usage and 38% health potion usage. Both are available on separate CDs. Using them consistently keeps you alive longer — and every second alive is more damage on the boss.
3
Maximize Divine Storm within current Templar build
Regardless of hero talent choice, Divine Storm should be the dominant finisher on a 3-boss council fight. At 8.3/min vs 26.5/min from top players, there’s significant room to increase Divine Storm usage within the Templar build. Every Holy Power spend should default to Divine Storm when multiple targets are in range.
