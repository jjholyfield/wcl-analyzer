Actionable Improvements

1. Close the Frost Strike Gap (37% Behind Reference)
Impact: ~47 additional Frost Strike casts × avg 22K per hit = ~1.03M+ additional damage (~2.1K DPS)
Frost Strike CPM is 8.6 vs reference's 13.6. This is the largest single-ability gap. Frost Strike dumps Runic Power generated by Obliterate and Empower Rune Weapon. At 70 Frost Strike casts in 8:09, you are leaving substantial Runic Power unspent or never generating it due to GCD gaps. The reference achieves nearly equal Obliterate and Frost Strike casts (117 each) — every Obliterate should be followed by a Frost Strike when Runic Power allows. Focus on never capping Runic Power and always having a Frost Strike queued between Obliterate casts.
2. Use Frostscythe During Add Windows (58% Behind Reference)
Impact: ~36 additional Frostscythe casts × cleave damage = significant add phase DPS gain
Frostscythe CPM is 2.9 vs reference's 7.0. On Mythic Rotmire with 8 add spawns, Frostscythe should replace Obliterate whenever 2+ targets are in melee range. The reference uses Frostscythe 60 times (more than double your 24). During add phases, switch from Obliterate to Frostscythe for Killing Machine procs. This directly addresses the low CPS during Awaken Fungi windows — the reference maintains high CPS during adds because they are pressing Frostscythe on every GCD instead of losing time to target switching or hesitation.
3. Eliminate Dead Time During Add Phases
Impact: 3 gaps of 5-8 seconds = ~19s dead time during add windows alone, ~17 lost casts
The three longest cast gaps (8.1s, 6.1s, 5.1s) all occur during Awaken Fungi windows. On Mythic Rotmire, Cross Fertilization requires careful corpse placement, but positioning adjustments should not cost 5-8 seconds of zero damage. The reference maintains 1.0-1.4 CPS during adds while presumably dealing with the same mechanic. Solutions: (a) Pre-position before adds spawn — the spawn timer is fixed and predictable. (b) Use Death Grip to bring adds to you rather than moving to them. (c) Keep pressing Howling Blast on the move if melee abilities are not available.
4. Address Late-Fight CPS Decline
Impact: Add windows #5-8 average 0.60 CPS vs 0.89 CPS in windows #1-4. ~12 lost casts in late add phases.
CPS during add phases degrades significantly in the second half of the fight. Adds #1-4 average 0.89 CPS, while Adds #5-8 average 0.60 CPS. This could be fatigue, increasing panic from Pustule stacks, or resource management breaking down as the fight extends. The reference player maintains steady CPS throughout. Focus on maintaining the same button-pressing cadence regardless of fight timer — the rotation does not change with soft enrage stacks.
5. Reduce Post-Howling Blast Hesitation
Impact: 9 of 25 gaps follow Howling Blast. Eliminating hesitation could recover ~8-10 casts.
Howling Blast appears as the ability before 9 of the 25 identified cast gaps. This suggests a pattern: after consuming a Rime proc with Howling Blast, there is a pause before the next action. Frost DK rotation priority after Howling Blast should be immediate — check for Killing Machine (Obliterate), check for Runic Power cap (Frost Strike), press Obliterate if runes are available. Queue the next ability during the Howling Blast GCD so there is zero delay.

Performance Context

The 42% DPS gap to the top-ranked reference is driven by three factors:

  • Execution (primary): 10 CPM deficit (54.0 vs 64.0), 95.7 seconds of dead time, and 86 estimated lost casts. This is the main driver.
  • Cleave usage: Frostscythe and Frost Strike underuse account for ~83 missing casts combined. These are the two specific abilities to focus on.
  • Gear: 4 ilvl gap (287 vs 291) contributes roughly 3-5% of the DPS difference. Minor factor.
  • Talents: 8 talent differences. Some of these may affect rotational flow, but the CPM gap is too large to be talent-driven alone.

What Bebeshakur Does Well

  • Zero deaths on the kill fight. Clean mechanical execution on a boss that punishes corpse placement errors.
  • Pillar of Frost, Breath of Sindragosa, and Frostwyrm's Fury are all used on cooldown with proper alignment. Cooldown discipline is strong.
  • Defensive usage is excellent — 6 well-timed AMS, AMZ for Bloom #3, 6 interrupts, 10 Death Grips for add positioning.
  • Reaper's Mark and Exterminate proc management matches the reference almost exactly (7.4 vs 7.5 CPM).
  • Opener was strong with proper Lust + PoF + BoS + Blood Fury alignment.
  • Bloom #2 handling was better than the reference (1.27 vs 1.20 CPS). The capability exists.

Summary

Bebeshakur's cooldown usage and defensive play are already at a high level. The improvement path is entirely about filling GCDs between cooldown windows — particularly Frost Strike (primary RP spender) and Frostscythe (cleave during adds). Eliminating the 95.7 seconds of dead time and matching the reference's ability cadence would close the majority of the DPS gap without any gear or talent changes.
