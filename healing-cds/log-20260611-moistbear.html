Critical: Shred Replaced by Swipe

Shred is the primary combo point generator for Feral in single-target — it generates combo points, procs Clearcasting, and deals strong direct damage. Moistbear cast Shred at 8.2 CPM (33 casts) vs the reference's 14.6 CPM (115 casts) — a 44% deficit. Meanwhile, Swipe was cast 18 times (4.4 CPM) vs just 3 times (0.4 CPM) by the reference. Swipe is an AoE ability that generates combo points less efficiently in single-target and deals significantly less damage per GCD than Shred. Those 18 Swipes should be Shreds — this alone accounts for roughly 15 lost Shred GCDs and cascading combo point / Ferocious Bite losses.

Critical: Zero Regrowth Casts (Bloodtalons)

The reference cast Regrowth 28 times (3.6 CPM). Moistbear cast it zero times. In the current Feral rotation, Regrowth is consumed via Predatory Swiftness procs (which Moistbear earned 9 of) to activate Bloodtalons — a talent that increases the damage of the next 3 Combo Point-generating abilities by 30%. By never casting Regrowth, Moistbear is leaving every single Bloodtalons window on the table. The reference gets Bloodtalons-buffed Shreds and Rakes throughout the fight. This is likely the single largest DPS loss per GCD.

Moonfire Uptime Severely Low

With the Lunar Inspiration talent (which both Moistbear and the reference use), Moonfire becomes a rotational DoT that should be maintained at near-100% uptime. Moistbear cast Moonfire 8 times (2.0 CPM) vs the reference's 44 times (5.6 CPM) — a 65% deficit. The reference refreshes Moonfire aggressively, cycling it across targets during add phases and maintaining it as a DoT on the boss. Moonfire accounted for 6.7% of reference damage and is essentially free DPS when maintained. Missing Moonfire refreshes directly loses 2-3% of total damage output.

Actionable Items

1. Use Regrowth to proc Bloodtalons. This is the single highest-impact change available. Moistbear cast Regrowth zero times while the reference cast it 28 times (3.6 CPM). Every time Predatory Swiftness procs (after a 5-CP finisher), cast an instant Regrowth. This activates Bloodtalons, which increases the damage of the next 3 combo-point-generating abilities (Shred, Rake, Moonfire) by 30%. Moistbear earned 9 Predatory Swiftness procs and consumed none of them. At the reference's Bloodtalons uptime, this represents roughly 8-12% of total damage being left on the table. The Regrowth is instant-cast and off the GCD when Predatory Swiftness is active — it costs no DPS time to press.
2. Replace Swipe with Shred in single-target. Moistbear cast Swipe 18 times (4.4 CPM) vs the reference's 3 times (0.4 CPM). Swipe is an AoE ability that does significantly less single-target damage than Shred and generates combo points less efficiently. Shred is at 8.2 CPM vs the reference's 14.6 CPM — a 44% deficit. Swapping 15+ Swipes to Shreds would directly increase both direct damage and Unseen Slash proc rate (which scales from Shred hits). Estimated gain: 5-7% total DPS. Only use Swipe when 3+ targets are stacked.
3. Cut phase transition downtime from 51s to <30s. From 2:09 to 3:00, Moistbear had zero offensive casts and 6 repeated Cat Form casts suggesting movement/repositioning difficulty. The reference handles the same transition in ~29 seconds by: (a) refreshing all DoTs (Rake, Rip, Moonfire) before leaving melee range, (b) using instant Regrowth during movement, (c) pre-positioning with Travel Form + Wild Charge to return to the boss faster. Saving 22 seconds of downtime at 147K DPS = ~3.2M recovered damage on this pull alone.
4. Maintain Moonfire uptime (Lunar Inspiration). Moonfire at 2.0 CPM vs 5.6 CPM is a 65% deficit. With Lunar Inspiration talented, Moonfire is a ranged instant-cast DoT that should be maintained on the primary target at all times and cycled across Sentinels in Phase 1. It is also castable during movement — the reference uses Moonfire extensively during the phase transition to maintain damage output while repositioning. Refreshing Moonfire within the pandemic window (last 30% of duration) avoids wasting ticks.
5. Increase Ferocious Bite frequency through faster combo point generation. Ferocious Bite at 7.7 CPM vs 10.8 CPM is a 29% deficit. This is downstream from the Shred deficit — fewer Shreds = slower combo point generation = fewer Ferocious Bites. Fixing Shred CPM (item #2) and Bloodtalons (item #1) will naturally increase Ferocious Bite frequency. Additionally, ensure Ferocious Bite is always cast at 50+ Energy for the damage bonus — the Feral rotation rewards pooling Energy briefly before spending finishers.
