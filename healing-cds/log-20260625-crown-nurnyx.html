Actionable Improvements
Priority 1 — Survival
P2: Dodge Void Remnants ground effects
The single actionable death this night (Fight 6: first to die at 4:33 to Void Remnants in P2) cost an estimated 16K+ DPS on that pull (29.3K vs the 45K+ Nurnyx produces when surviving past 5:30). Void Remnants are avoidable ground effects from Rift Simulacrum. Pre-position away from Rift spawn points and use instant casts during repositioning.

Tools available: Fade (threat drop + potential DR with talents, 10s CD), Desperate Prayer (instant self-heal), Dispersion (90% DR for 6s), Vampiric Embrace self-healing. With vers at only 27, personal defensive usage is especially important — there is almost no passive damage reduction.
Priority 2 — DPS Uptime
P2 DPS: Maintain DoTs and Insanity flow during Rift mechanics
P2 average DPS (106.5K) drops significantly from P1 burst (315K). While P1 has multi-target cleave padding, improving P2 sustained damage is the highest-impact DPS gain because the raid spends the most progression time in this phase. Shadow Priest has strong tools for maintaining DPS during movement: Vampiric Touch and Shadow Word: Pain via Misery, Shadow Word: Death (instant, execute range), and proc-based Mind Spike: Insanity.

Key habits: Refresh DoTs before forced movement. Use Void Torrent during safe windows (channels but deals strong damage). Clip Mind Flay early for movement rather than finishing channels. Maintain Insanity spending (Devouring Plague / Shadow Word: Madness) to avoid capping.
Priority 3 — Consistency
Reduce pull-to-pull variance (29K-55K range)
The 26K DPS spread between best and worst pulls is high, but it maps almost entirely to survival time. When Nurnyx dies before 5:00 the average is 33.5K. When surviving past 5:30 the average is 50.6K. Fixing Priority 1 (avoiding P2 deaths) will naturally reduce this variance. The best pulls (F2: 55.4K rank 3rd, F27: 53.9K rank 4th) prove the rotation is competitive when Nurnyx stays alive.
Priority 4 — Positive Trend
Maintain the late-session improvement curve
DPS improved +8.5% from the first half to the second half of the session. The final three pulls of the night (F26: 33.5K, F27: 53.9K, F28: 47.2K) included two of the three best DPS pulls overall. This demonstrates active fight learning. Continue building P2/P3 familiarity — the mechanical comfort needed for survival and sustained DPS comes with more reps.
Priority 5 — P3 Preparation
Build P3 consistency as the raid pushes deeper
P3 DPS has enormous variance (29K - 114K) across only 6 exposures. As the raid begins consistently reaching P3, Nurnyx needs to stabilize damage output during the chaotic overlap phase. Pre-plan cooldown usage: if Voidform and Power Infusion are available entering P3, use them for a strong damage window. Ensure all active targets are dotted immediately when P3 begins — Shadow Priest multi-dot pressure is strongest in phases with multiple targets active simultaneously.
Summary Verdict

What is working: Nurnyx's survivability is genuinely strong under the 2-death lens — only 1 individual failure in 19 pulls. Consumables, enchants, and Vantus Rune are all correct. Talent build matches the top reference within 2 talent points. Best-pull DPS (55.4K on F2, 53.9K on F27) is competitive with or above the raid median when survival allows it. The improvement trend through the session is positive (+8.5% first half to second half).

Primary gap: Overall DPS is dragged down by early deaths and P2 uptime. On pulls where Nurnyx dies before 5:00, DPS averages 33.5K (bottom 3 in raid). On pulls with late survival, DPS averages 50.6K (competitive). The P2 damage drop is steeper than expected even accounting for the phase transition — improving DoT uptime and instant-cast usage during Rift mechanics is the highest-leverage DPS improvement.

Bottom line: This is a survival-gated DPS profile, not a rotation problem. The talent build is correct, the consumables are correct, and the best-pull numbers prove the rotation is competitive. Fix the one actionable death pattern (P2 Void Remnants positioning), maintain the late-session improvement trend, and overall DPS will naturally climb as the raid pushes deeper into P3 with cleaner pulls.
