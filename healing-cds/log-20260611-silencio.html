Actionable Items

1. Swiftmend on cooldown — the #1 throughput lever. At 2.3 CPM vs 4.7 (reference), Silencio is using Swiftmend roughly half as often as they should. Every Swiftmend triggers Everbloom (cleave heal to 6 allies), extends HoTs via Verdant Infusion, and applies 3 Rejuvenations via Power of the Archdruid. This single ability generates more healing per GCD than any other button in the Resto Druid kit. In a 4:24 window, 10 Swiftmends vs the expected ~20+ means roughly 10 missed Everbloom procs and 30 missed Rejuvenation applications. Estimated throughput gain: +15–25%.
2. Time Tranquility for damage events, not low-damage windows. Tranquility at 80% overheal means the channel went almost entirely to waste. Tranquility extends all active HoTs by 10 seconds — this extension is the real value, not just the channel healing. Pre-apply Rejuvenations to 10–12 targets, then channel Tranquility DURING raid-wide damage. The HoT extension amplifies all existing HoTs through the damage window. On Crown of the Cosmos, the Phase 1 Sentinel abilities and Phase 2 Rift Simulacrum have clear raid-wide damage windows to target.
3. Increase Wild Growth usage to match cooldown (−20% vs reference). Wild Growth at 4.3 CPM vs 5.4 means the ability is off cooldown and not being pressed for ~4 seconds every cycle. With the tier set bonus giving +25% healing and −2s cooldown, Wild Growth should be pressed nearly on cooldown during any active damage. Each Wild Growth also adds another HoT to Mastery (Harmony) stacking. 19 casts in 4:24 vs 42 in 7:50 — the reference maintains a higher rate even across a much longer fight.
4. Regrowth CPM gap: leverage Abundance stacks. At 9.8 CPM vs 14.7 (−33%), Regrowth is significantly under-cast. With sufficient Rejuvenation/Germination HoTs out, Abundance stacks reduce Regrowth mana cost and increase its crit chance. At high stacks, Regrowth becomes free and guaranteed to crit. The fix is upstream: more Swiftmend (which applies Rejuvenations) builds more Abundance stacks, which makes Regrowth free, which enables more Regrowth casts. This is why Swiftmend is the keystone.
5. Gear toward Haste and Mastery over Crit. Silencio has 279 Crit vs 138 for the reference, but Crit is the lowest-priority stat for Resto Druid. Meanwhile Haste (1,106 vs 1,365) and Mastery (892 vs 1,165) are both significantly lower. Haste directly increases the number of GCDs available (partially explaining the CPM gaps), and Mastery increases the value of each HoT on a target. Swapping Crit-heavy pieces for Haste/Mastery alternatives would passively close some of the throughput gap.
