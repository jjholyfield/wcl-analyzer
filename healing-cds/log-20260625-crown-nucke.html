Nucke's survivability on Crown is excellent — across all 19 pulls, he was never among the first 2 deaths in any pull. Every death was a cascade wipe death (death order #7 or later). This means zero actionable tank deaths on the night. Defensive CD discipline is solid: Demon Spikes usage averages 32.8 casts per deep pull (4.7 CPM), Fiery Brand at 7.8 casts per deep pull with good phase coverage, and Metamorphosis used aggressively at 7.5 casts per deep pull. The key growth area is overall CPM — at 103 CPM (fight 14) vs the reference Melvabeam's 130 CPM, Nucke is pressing 21% fewer buttons per minute. Soul Cleave is the biggest gap at 9.0 vs 13.5 CPM (33% under), which directly impacts self-healing throughput. Fel Devastation usage is nearly absent (1 cast in the best pull vs 10 for the reference), representing a significant missed defensive and damage tool. DTPS rises from 59.6K in P1 to 63.7K in P3, a manageable 7% increase that suggests mitigation timing is translating well across phases.

Actionable Items

Survivability is not a concern

Zero actionable deaths across 19 pulls. Nucke was never among the first 2 deaths in any pull (lowest death order: #7). Defensive CD timing is solid, with Demon Spikes maintaining high uptime and Fiery Brand/Metamorphosis well-distributed across phases. This is exactly what you want from a tank on prog night 4.

Soul Cleave usage is 33% below reference (9.0 vs 13.5 CPM)

Soul Cleave is the primary spender and the main source of on-demand self-healing. At 9.0 CPM vs the reference's 13.5 CPM, Nucke is missing roughly 4.5 Soul Cleaves per minute. Over a 7-minute pull, that is approximately 31 missed Soul Cleaves. More Soul Cleave casts mean more self-healing, more Soul Fragment consumption, and more consistent damage output. This is the single biggest rotational improvement available.

Fel Devastation is nearly absent (1 cast in 7:12 vs 10 in 8:26)

Fel Devastation is a 60-second cooldown that deals frontal AoE damage, heals the caster, and provides a short defensive window. The reference used it 10 times in their kill. Nucke used it once in the best pull. This is a free defensive + damage + healing button that should be pressed on cooldown. Adding 9 more Fel Devastation casts per pull would directly improve both DPS and survivability.

Overall CPM gap: 103 vs 130 (21% fewer button presses)

Across all abilities, Nucke presses 21% fewer buttons per minute than the reference. The biggest contributors are Soul Cleave (-33%), Immolation Aura (-33%), Fracture (-12%), and Metamorphosis (-58%). Closing the Fracture and Immolation Aura gaps will generate more Soul Fragments, which enables more Soul Cleave/Spirit Bomb usage, creating a positive feedback loop. Some of the Metamorphosis gap may be talent-driven (5 talent diffs with the reference), but the overall pattern suggests room to tighten the rotation.

Immolation Aura is 33% under reference (2.8 vs 4.2 CPM)

Immolation Aura should be pressed on cooldown for AoE damage and Soul Fragment generation (via the Fallout talent). At 2.8 CPM, Nucke is likely missing some casts during transitions or when focused on positioning. This is a simple press-on-cooldown ability that should be near the reference CPM.

Vengeful Retreat not used (0 vs 0.8 CPM)

The reference uses Vengeful Retreat 7 times in their kill (0.8 CPM). This could be a talent difference (Felblade vs Momentum/Initiative), but if Nucke has Initiative talented, Vengeful Retreat should be used on cooldown for the damage buff it provides. This is lower priority than the Soul Cleave and Fel Devastation gaps.
