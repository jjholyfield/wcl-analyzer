How Restoration Druid Works

Restoration Druid healing is all about HoTs — Heal over Time effects. Rejuvenation blankets the raid, and you want it rolling on as many targets as possible before damage hits. Regrowth is your direct heal + HoT combo — it heals immediately AND leaves a HoT ticking. Swiftmend consumes a HoT for an instant burst heal and generates Mastery stacks through Harmony.

The core loop: Rejuvenation on as many targets as possible → Regrowth for direct healing → Wild Growth on AoE damage → Swiftmend for emergency heals. The more HoTs you have rolling, the more your Mastery (Harmony) amplifies all your healing. Every active HoT is a multiplier on everything else you do.

Swiftmend at 3.1/m vs the #1’s 4.6/m is a significant gap. Swiftmend is on a short cooldown, and each use is an instant burst heal plus it fuels your Mastery. Missing 1.5 casts per minute means less Mastery uptime and fewer emergency saves when they matter most.

The main issue isn’t spell selection — it’s casting speed. CPM at 54.4 vs 60.7 means roughly 6 fewer casts per minute. At 17.2% dead time, there are long windows where no HoTs are going out, which means HoTs fall off, raid coverage drops, and Mastery uptime tanks. For a HoT healer, standing still is the worst thing you can do.

How It’s Affecting Your Healing

Dead time at 17.2% is the biggest single factor in the HPS gap. For a HoT healer, dead time is deadly. Every second without casting is a Rejuvenation that falls off, a Wild Growth that didn’t go out, and Mastery uptime lost. The top RDruids sit around 10% dead time. Closing that gap from 17.2% to 12% would add roughly 5 more casts per minute, which translates directly into more HoTs rolling and more Mastery value on every heal.
Rejuvenation at 14.1/m vs 17.4/m (81%). That’s 3.3 fewer Rejuvenations per minute, which means fewer raid members covered by HoTs at any given moment. Less Rejuvenation coverage means lower Mastery value on every other heal, because Mastery (Harmony) scales with how many HoTs are active on the target. It’s a compounding loss.
Regrowth at 14.7/m vs 17.7/m (83%). Each Regrowth is both a direct heal AND a HoT. 3 fewer per minute means missing both the upfront healing and the ongoing ticking. Over a 6-minute pull, that’s 18 Regrowths that didn’t happen — 18 direct heals and 18 HoTs that would have been feeding Mastery.
Swiftmend at 3.1/m vs 4.6/m (67%) — the biggest single spell gap. Swiftmend is burst healing plus Mastery fuel. All three of the top RDruids on this fight cast it at 4.6-4.7/m. Using it 33% less means missing emergency saves when the raid is taking heavy damage and lower Mastery uptime across the board.

What You Need to Change

1
Fill every GCD — target under 12% dead time
As a HoT healer, you should always be casting. If nobody needs direct healing, blanket Rejuvenation. There is never a moment where you can’t put a HoT on someone. The difference between 17.2% and 10% dead time is roughly 6 extra casts per minute — that’s 6 more HoTs rolling, 6 more chances for Mastery to multiply your healing.
2
Cast Swiftmend on cooldown — target 4+/min
Swiftmend is instant, it heals big, and it fuels your Mastery. Don’t hold it for emergencies — use it proactively. The top 3 RDruids all cast it at 4.6-4.7/m. You’re at 3.1. That means you’re sitting on this button for extended periods when it should be pressed the moment it comes off cooldown.
3
Use consumables every pull
0 pre-pots and 0 health pots across 30 pulls is leaving free resources on the table. Pre-pot every pull for early throughput. Health potion is on a separate cooldown from mana potion — you can use both in the same pull. 8/30 healthstone is also low. These are survival tools that cost nothing to press.
